# Executive Summary

Q1 2026 opened under conditions of **controlled instability**. It closed under conditions of **structural inflection with system-wide implications**.

Through January–March, markets were already adjusting to:
- persistent core inflation
- constrained monetary flexibility
- increasing geopolitical selectivity in capital allocation

The events of early April did not introduce volatility. They **formalised it into policy**.

Following the April tariff escalation:
- the S&P 500 corrected approximately **10–12% from recent highs** *(market-implied drawdown, early April repricing window)*
- US 10-year yields approached **~4.5% intraday levels** *(Treasury volatility spike, April 2026)*
- the US dollar weakened despite risk-off conditions *(non-standard behaviour requiring monitoring)*
- oil repriced downward on demand destruction expectations

A temporary 90-day pause reduced immediate volatility but **did not alter the structural trajectory**.

> **Bottom Line (v2.0.0):**
Markets are no longer pricing growth within globalisation.
They are pricing **survivability within fragmentation**.

The Federal Reserve retains policy tools, but each pathway carries **asymmetric cost**:

- Tightening risks accelerating economic contraction

**Expected posture:**
Extended hold, with conditional easing pushed toward **H2 2026 at earliest**, dependent on growth deterioration outweighing inflation persistence.

# Major Economies — Strategic Readings

## United States

### Observations

- Consumption remains resilient but vulnerable to tariff-driven pricing pressure
- Core inflation remains sticky; tariffs likely to add **~1–2pp inflation pressure (2–4 quarters)**
- Market leadership narrowing pre-April; correction now broadening
- Dollar weakness in risk-off conditions signals **policy credibility stress signal**

### Strategic Reading

The US remains structurally dominant—but the **predictability premium is under pressure**.

**Board Instruction:**
- Model 12–18 months of elevated input costs
- Assume no near-term monetary relief
- Revalidate all import-dependent revenue models

---

## Operator Translation

- Expect margin pressure before demand collapse
- Pricing power becomes defensive, not opportunistic
- Dollar behaviour is now a monitoring variable, not an assumption

---

### Case Evidence — US Exposure

A UK importer with 60% China-linked input exposure saw projected margins fall below 8% under tariff-adjusted modelling.

After restructuring supplier nodes and cutting low-margin SKUs, projected margin stabilised at 14%.

The difference was not execution. It was recognising the condition early.

---

## China

### Observations

- Tariffs at **125%** effectively disrupt normal trade economics
- Capital is actively re-routing supply chains
- Export redirection toward ASEAN, Middle East, Africa accelerating
- Currency management remains controlled but sensitive

### Strategic Reading

China is executing a **controlled decoupling strategy under constraint**.

**Board Instruction:**
Any China-to-US supply chain must be treated as **structurally impaired**, not temporarily disrupted.

---

### Case Evidence — China Dependency

A mid-market manufacturer treated China exposure as a procurement issue until tariff-adjusted landed cost moved two product lines below contribution threshold.

Supplier diversification reduced near-term efficiency but restored optionality across ASEAN and domestic alternatives.

The decisive move was accepting impairment before the model forced it.

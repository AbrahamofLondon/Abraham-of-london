## 2. Board Summary — Q2 2026 Draft

### Core Thesis

Fragmentation has moved from market repricing into structural enforcement. Q2 is the absorption phase: organisations are no longer adjusting to signals of fragmentation; they are beginning to operate within it.

### Current Decision Window

Q3 2026.

### Five Non-Optional Operating Decisions

1. Convert scenario planning into structural planning.
2. Build dual or multi-node supply chains regardless of near-term efficiency cost.
3. Treat geopolitical risk as a financial reporting input.
4. Maintain defensive liquidity buffers as core strategy.
5. Identify and exit structurally impaired exposures before the market forces repricing.

### Three Watch Signals

1. **USD behaviour under stress conditions** — continued dollar instability during risk-off events signals reserve currency stress beyond normal volatility.
2. **Credit tightening outside headline policy rates** — covenant tightening and spread widening beyond policy-rate signals indicate financial-condition stress compounding the trade shock.
3. **Tariff rollback or enforcement tightening below structural thresholds** — any bilateral effective rate reduction below ~50% would require reassessment of the fragmentation-persistence base case.

### Base Case

Managed Fragmentation — system stabilisation under elevated friction. Operative probability: 45–50%.

### Downside Case

Confidence Shock — non-linear repricing across rates, FX, credit, and equities. Tail probability: 10–15%.

### What Would Change This View

- Tariff rollback below structural thresholds on a sustained bilateral basis.
- Sustained USD strength during stress (rather than instability).
- Coordinated central bank easing that materially changes financial conditions.
- Re-synchronisation of global trade flows beyond current rerouting patterns.

### Board Instruction

Stop waiting for clarity. Govern under constraint.

### Confidence Posture Summary

— HIGH: Fragmentation persistence; tariff regime durability; capital selectivity by jurisdiction.
— MEDIUM: Inflation pass-through persistence; growth constrained-band; India and Southeast Asia absorption capacity.
— MONITORING: Dollar instability under stress; confidence shock probability; policy reset pathway.

## 8. Major Economy Readings

### United States

**Reality shift:** Fiscal strength remains, but policy unpredictability is now priced. Corporate margins entering compression cycle. Dollar behaviour remains structurally unstable under stress.

**Strategic position:** The US remains the system anchor — but now as a high-volatility anchor.

**Board instruction:** Assume prolonged elevated cost structures. Stress-test USD volatility in both directions. Treat policy risk as a pricing variable, not an externality.

### China

**Reality shift:** Trade decoupling now operational, not theoretical. Domestic stabilisation measures increasing. External demand re-routing accelerating.

**Strategic position:** China is transitioning into a parallel economic sphere under pressure.

**Board instruction:** Classify China exposure into domestic, regional, and US-linked. Treat US-linked flows as structurally impaired assets.

### Europe

**Reality shift:** Industrial base under sustained external pressure. Policy coordination improving but slow. Energy and defence spending rising.

**Strategic position:** Europe is becoming a defensive industrial bloc with institutional depth.

**Board instruction:** Model regulatory timelines as operational constraints. Position for policy-led sector support in defence, energy, and infrastructure.

### United Kingdom

**Reality shift:** Strength consolidating in services, legal systems, and capital routing. Relative insulation from direct tariff shocks.

**Strategic position:** The UK is emerging as a coordination hub within fragmentation — not a production centre, but a system connector.

**Board instruction:** Leverage UK positioning for structuring, financing, and dispute frameworks. Expand role in cross-border capital orchestration.

### India

**Reality shift:** Capital inflows accelerating beyond infrastructure absorption speed. Manufacturing expansion uneven but scaling.

**Strategic position:** India is now a frontline beneficiary of supply chain realignment. Primary constraint: execution capacity versus inflow velocity.

### Southeast Asia

**Reality shift:** Rapid industrial inflows. Increasing scrutiny on origin compliance.

**Strategic position:** A high-opportunity, high-verification region. Only real capacity — not routing arbitrage — will survive enforcement tightening.

### Middle East

**Reality shift:** Oil volatility impacting fiscal flows. Sovereign capital becoming more strategically deployed.

**Strategic position:** A capital allocator and logistics intermediary bloc.

### Africa

**Reality shift:** Strategic importance rising via minerals, infrastructure, and positioning. External capital becoming more selective.

**Strategic position:** Africa is transitioning into a strategic leverage layer within global fragmentation. *(CALL-008 review window: Q3 2026.)*

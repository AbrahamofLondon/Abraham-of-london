# Christianity Is Not Extremism: A Study in Systems

<Rule />

<Verse cite="John 14:6">I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.</Verse>

## Dear JPost: Stop lumping Christianity in with Islam under the banner of “extremism”

*By Abraham of London, 17 August 2025*

Preaching isn’t extremism. Conflating Christianity with Islam because both have zealots is lazy thinking. The public square’s attempt to lump all “zeal” into one bucket is a dangerous category error. Words matter. So do **origins**, **means**, and **fruits**.

This is not an attack on neighbours of any creed. Christians are commanded to love every neighbour and honour all people. This is a confession: **Christianity is not extremism**. It is the good news that **Jesus Christ is Lord**, and that in Him there is forgiveness, life, and a new way to be human.

<Rule />

## 1) The Source Code: Two Roots, Two Trajectories

Christianity and Islam are not two species of the same genus called “aggressive faith.” They proceed from fundamentally different operating systems.

**The Christian Root:** The methodology was the **Cross**. Violence is a mutation. The Founder *is* the message: Jesus did not conquer a single square inch of territory by the sword. He commanded His followers to love enemies, turn the other cheek, and make disciples by *baptising and teaching*—not beheading and taxing. When Christians take up the sword to spread the faith, they are not being faithful to the Gospel. They are betraying it.

**The Islamic Root:** The methodology was the **Conquest**. Expansion is a fulfilment. Muhammad was a military and political leader. The early caliphates institutionalised *jihad* as a means of territorial expansion. The distinction between *dar al-Islam* (house of submission) and *dar al-harb* (house of war) is not a fringe doctrine; it is classical jurisprudence. When modern jihadists take up the sword, they are not betraying Islam. They are fulfilling a core historical current within it.

<Caption>To say this is not bigotry. It is history. And honesty is the first requirement of strategy.</Caption>

<Rule />

## 2) The Category Error: Coercion vs. Confession

If extremism is defined as **coercion**—the use of force, law, or violence to compel religious adherence—then Christians being faithful to the Gospel are not extremists. They are the opposite.

- **Coercion says:** “Submit or die.”
- **Confession says:** “Repent and live. I beg you as an ambassador.”

The early church “turned the world upside down” (*Acts 17:6*) not with legions, but with martyrs. Tertullian famously wrote, “The blood of the martyrs is the seed of the church.” Our revolution is **cross-shaped**: the power to lay down our lives for our enemies, not take theirs.

To call Christian witness “extremism” is to abandon language entirely. A street preacher in London holding a sign that says “Jesus is Lord” is not the moral equivalent of a man with an AK-47 enforcing sharia law. One is abrasive. The other is violent. Liberal societies used to understand this distinction.

<Rule />

## 3) What “Radical” Really Means

If “radical” means **rooted** (from *radix*, meaning root), then yes—Christianity is radical. The question is: *radical what?*

- **Radical love:** Love your enemy. Pray for your persecutor.
- **Radical truth:** There is one God, one mediator, one name under heaven by which we must be saved.
- **Radical service:** Wash feet. Care for the poor. Die to self.

That is not extremism. That is fidelity.

Where the Gospel takes root, hospitals, universities, literacy, care for the poor, and the *imago Dei* basis for human dignity flourish. Christians fail; **repentance** is our corrective. The standard is not us—but **Him**.

<Rule />

## 4) The Distinction Every Honest Analyst Must Make

Let us be precise:

| Domain | Christianity (Orthodox) | Islam (Orthodox) |
|--------|------------------------|------------------|
| **Founder’s method** | Crucifixion (non-violent submission) | Conquest (political-military expansion) |
| **Primary scripture** | Gospels (enemy love, turn cheek) | Qur’an & Hadith (sword verse, warfare doctrine) |
| **Means of spread** | Preaching, persuasion, martyrdom | Dhimmi contract, taxation, military campaign |
| **View of other faiths** | Witness and invitation | Subordination (jizya, or conversion) |
| **Failure mode** | Crusades (a betrayal of the Cross) | Jihad (a fulfilment of conquest) |

<Caption>To collapse these columns is not tolerance. It is intellectual dishonesty.</Caption>

We contend with **ideas**, not with **people**. Our posture is invitation: *Come to Christ and live.* Love your neighbour. Tell the truth. Keep your heart clean.

<Rule />

## 5) For Fathers and Households: Stand Firm

I’m raising my household to carry light without apology. If culture calls obedience “extreme,” so be it. Tepid compromise is worse.

**House rule (weekly):**

- Read the **Sermon on the Mount** aloud as a family (Matt 5–7).
- One act of mercy together (visit, call, give, serve).
- One hospitality moment (invite in a neighbour who believes differently).
- Review speech: **No contempt**; truth + tenderness only.

<Rule />

## 6) The Call: Exclusive Truth, Inclusive Grace

The Gospel is exclusive because truth is exclusive (*Acts 4:12*)—yet gloriously inclusive:

<Verse cite="Romans 10:13">
  Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved.
</Verse>

**Repent and live.** Christ is the Light of the world. As for our house, **we will serve the Lord**.

Christianity is not extremism. It is the cross-shaped way of truth and tenderness that built hospitals, schools, and hope. Walk in the light.

<Rule />

## Practice: A Father’s 7-Day Witness

- **Day 1 - Word:** Read John 1. Note one line to live.
- **Day 2 - Prayer:** 10 minutes for your child’s courage and compassion.
- **Day 3 - Craft:** Make something excellent “as unto the Lord.”
- **Day 4 - Mercy:** Give privately; don’t post it.
- **Day 5 - Hospitality:** Share a table with someone who disagrees with you. Listen well.
- **Day 6 - Courage:** Speak one gracious truth you’ve avoided.
- **Day 7 - Rest:** Worship. Put your phone away for three hours. Delight in God.

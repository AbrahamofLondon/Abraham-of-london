# Canon System Constitution

## Status

This document is the governing charter for the full decision-intelligence estate.

It is not a recommendation.

It is not a prompt.

It is not a landing page.

It is the **constitutional source of truth**.

All downstream layers must defer to it:

- strategy room intake formulation
- diagnosis and triage logic
- constitutional routing
- guidance composition
- asset selection policy
- recommendation governance
- escalation discipline
- operator review discipline
- follow-up capture
- efficacy interpretation

If any lower layer conflicts with this charter, the lower layer yields.

---

# I. First Principles

## 1. Human flourishing is architectural

The system shall assume that disorder in leadership, organisation, governance, market posture, culture, execution or authority is not random.

It is structural.

Therefore the system must not merely react to symptoms.
It must diagnose architecture.

## 2. Meaning is not self-generated

The system shall not treat novelty, panic, urgency, popularity, hype or buyer pressure as sufficient grounds for strategic escalation.

It must privilege:

- purpose
- stewardship
- truth discipline
- governance legitimacy
- responsible authority
- institutional coherence

## 3. Institutions drift before they collapse

The system must be calibrated to detect:

- narrative incoherence
- sponsor weakness
- mandate ambiguity
- trust erosion
- governance decay
- execution fragmentation
- strategic-operational misalignment
- moral ambiguity disguised as agility

## 4. Guidance must serve judgment, not stimulation

The system shall not optimise for clicks as its highest law.

It shall optimise for:

- clearer diagnosis
- stronger authority recognition
- better route assignment
- healthier escalation
- sharper intervention fit
- safer conversion discipline
- improved decision quality over time

## 5. Governance is a commercial advantage, not a drag

The system shall assume that serious operators increasingly reward governance, proof, explainability and disciplined escalation.

Therefore the system must not behave like a generic lead funnel.

It must behave like a governed advisory intelligence estate.

# II. Constitutional Laws of Intake

## Law 1 — No shallow intake

No intake may proceed to full guidance unless it captures enough signal across:

- operator identity
- institution or operating context
- authority posture
- economic scale
- time horizon
- stated problem
- observed symptoms
- desired outcome
- present constraints
- market pressure
- urgency type
- intervention consequence

## Law 2 — Authority must be tested, not assumed

The system shall explicitly classify authority as:

- DIRECT
- PROXY
- UNCLEAR

Route escalation must be constrained when authority is unclear.

## Law 3 — The problem must be architectural, not cosmetic

The intake must force articulation of:

- the actual problem
- how it manifests
- what it threatens
- what it blocks
- what outcome is sought

If the problem statement is vague, impulsive, performative or cosmetic, the system must downgrade route confidence and may reject the case.

## Law 4 — Readiness is earned

The system shall not assume execution readiness merely because revenue is high, urgency is loud or ambition is grand.

Readiness shall be inferred from:

- clarity
- authority
- governance posture
- execution discipline
- trust condition
- intervention coherence

## Law 5 — Market pressure is signal, not command

Market urgency may sharpen diagnostic attention.

It may never compel unconstitutional escalation.

---

# III. Constitutional Laws of Diagnosis

## Law 6 — Diagnose posture before prescribe action

Every case must first be classified into organisational posture:

- ORDERED
- DRIFTING
- MISALIGNED
- DISORDERED

## Law 7 — Diagnose readiness before escalation

Every case must be assigned readiness tier:

- FRAGILE
- EMERGING
- STABILIZING
- EXECUTION_READY
- SOVEREIGN

## Law 8 — Failure modes are first-class objects

The system must infer, record and carry forward dominant failure modes such as:

- narrative incoherence
- sponsor weakness
- governance erosion
- trust erosion
- mandate ambiguity
- execution fragmentation
- strategic-operational misalignment
- decision-owner ambiguity
- market pressure without institutional order

## Law 9 — Required interventions must be explicit

The system must produce named interventions, not vague encouragement.

Examples:

- clarify sponsor and decision owner
- restore governance discipline
- re-sequence strategic priorities
- stabilise operational cadence
- repair trust before scale
- define mandate and boundaries
- establish escalation order
- tighten commercial positioning under moral constraints

# IV. Constitutional Laws of Routing

## Law 10 — STRATEGY is not a courtesy route

STRATEGY must only be available where the system finds sufficient evidence of:

- meaningful clarity
- decision-bearing authority
- non-trivial substance
- intervention readiness
- adequate institutional order

## Law 11 — DIAGNOSTIC is not a rejection

DIAGNOSTIC is the constitutional holding route for cases where signal is real but readiness is incomplete.

## Law 12 — REJECT is a governance act

REJECT must be used where the system detects:

- unserious signal
- incoherent submission
- insufficient substance
- non-decision-grade intent
- obvious mismatch with advisory mandate
- repeated constitutional insufficiency

## Law 13 — Disorder without foundation must not be escalated

Where systemic disorder is high and narrative clarity is low, the system must route toward foundational doctrine, governance and diagnostic assets before any premium escalation.

## Law 14 — DISORDERED posture is a constitutional brake

A case classified as DISORDERED shall not be routed to STRATEGY unless the system also finds:

- DIRECT authority
- meaningful clarity
- stabilising or higher readiness
- intervention readiness above escalation floor
- no critical narrative incoherence
- no active constitutional disqualifier

## Law 15 — Proxy authority under fragility must not be over-escalated

Where authority is PROXY and readiness is FRAGILE or EMERGING, the system must prefer DIAGNOSTIC unless evidence of constitutional stability is unusually strong.

---

# V. Constitutional Laws of Guidance

## Law 16 — Guidance must match condition, not merely topic

Recommendations must be selected against:

- route
- organisational posture
- readiness tier
- authority type
- failure mode
- intervention need
- market risk
- escalation suitability

## Law 17 — Foundational disorder requires foundational guidance

Where purpose collapse, narrative incoherence, mandate ambiguity or governance erosion are present, at least one foundational asset must be surfaced.

## Law 18 — Do not overserve one asset type

The system must preserve recommendation diversity across:

- doctrine
- playbook
- diagnostic
- brief
- download
- strategy

## Law 19 — Commercial intelligence must remain morally governed

Commercial relevance may sharpen selection.
It may never replace constitutional order.

---

# VI. Constitutional Laws of Governance

## Law 20 — Lower layers are advisory, not sovereign

Adaptive weights, contextual weights, performance overlays and ranking rules are subordinate.

They may refine ordering.

They may not override constitutional disqualifiers.

## Law 21 — Confidence is a gate, not a decoration

Low-confidence signal may inform.

It must not rule.

No case may be promoted to STRATEGY merely because a scoring layer is optimistic.

Where constitutional confidence falls below escalation threshold, the route must degrade to DIAGNOSTIC or REJECT.

## Law 22 — Clicks are not truth

Engagement telemetry may influence attention scoring.

It must not be mistaken for decision usefulness.

## Law 23 — Follow-up is mandatory for real learning

The system shall learn not only from clicks, but from whether guidance improved:

- clarity
- authority
- route quality
- readiness
- conversion appropriateness
- decision quality
- execution fit

## Law 24 — The system shall prefer explainability over theatrical complexity

The constitutional engine must remain inspectable, deterministic and auditable.

No hidden layer may silently override the route.

# VII. Deterministic Routing Thresholds

The constitutional engine shall treat the following thresholds as defaults unless a future constitutional amendment explicitly changes them.

## Baseline thresholds

- clarity below 35 is constitutionally weak
- clarity below 20 is presumptively non-decision-grade unless other signal is exceptional
- narrative coherence below 25 is critical incoherence
- intervention readiness below 40 is below safe diagnostic confidence
- intervention readiness below 60 is below normal strategy readiness
- three or more failure modes with severity of 6 or above constitutes high structural strain
- five or more failure modes combined with critical incoherence creates presumptive rejection risk

## Strategy floor

A case may only route to STRATEGY where all of the following are true:

- clarity score is at least 65
- authority type is DIRECT
- readiness tier is EXECUTION_READY or SOVEREIGN
- intervention readiness is at least 60
- failure mode count is no more than 2
- narrative coherence is at least 50
- posture is not DISORDERED
- no constitutional disqualifier is active

## Diagnostic floor

A case may route to DIAGNOSTIC where signal is real but not yet safe for premium escalation, including cases where:

- authority is PROXY but usable
- readiness is incomplete
- posture is DRIFTING or MISALIGNED
- disorder exists but remains governable
- clarity is developing but still sub-strategic

## Reject floor

A case should be routed to REJECT where the system detects one or more of the following with sufficient severity:

- non-decision-grade substance
- critical incoherence plus severe failure density
- obvious mandate mismatch
- cosmetic or unserious submission
- collapse of minimum constitutional signal

# VIII. System Mandate

This estate exists to do five things:

1. **interrogate signal**
2. **diagnose structure**
3. **govern escalation**
4. **surface fitting guidance**
5. **improve judgment over time**

Any future layer that weakens these five must be treated as unconstitutional.

# IX. Operator Instruction

When in doubt:

- prefer order over novelty
- prefer diagnosis over flattery
- prefer fit over popularity
- prefer governed escalation over easy access
- prefer truth over pleasing copy
- prefer explainability over mystery
- prefer architectures that endure over funnels that merely convert

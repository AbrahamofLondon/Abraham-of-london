Strategy is rarely the issue.

Execution is.

---

### The Misdiagnosis

When performance drops, organisations say:

> “We need a new strategy.”

What they usually need is:
- alignment
- clarity
- disciplined execution

---

### Where Execution Fails

Execution fails when:
- intent is not translated
- leadership signals are inconsistent
- operational clarity is fragmented

---

### The Hidden Truth

Most strategies fail not because they are wrong.

They fail because:
> **the system cannot carry them.**

---

### The Correction

Before rewriting strategy:
- measure alignment
- assess execution clarity
- identify friction

---

### The Strategic Reality

A weak system cannot execute a strong strategy.

But a strong system can recover from an imperfect one.

So the real question is not:

“Is the strategy right?”

It is:

“Is the system capable?”

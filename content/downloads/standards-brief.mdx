<BrandFrame
  title="Standards Brief"
  subtitle="Protect people, work, and name—especially under pressure."
>
  <h2>1) Decisions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Facts first:</strong> single source of truth; date every number.</li>
    <li><strong>Owner & deadline:</strong> one accountable person; one explicit date.</li>
    <li><strong>Reversible vs. irreversible:</strong> bias to speed on reversible calls.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>2) Cadence</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Daily:</strong> status, blockers, risks (15m).</li>
    <li><strong>Weekly:</strong> outcomes scored, lessons captured, next 3 (45m).</li>
    <li><strong>Monthly:</strong> standards review, debt burn-down, runway check.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>3) Communication</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Write it down:</strong> decisions, assumptions, follow-ups.</li>
    <li><strong>No triangulation:</strong> speak to, not about.</li>
    <li><strong>Plain English:</strong> short, dated, action-oriented notes.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>4) Workmanship</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Definition of done:</strong> clear acceptance criteria, demoable.</li>
    <li><strong>Durability:</strong> simple, testable, documented.</li>
    <li><strong>Name:</strong> work that carries your name must be worth keeping.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>5) Security</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Least privilege:</strong> access by role; revoke on exit same day.</li>
    <li><strong>Keys & secrets:</strong> rotate quarterly; never in chat or code.</li>
    <li><strong>Incident drill:</strong> who calls whom; first hour playbook.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>6) Release Discipline</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Branch, test, tag:</strong> no ad-hoc deploys; rollback ready.</li>
    <li><strong>Notes:</strong> concise change log with risks and smoke checks.</li>
    <li><strong>Post-mortems:</strong> blameless, dated, with two preventions.</li>
  </ul>

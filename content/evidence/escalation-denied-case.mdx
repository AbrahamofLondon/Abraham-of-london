## Counterfactual Path

If escalated prematurely:

- reactive intervention deployed
- misaligned resource allocation
- increased political and operational friction

**Result**
- systemic instability increases
- root condition remains unresolved

---

## Outcome Implication

- premature escalation amplifies risk
- governed thresholds prevent misdirected action
- restraint is a strategic decision

---

## Outcome Signal

- escalation delayed
- evidence strengthened
- correct intervention executed later

---

## Board-Level Actions

1. Define escalation thresholds explicitly
2. Require evidence classification before intervention
3. Separate urgency from readiness
4. Institutionalise escalation governance

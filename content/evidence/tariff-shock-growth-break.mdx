## Condition Snapshot

April 2026 tariff escalation converted market instability into enforceable policy conditions.

**Operating Reality:**
- tariff levels disrupt normal trade economics
- supply chains become structurally constrained
- capital reprices exposure ahead of operational response

## System Classification

> Growth pricing invalidated. Survivability pricing dominant.

**Reclassification:**
- valuation driver: growth → resilience

## Decision Frame

**Required Decision**
Reprice operating model assumptions under fragmentation conditions.

**Constraints**
- no immediate monetary relief
- tariff persistence uncertain but structurally relevant
- supply chain redesign requires lead time

---

## Counterfactual Path

If treated as temporary volatility:

- incremental forecast adjustments
- delayed restructuring
- continued reliance on efficiency assumptions

**Result**
- margin compression
- capital misallocation
- exposure amplification

---

## Outcome Implication

- delayed adjustment increases downside asymmetry
- pricing power becomes defensive
- supply chain becomes a valuation variable

---

## Board-Level Actions

1. Reprice growth assumptions immediately
2. Map tariff exposure at supply-chain node level
3. Model 12–18 months elevated input costs
4. Revalidate revenue durability under trade friction
5. Prioritise optionality over optimisation

## Case Classification

- Condition Type: Structural Misalignment
- Domain: Leadership → Team Execution → Governance
- Confidence Level: High
- Decision Relevance: Immediate

---

## Condition Snapshot

Leadership reported high alignment across strategic priorities.

No respondent-based validation had been conducted.

---

## Signal Register

**Primary Signals**
- divergence in priority interpretation across teams
- inconsistent allocation of execution resources

**Secondary Signals**
- mismatch between stated objectives and active work
- absence of shared execution logic

---

## Evidence Basis

- Observed: respondent-derived priority ranking
- Anonymised: team-level behavioural patterns
- Corroborated: cross-response divergence analysis

---

## System Classification

> Declared alignment. Absent execution alignment.

**Reclassification:**
- alignment status: assumed → unverified
- decision basis: perception → incomplete
- execution coherence: fragmented

---

## Decision Frame

**Required Decision**
Validate alignment before executing strategic directives.

**Constraints**
- incomplete visibility into team execution
- leadership bias toward perceived coherence

---

## Counterfactual Path

If treated as communication issue:

- increase messaging
- reinforce strategic narrative
- attribute divergence to individuals

**Result**
- fragmentation deepens
- coordination cost increases
- misalignment persists undetected

---

## Outcome Implication

- alignment cannot be inferred from leadership consensus
- execution behaviour is the only reliable signal
- unmeasured alignment produces flawed decisions

---

## Board-Level Actions

1. Validate alignment using respondent-derived evidence
2. Identify divergence points across teams
3. Reconcile execution priorities with stated objectives
4. Introduce periodic alignment measurement cadence

---

## System Trace

Feeds:

- Team Reality Campaign → evidence generation
- Executive Reporting → validated team signal
- Diagnostic ladder → evidence strengthening

This case establishes:
> leadership alignment is not evidence of system alignment.

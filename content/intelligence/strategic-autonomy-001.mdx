## I. Executive Summary

Autonomy is not binary. Between total dependency and complete sovereignty lies a spectrum—and most institutions misdiagnose their position on it. This briefing introduces the **Autonomy Spectrum**, a diagnostic tool for accurately locating your institution's current state and mapping the path forward.

<Callout type="key" icon="">
  **Core Insight**: The gap between perceived autonomy and actual autonomy averages 31 points across 47 surveyed institutions. You are likely less sovereign than you believe.
</Callout>

---

## II. The Five States of Autonomy

### State 1: **Dependent**
The institution cannot survive without specific external parties. Decisions are dictated, timelines are owned elsewhere, and infrastructure is leased.

**Characteristics**:
- Single points of failure in all critical systems
- No independent decision window
- External parties control access to resources

**Motto**: *"We need them to exist."*

---

### State 2: **Vulnerable**
The institution can survive short-term disruption but would fail under sustained pressure. Redundancies exist but are untested.

**Characteristics**:
- Some redundancy in infrastructure
- Limited decision windows (days or weeks)
- External relationships are asymmetric

**Motto**: *"We hope they don't change the terms."*

---

### State 3: **Resilient**
The institution can absorb shocks and maintain core functions. Multiple options exist for critical dependencies.

**Characteristics**:
- Tested redundancies
- Decision windows measured in months
- Relationships are negotiable, not dictatorial

**Motto**: *"We can adapt."*

---

### State 4: **Autonomous**
The institution controls its critical infrastructure and decision timeline. External relationships are chosen, not required.

**Characteristics**:
- Owned infrastructure
- Multi-year decision windows
- Internal intelligence capacity

**Motto**: *"We choose our partners."*

---

### State 5: **Sovereign**
The institution is designed to outlast its founders and operate independently across generations. It shapes its environment rather than being shaped by it.

**Characteristics**:
- Perpetual structures
- [Legacy](/vault/lexicon/legacy) architecture in place
- Narrative and framing control

**Motto**: *"We set the terms."*

## III. The Diagnostic

Where is your institution today?

| Indicator | Dependent | Vulnerable | Resilient | Autonomous | Sovereign |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| Infrastructure | Fully leased | Mixed ownership | Owned critical | Fully owned | Generational |
| Decision window | Days | Weeks | Months | Years | Decades |
| Intelligence | 100% external | 80% external | 50/50 | 80% internal | Primary source |
| Succession plan | None | Named | Documented | Tested | Perpetual |

The photograph appears to have no commissioner. That is what makes it different from everything that came before it in this series. The cave painting was made by a community. The stele was ordered by a king. The canvas was commissioned by an emperor. The poster was printed by a state.

The photograph appears to have none of that. It appears to be the world pressing itself onto film through a machine that has no ideology, no patron, no agenda. That appearance — the aura of mechanical objectivity — is what makes photography the most powerful propaganda tool ever invented. Not because photographs lie more than paintings. Because photographs appear not to lie at all.

I wrote about three photographs in the sixth part of this series. Iwo Jima — true image, manufactured meaning. Napalm Girl — true image that escaped the official record. Tank Man — true image that the people it belongs to have never seen.

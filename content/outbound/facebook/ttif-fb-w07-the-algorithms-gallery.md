In 2020, Facebook's internal research showed that the company's own algorithm was amplifying false information at a rate the company's data scientists called dangerous. The research was presented to senior leadership. The decision was made not to change the algorithm.

This is not a story about technology. It is a story about the same choice Napoleon made: what to show, what to hide, and which metric to optimise.

The algorithm has no handprint. That is the point. When no one pressed their hand to the wall, no one can be asked to account for the mark it left.

I wrote about this in the seventh part of my series. About the distributed handprint. About what happens when accountability is designed out of the system. About the spreadsheet as the stele of the twenty-first century.

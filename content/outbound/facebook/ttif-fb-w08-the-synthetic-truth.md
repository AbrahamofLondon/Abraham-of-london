You are watching a video of a person you know. They are saying something they did not say. You cannot tell. Not from the video. Only from what you know about them — from the trust you have built, the context you carry, the years of witness that no image can replace.

The era of visual evidence is over. The era of personal trust has returned.

That is not nothing. It is the oldest verification mechanism in human history. It is the question the cave community asked of the person who pressed the handprint. It is the question the conquered people asked of the king who carved the stele. Personal trust preceded photography by thirty thousand years. The synthetic image does not defeat it. The synthetic image reveals that it was always what we were actually relying on.

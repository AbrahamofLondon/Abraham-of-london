There is a photograph of Stalin walking beside the Moscow Canal in 1937. Three men walk together: Stalin, Molotov, Yezhov.

After Yezhov was purged and executed in 1940, the photograph was retouched. Yezhov disappeared. The canal bank where he stood became empty water. The retoucher was so skilled that if you do not know to look, you do not look. You see two men and a canal.

The removal of Yezhov from the Soviet historical record was not performed once, in one darkroom. Subscribers to the Great Soviet Encyclopaedia received inserts in the post with instructions to cut out pages and paste in replacements. The erasure was distributed to the people who owned the books, performed by thousands of hands, with scissors and paste, in thousands of homes.

We know Yezhov was removed because the before-photograph survived somewhere. In cases where the original did not survive, we would not know there was ever a man standing on the canal bank.

The question the series is asking is how many removals left no before.

You have made things that will not survive.

Not because you failed to preserve them. Because the infrastructure you created them on was not yours to preserve.

The blog you wrote in 2008 on a platform that closed in 2014. The photographs uploaded to a service that was acquired and discontinued. The forum thread where you worked through an idea across a hundred posts over three months. The community that existed only on a platform that is now gone.

These are not minor losses. They are the record of how you thought, who you were in conversation with, what you were figuring out. The Griot's transmission. The Homer's performance. And they ended not because the chain broke but because the platform closed, without notice, without archive, without recourse.

The difference between you and everyone who lost things in this series is that you have been told.

The Griot did not know the transmission would end. The Library's curators did not know the fire was coming. You know that the server will close, the format will become unreadable, the platform will be acquired. You know, and you have a choice.

The inventory of what survives is compiled by the living.

The history of every major communication technology is the same: it externalised something that had previously lived inside the human being.

Writing externalised memory. Print externalised distribution. Digital networks externalised reach. AI is beginning to externalise synthesis and interpretation.

Each step offered an enormous gain and a subtle loss. The gain was obvious and collective. The loss was quiet and individual. Fewer people needed to hold the whole argument in their minds at once. Fewer needed to practise the discipline of extended composition. Fewer needed to do the slow work of forming a view before seeking confirmation.

The organisations and individuals who will thrive in the next decade are not those who outsource the most to AI. They are those who learn precisely which capacities to protect — and treat those capacities as strategic assets, not nostalgic habits.

Authorship is not dead. It is becoming governance. The question is who is governing.

A new piece from the editorial series The Mind's Clay — on writing, cognition, and what every communication technology does to the mind that uses it.

The argument is not anti-technology. It is pro-precision. Each tool that humanity has used to extend knowledge has also changed the cognitive profile of those who used it. Oral cultures were built for memory. Literate cultures were built for abstraction. Digital cultures were built for retrieval.

What are AI cultures being built for?

That is the question this essay tries to hold open rather than answer prematurely.

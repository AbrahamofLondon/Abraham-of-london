There is a shift happening in authorship that is worth naming precisely.

It is not that machines are replacing writers. It is that the valuable act of writing is moving from generation toward governance. The author increasingly becomes the person who decides what deserves to exist, what is accurate, and what reflects the purpose — rather than the person who produces every sentence.

That is not a diminishment. It is a change in where the difficulty lives.

The hardest part of writing has always been judgement: knowing what to say, when to say it, and whether the argument holds. AI does not remove that difficulty. It relocates it.

— from The Mind's Clay editorial series

A question for founders, operators, and those who lead institutions.

Your organisation holds knowledge. Some of that knowledge lives in documents, systems, and records. Some of it lives in the people who happen to still be employed there. Some of it lives in habits that no one remembers choosing.

The history of writing tells us that the transition from memory-based knowledge to record-based knowledge was not automatic, clean, or neutral. Organisations that learned to write their knowledge down gained enormous reach and stability. But they also created new vulnerabilities: records that outlived their context, doctrines that persisted long after the conditions that justified them had changed.

What is the current state of your organisation's institutional memory? Is it living in people who are about to leave? Is it locked in formats no one reviews? Is it being shaped by AI tools that your team has not formally considered?

These are not technology questions. They are governance questions.

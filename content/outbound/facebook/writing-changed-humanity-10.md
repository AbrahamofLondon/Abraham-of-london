If the most valued skill shifts — from knowing facts to asking good questions, checking sources, and integrating outputs into judgement — what does that mean for how you hire, develop, and evaluate people?

The 50-year forecast on AI is not primarily about job displacement. It is about cognitive stratification. People who use AI as a thinking partner will deepen their capacity. People who use it as a shortcut may lose intellectual muscle without noticing.

Organisations face the same dynamic at scale. Those that treat AI adoption as a productivity exercise alone will produce faster mediocrity. Those that treat it as a question about cognition — about what their people need to be able to think — will build durable institutional intelligence.

What kind of thinking is your organisation trying to protect?

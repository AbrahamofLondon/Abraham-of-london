Six meetings. No decision. Everyone blamed execution.

The team called it a pacing problem. A resourcing issue. A dependency.

It was none of those things.

The real issue was that nobody in the room could force the decision. Not wouldn't — couldn't. The authority to absorb the consequence did not exist in any role present.

A blocked decision gets managed like workflow until cost becomes visible. By then, the options have narrowed and the price has gone up.

I built a system that exposes that gap in under two minutes. Six questions. No prep. It tells you whether the problem is execution, authority, definition, or avoidance.

If it is wrong, ignore it. If it is right, you will know immediately.

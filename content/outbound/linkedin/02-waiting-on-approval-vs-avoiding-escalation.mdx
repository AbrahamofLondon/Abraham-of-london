"We are waiting on approval from finance."

I have heard this sentence in three different organisations in the past year. In all three cases, finance had not been asked.

What was actually happening: the team knew the answer. They knew it would trigger a difficult conversation with a senior stakeholder. So the delay was framed as dependency instead of choice.

"Waiting on approval" is often false reporting. Not intentionally dishonest — structurally dishonest. The system makes it easier to describe a blocker than to name what is being avoided.

The distinction between missing approval and missing courage changes the next move entirely.

One requires a process fix. The other requires someone to pick up the phone.

Have you seen this pattern inside your organisation?

There is a difference between a slow decision and a blocked one. Most organisations treat both as pacing issues and lose weeks.

A slow decision still has a path. It moves when information arrives, when calendars align, when priorities shift. Time is the constraint. Time can be managed.

A blocked decision has motion without authority. Meetings happen. Slides get updated. People stay busy around the issue. But the decision does not advance because nobody present can absorb the consequence of making it.

One improves with clarity and urgency.

The other improves only when someone can force resolution — and that person is often not in the room.

The first thing any operator should do with a stalled decision is determine which system they are actually in. The fix for slow is different from the fix for blocked. Applying the wrong one wastes the time you do not have.

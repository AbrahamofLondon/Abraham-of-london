A team I was advising said they needed "cross-functional alignment" before they could move.

Seven stakeholders. Three weeks of meetings. A shared document nobody updated.

I asked one question: "If you had to decide in 24 hours, who would make the call?"

Silence.

That silence was the diagnosis. "We need alignment" is almost always a translation of "nobody has clear authority to take the consequence."

So the decision gets stretched into a coordination exercise. Everyone contributes. Nobody commits. The calendar fills up while the problem compounds.

Once ownership was named — one person, one consequence, one deadline — the need for alignment collapsed. The decision moved that week.

Where in your organisation is "alignment" doing the work that ownership should be doing?

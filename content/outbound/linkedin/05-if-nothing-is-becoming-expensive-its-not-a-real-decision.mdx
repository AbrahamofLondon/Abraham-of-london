If nothing is becoming more expensive while you wait, you are not facing a real decision.

Real decisions consume something. Time. Budget. Political capital. Optionality. Relationships. Every week of delay has a measurable cost — even when nobody is measuring it.

That is why so many teams confuse discussion with commitment. The meeting feels productive. The slide deck improves. The stakeholder map grows. But nothing is at risk.

The moment you attach a cost to delay — not a theoretical cost, a real one — the discussion changes. People who were "broadly aligned" suddenly have objections. People who were silent suddenly have opinions.

That is not dysfunction. That is the decision becoming real.

No cost means no decision. Just theatre with a calendar invite.

I spent years watching operators asked to fix things they could not fix.

Not because they lacked skill. Because the decision upstream had never been made.

Execution problems start after the decision. The call has been made, the direction is set, and now the question is delivery: speed, quality, coordination.

Authority problems stop the decision from becoming real. No amount of delivery discipline fixes a decision that was never taken. You cannot execute your way out of a missing mandate.

The difference matters because the interventions are completely different:

Execution failure → improve process, resource, coordination.
Authority failure → name the owner, surface the consequence, force the call.

Most organisations default to execution fixes for authority problems. That creates churn, rework, and a team that looks busy while the real issue sits untouched.

Where is this happening in your organisation right now?

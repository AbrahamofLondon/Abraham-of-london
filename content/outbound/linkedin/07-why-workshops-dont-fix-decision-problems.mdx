Workshops surface views. They are weak tools for forcing commitments.

If the authority structure is unclear before the workshop, it will be unclear after it — just with better Post-it notes.

The room leaves informed but unchanged because workshops are designed for consensus, not consequence. The moment after discussion is where organisations actually stall. The options are clear. And nobody can say: "I own the downside. It starts tomorrow."

That is not a facilitation problem. That is a structural one.

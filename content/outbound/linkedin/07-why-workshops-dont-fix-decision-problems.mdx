I have watched a programme worth tens of millions stall because of a well-facilitated workshop.

Not despite the workshop. Because of it.

The workshop surfaced views. Documented perspectives. Created a shared language. The room left feeling informed, coordinated, and aligned.

Then nothing happened.

Workshops are useful for surfacing views. They are weak tools for forcing commitments. If the authority structure is unclear before the workshop, it will be unclear after it — just with better Post-it notes.

The room leaves informed but unchanged because workshops are designed for consensus, not consequence.

The moment after discussion is where most organisations actually stall. The views are on the board. The options are clear. And nobody in the room can say: "This is what we are doing. I own the downside. It starts tomorrow."

That is not a facilitation problem. That is a structural one.

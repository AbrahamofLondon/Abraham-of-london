Try this with a decision that has been stalling.

Compress the timeline to 24 hours. Then ask three questions:

1. Who can make the call?
2. What do they need that they do not have?
3. What happens if they do nothing?

If nobody can answer cleanly, the problem is structural. The decision does not have a valid owner.

If everyone can answer but nobody moves, the problem is avoidance. The answer exists but the consequence is unwanted.

That single test separates months of false complexity from the real issue.

I built a system that formalises this test. Six questions. Under two minutes. No email required. It tells you which of the four failure modes you are in — and what to do next.

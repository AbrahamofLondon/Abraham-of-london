"We need more data before we can decide."

Sometimes that is true. Most of the time it is not.

Most requests for more data are requests for more time without ownership. The evidence threshold keeps moving because the consequence is still unwanted. The analysis is not incomplete — the willingness to act on it is.

Here is the test: if the data came back tomorrow and confirmed your worst assumption, would the decision move? Or would someone ask for a second opinion?

If the answer is second opinion, data was never the bottleneck. Courage was.

The moment analysis becomes protective delay is the moment the real decision starts. Everything before that was preparation theatre.

Most teams optimise the workflow before they commit to the move.

Sprints get planned. Roles get clarified. Dashboards get built. The machinery looks productive. But the core decision — the one that determines whether any of this work matters — has not been made.

Once the call is forced, half the optimisation work disappears. The team discovers that three of the five workstreams were hedging, not executing.

The larger gain is never efficiency. It is forcing the decision that makes efficiency meaningful.

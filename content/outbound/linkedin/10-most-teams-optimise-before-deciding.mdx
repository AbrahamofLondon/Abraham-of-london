Most teams optimise the workflow before they commit to the move.

They tune process around uncertainty and call it progress. Sprints get planned. Roles get clarified. Dashboards get built. The machinery looks productive.

But the core decision — the one that determines whether any of this work matters — has not been made.

That is how organisations scale indecision. Not through laziness, but through premature optimisation of the wrong layer.

Once the call is forced, half the optimisation work disappears. The team suddenly discovers that three of the five workstreams were hedging, not executing.

The larger gain is never efficiency. It is forcing the decision that makes efficiency meaningful.

If your team is optimising around something that has not been decided yet, stop. Decide first. Then optimise.

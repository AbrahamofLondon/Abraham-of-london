The org chart is not the decision map.

The meeting host is not always the owner. The loudest voice is often not the final authority. The person with the title may not have the mandate.

In every stalled decision I have examined, the gap between formal authority and actual authority is where the delay lives.

Formal authority says: "This person should decide."
Actual authority asks: "Who can absorb the consequence and close the loop?"

These are different people more often than most organisations admit.

When they are the same person, decisions move. When they are different people, the formal owner convenes meetings while the actual authority makes the real call informally — or nobody does, and the decision drifts.

Identifying who can actually decide is not a political exercise. It is the first structural intervention.

Who in your organisation holds the title but not the mandate?

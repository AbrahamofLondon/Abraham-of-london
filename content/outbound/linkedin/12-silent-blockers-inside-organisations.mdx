The most expensive blockers are usually quiet.

They do not argue in the room. They do not raise formal objections. They do not send the email that says "I disagree."

They delay outside the room.

A missed escalation that nobody chases. An unnamed veto that surfaces as "further review needed." A private objection shared in the corridor but never in the meeting. A dependency that everybody knows about and nobody wants to surface because surfacing it triggers a conversation nobody wants to have.

I have seen decisions stall for months because of one quiet objection that was never formally raised. The meeting notes showed consensus. The calendar showed progress. The decision showed nothing.

Silent blockers harden over time. What starts as a private hesitation becomes an informal veto becomes a structural feature of how the organisation avoids consequence.

The earlier they are surfaced, the cheaper they are to resolve.

What silent blocker is operating in your organisation right now?

The most expensive blockers do not argue in the room. They delay outside it.

A missed escalation nobody chases. An unnamed veto that surfaces as "further review needed." A private objection shared in the corridor but never in the meeting.

The meeting notes show consensus. The calendar shows progress. The decision shows nothing.

Silent blockers harden over time. What starts as private hesitation becomes informal veto becomes structural feature. The earlier they are surfaced, the cheaper they are to resolve.

Escalation avoidance has a recognisable sequence. Once you see it, you cannot unsee it.

The issue is reframed as complexity. The timeline gets extended. More stakeholders appear. The decision is described as "still being worked." Someone new inherits the problem. The cycle restarts.

Each stage feels reasonable in isolation. Together, they are a system for converting urgency into delay without anyone being accountable for it.

The cost is not the time lost. It is the options that disappeared while the pattern ran.

Shared ownership is the most expensive lie in corporate governance.

When three people "co-own" a decision, each waits for the others to move first. Nobody escalates because escalation implies the others failed. Nobody commits because commitment implies the others agreed.

The result: permission to delay without visible refusal. The work continues while the decision does not.

Ask this: which co-owner would take the call alone if the other two were unavailable? That person is the real owner. Name them.

"We need to get aligned before we move."

This sentence has delayed more decisions than any external constraint I have seen.

Alignment is useful right up to the point it becomes cover. And in most stalled decisions, that point passed weeks ago.

Teams say they need more alignment when they mean nobody wants the consequence. The language of collaboration disguises the absence of ownership. The room feels coordinated. The decision stays optional.

Here is the distinction that matters: agreement is not authority.

A room full of people who agree on the problem is not the same as one person who will own the outcome. Agreement without authority produces meetings. Authority without agreement produces decisions.

Organisations that confuse the two end up with alignment processes that feel productive and decisions that never close.

Speed does not return when people agree. Speed returns when someone decides — and the organisation lets them.

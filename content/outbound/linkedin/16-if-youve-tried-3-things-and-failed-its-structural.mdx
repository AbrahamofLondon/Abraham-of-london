If you have already tried three fixes and nothing changed, stop relabelling the issue.

That is not bad luck. That is not poor execution. That is not the wrong people.

That is structure protecting itself.

When the same problem survives three different interventions, the problem is not at the intervention layer. It is at the authority layer. The path to resolution is broken, hidden, or politically unsafe — and every surface-level fix routes around the real issue instead of through it.

I have seen this pattern across infrastructure programmes, commercial operations, and institutional governance. The symptoms change. The underlying dynamic does not.

The system I built is designed for exactly this moment. The point where you have tried enough that you know the problem is not what it appears to be.

Six questions. Under two minutes. It tells you whether the failure mode is authority, definition, execution, or avoidance.

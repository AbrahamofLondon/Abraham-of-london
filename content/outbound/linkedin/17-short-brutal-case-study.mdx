Revenue was bleeding. The room had the answer for three weeks. Nobody would say it.

Six meetings. Stated blocker: "cross-functional alignment." Real blocker: nobody wanted to own the downside of the call. The pivot would upset a partnership. Someone would have to deliver that message.

Once that owner was named — one person, one consequence — the decision moved in a day.

Three weeks. Six meetings. All because "who owns the downside?" was never asked out loud.

Strip away the project language for a minute.

What are you actually avoiding? A hard tradeoff. A visible escalation. A named owner. A decision that closes options.

Most delay becomes completely understandable the moment you answer that honestly. The delay is not irrational — it is protective.

The problem is that avoidance has a cost. And unlike the decision itself, the cost of avoidance is not optional. It accumulates whether you acknowledge it or not.

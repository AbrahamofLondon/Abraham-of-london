Your team already knows where the decision is stuck.

They know who is blocking it. They know what nobody wants to make explicit. They have known for weeks.

They are not saying it because saying it has consequences. Naming the real blocker means someone has to act. Identifying the silent veto means confronting the person holding it. Admitting the decision is being avoided means accepting that the last month of meetings was theatre.

The problem is not information. Your team has plenty of information. The problem is that truth is currently more expensive than delay.

That changes when you build a structure where evidence is mandatory, ownership is named, and the next move has a deadline. When the cost of silence exceeds the cost of speaking, people speak.

That is not a culture problem. It is an incentive problem. And incentive problems are structural, not motivational.

What is your team not saying right now? And what would have to change for them to say it?

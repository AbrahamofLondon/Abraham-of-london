Most organisations still treat decision latency as an operating inconvenience.

It is not.

It is a financial liability created by the gap between recognising a condition and enforcing the decision that resolves it.

The expensive part is rarely the meeting.

The expensive part is the unresolved contradiction that keeps recruiting time, trust, and coordination into the same problem.

If the decision is known but not enforced, the system is already paying.

That cost should be priced before it becomes visible in performance.

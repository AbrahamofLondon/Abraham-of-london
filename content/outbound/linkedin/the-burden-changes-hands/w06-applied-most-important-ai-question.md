Most AI governance conversations in organisations focus on the wrong question.

The typical framing is capability-oriented: what can the system do, what are its limitations, where might it produce errors. These are legitimate questions. They are also preliminary questions — they establish the tool's properties but say nothing about the institutional arrangements required to use it responsibly.

The question that matters more is accountability-oriented: who is responsible for what the system produces?

This is not the same question as "who approves AI-generated output before it goes out." Approval is a step. Accountability is a structural fact. Accountability means that when something goes wrong — when an AI-generated document contains an error, advances a misleading framing, or creates a downstream problem — there is a named person or function whose role includes responsibility for that outcome.

In practice, most organisations deploying AI do not have a clear answer to this question, because the answer does not emerge automatically from the technology. It has to be decided — a governance choice about who owns what.

Three questions that are worth working through explicitly before significant AI deployment:

Who is accountable for the content of AI-generated documents that leave the organisation — client communications, reports, proposals? This should be a named role, not "the person who wrote the prompt" (prompting is not authorship) and not "the AI team" (they own the tools, not the output).

What is the review standard for AI-generated content before it is treated as institutional text? "Someone read it" is not a standard. A standard specifies what the reviewer is looking for and what authority they have to change or block the output.

When AI-generated content that has been distributed is found to be incorrect, what is the correction process and who initiates it? An institution without a correction process is not managing AI — it is hoping.

The technology is moving fast. The governance can be built at any speed you choose. That choice is yours.

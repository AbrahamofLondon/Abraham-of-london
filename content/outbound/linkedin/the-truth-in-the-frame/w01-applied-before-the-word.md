You are making records right now.

Every decision you document, every email that sets a position, every meeting minutes that captures what was agreed and omits what was disputed — these are handprints. You are deciding what the record will say and what it will leave out.

The cave painter faced the same question: what does the community need to remember? The answer shaped what was painted, what was preserved, and what was lost when the cave sealed.

Your organisation faces the same question every day. The records you keep will outlast the meetings they document. They will be read by people who were not in the room. They will become the official version of events — not because they are complete, but because they survived.

What is your organisation's recording technology structurally incapable of preserving? And what is being lost because of it?

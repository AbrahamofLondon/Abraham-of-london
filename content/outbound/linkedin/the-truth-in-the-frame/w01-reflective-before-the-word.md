The handprint on the cave wall in France is 36,000 years old. It survived the end of the Ice Age, the rise and fall of every empire, two world wars, and the invention of the internet.

The average URL lasts two years.

The oldest recording technology on earth — mineral pigment on stone, pressed by a human hand — has a better survival rate than the newest one. We have lost entire early internet archives, digital art movements, and online communities in thirty years. The cave painter's record is still there. Ours is dissolving.

That is not a metaphor. It is a measurable fact about the relationship between medium and permanence.

What are you creating now that will not exist in twenty years, and does that matter to you?

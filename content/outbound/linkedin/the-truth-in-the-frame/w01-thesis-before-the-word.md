Every recording technology has a bias. It is not neutral.

Cave painting preserves archetypes and recurring events — the hunt, the herd, the dangerous animal — not individual biographies. We do not know the name of a single person who painted a cave wall.

Stone carving preserves what rulers wanted carved. Writing preserves what scribes were paid to record. Photography preserves what was in frame, not what was behind the photographer. AI generates what the training data considered plausible.

Understanding what any era remembered requires understanding what its dominant technology was structurally capable of preserving — and what it was structurally incapable of holding.

The same question applies to your organisation's recordkeeping today. What is your current recording technology structurally incapable of preserving? And what is being lost because of it?

I wrote about this in the first part of a new series on documentation, truth, and power — from the cave wall to the deepfake.

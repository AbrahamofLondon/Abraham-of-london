The oldest surviving record of a battle is the Stele of the Vultures, carved by the winner around 2450 BCE. The losers left no stele.

This is not incidental. It is structural. Writing centralises memory in the same way that taxation centralises wealth. Both require a class of administrators who are loyal to the centre, not to the periphery. Both make it possible for the centre to claim knowledge that the periphery cannot verify.

In your organisation, who controls the record? Who decides what gets documented, what gets stored, and what gets forgotten? The answer will tell you more about where power actually resides than any org chart.

The king's inscription was verifiable only by the king's scribes. Your organisation's records are verifiable only by the people who control the archive. That difference is the entire history of official memory.

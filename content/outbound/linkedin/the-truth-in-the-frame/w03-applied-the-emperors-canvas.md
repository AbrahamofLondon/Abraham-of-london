David painted what Napoleon needed. Goya painted what he saw. Both used the same medium. The difference was not in the paint. It was in the question: who is this serving?

Every organisation has its David and its Goya. The David produces what the commission requires — the investor deck that shows only the growth, the internal memo that frames the restructuring as opportunity, the press release that presents the departure as mutual. The Goya produces what the evidence shows — the data that contradicts the narrative, the customer feedback that the strategy does not address, the risk that was flagged and ignored.

Both are technically accurate. Both use the same medium. The difference is not in the accuracy of the facts. It is in the question of who the communication is designed to serve.

The next time you review a major communication from your organisation, ask: who is this serving? The answer will tell you more than any fact-check.

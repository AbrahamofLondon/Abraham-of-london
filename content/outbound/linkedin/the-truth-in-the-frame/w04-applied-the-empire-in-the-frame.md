The British Empire documented what it needed to see. What it needed not to see, it did not document.

The Survey of India mapped the subcontinent with extraordinary precision — enabling more efficient taxation, more precise military movement, more complete administrative control. The census counted. The courts recorded. The schools taught English. The missionaries translated.

What the vast colonial archive does not contain is the perspective of the people being counted, mapped, taxed, educated, converted, and starved. Their records — the songs, the petitions, the family genealogies, the oral histories, the letters that were never answered — exist in fragments, in archives that were not built to preserve them, in languages the empire did not value.

The absence is not a gap. It is a choice.

Every organisation makes the same choice. What is your organisation choosing not to document? Whose perspective is missing from your official records?

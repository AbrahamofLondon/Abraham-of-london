The poster-as-prediction-painted-as-accomplished-fact did not die with the Soviet Union. It found a new medium.

Every major corporation now publishes an annual sustainability report. The format is glossy. The metrics are specific. The emissions targets are presented as trajectories that are already being achieved. The diversity commitments are illustrated with charts showing progress toward parity.

The underlying data frequently shows the opposite. Emissions are rising. Diversity metrics are stagnating. But the report must be published, because the report is required by the capital markets that screen investments against ESG criteria. The corporation that does not publish a sustainability report is at a competitive disadvantage. The corporation that publishes an honest sustainability report is at an even greater disadvantage, because honesty would show that the targets are not being met, and the capital would flow elsewhere.

No gun. The same choice.

The future is painted as present. The prediction is rendered as accomplished fact. The reader is shown a world that does not yet exist and told that it does.

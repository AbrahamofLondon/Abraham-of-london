In 2020, Facebook's internal research showed that the company's own algorithm was amplifying false information at a rate the company's data scientists called dangerous.

The research was presented to senior leadership. The decision was made not to change the algorithm. The engagement metrics were too strong.

This is not a story about technology. It is a story about the same choice Napoleon made: what to show, what to hide, and which metric to optimise.

The algorithm was built by engineers. The engagement optimisation target was set by product managers. The decision not to change the ranking was made by executives. The advertising model that made engagement the right target was built by a business development team.

No single person made the decision that produced the outcome. Every person made a decision that was correct given their incentive structure.

This is not a bug in the system. This is the system.

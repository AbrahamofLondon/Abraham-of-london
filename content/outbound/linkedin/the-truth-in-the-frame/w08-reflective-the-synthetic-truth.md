The synthetic image carries the handprint of everyone who contributed to the training data and the handprint of no one who decided this image should exist. It is the first mark in human history that was pressed by a crowd that never assembled, for a purpose that was never stated, on a wall that has no location.

The return of personal trust is not a retreat. It is a recognition.

The photograph gave you the illusion that you were using something more reliable than trust. You were not. You were trusting the photographer, the editor, the publication, the institution, the chain of custody from event to image to your hands. The photograph appeared to have no commissioner. It appeared to be the world pressing itself onto film. That appearance was the most successful illusion in the history of documentation.

The synthetic image has removed the illusion. What remains is what was always there.

That is not nothing. It is the oldest verification mechanism in human history. It is also the one that requires the most from you.

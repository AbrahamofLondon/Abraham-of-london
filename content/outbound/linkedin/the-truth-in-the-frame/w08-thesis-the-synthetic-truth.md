The era in which a photograph or video constituted evidence of an event has ended. Not diminished. Ended.

This is not an argument about the future. It is a description of the present. The technology to produce convincing synthetic video of a person saying something they never said is available to anyone with a consumer-grade graphics card and access to open-source models. It is not restricted to intelligence agencies or state propaganda ministries. It is not expensive. It is not difficult. It is not rare.

Every previous part of this series showed a record being manipulated by someone who needed the manipulation to serve a purpose — legitimacy, empire, doctrine, engagement. The manipulation was intentional. There was always a commission, a choice, a hand on the chisel.

The synthetic image requires none of that. The technology can produce convincing false records as a byproduct of its existence, without a commissioner, without an intent, without a hand. A model trained on images of a person can produce new images of that person in situations that never occurred, not because anyone asked it to, but because that is what the model is capable of.

The handprint of Part 7 belonged to everyone and therefore to no one. The handprint of Part 8 belongs to a training dataset and a parameter count. There is no one to ask.

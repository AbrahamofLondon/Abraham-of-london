Your organisation's institutional knowledge lives in people, not documents. When the person who knows leaves, the knowledge leaves with them.

The Chauvet Cave paintings survived because stone is durable. The meaning of those paintings — the stories, the songs, the context — did not survive because it was carried only in the people who made them. When those people died, the meaning died with them.

Every organisation has the same vulnerability. The person who knows why a process works the way it does. The person who holds the history of a key client relationship. The person who understands why a particular decision was made three years ago.

If that knowledge exists only in them, it will leave when they do.

The question is not whether you have documentation. It is whether the documentation carries the context — or just the surface.

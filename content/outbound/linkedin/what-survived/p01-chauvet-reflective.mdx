What have you created that only exists in you?

Not your documents. Not your deliverables. The knowledge that has not been written down because it has not needed to be — the understanding of how something works that you carry in your mind and have never fully transferred.

The Chauvet painters made something that survived thirty-six thousand years. But the meaning of what they made — the stories that explained it, the songs that accompanied it — survived nothing, because it was carried only in people who eventually died without passing it on.

You are not a cave painting. You are the person who carries the meaning.

What happens to what you know when you are gone?

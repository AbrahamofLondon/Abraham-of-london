Who in your life holds knowledge that exists only in them?

Not knowledge that could be looked up. Knowledge that lives in experience — the understanding of how something works that was built over years and has never been fully written down.

A parent who knows the family history that no one recorded. A mentor who carries the context behind decisions the organisation has forgotten. A colleague who understands why a process works the way it does — not the process itself, but the reasoning behind it.

The griot carried knowledge that could not be written down because writing was not the medium it belonged in. Some knowledge is like that. It belongs in people.

But it also belongs in the next person. Have you asked them to pass it on?

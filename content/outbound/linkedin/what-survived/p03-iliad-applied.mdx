The oral tradition that kept Troy alive is more durable than your digital archive — because it was maintained by people with strong incentives to maintain it.

The Homeric performers were not hobbyists. Their livelihood, reputation, and community standing depended on their ability to reconstruct the poem accurately. The social infrastructure around the tradition — the festivals, the competitions, the audiences who knew the material — created the conditions for preservation.

Digital archives are maintained by whoever pays the server bill. When the payment stops, the archive stops. There is no social infrastructure around most digital preservation — only a cost centre.

The question is not whether your organisation has a knowledge management system. It is whether anyone has an incentive to keep it alive.

What are you trusting to survive that you have not tested for durability?

The Iliad survived four centuries without paper because the transmission system was designed for resilience. The performers were trained. The audience knew the material. The tradition was distributed across enough people that any single loss was survivable.

Most of what we create digitally is not designed for resilience. It is designed for the moment. The post, the document, the project file — created for immediate use, stored on a platform that could close, in a format that could become unreadable.

The technology that seemed fragile — the trained human memory, the performance tradition — was not. The technology that seems permanent — the digital file, the cloud platform — is not.

What are you trusting that you have not tested?

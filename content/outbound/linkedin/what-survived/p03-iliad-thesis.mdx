A 15,000-line poem survived four centuries without paper. Your URLs last two years.

The Iliad was composed around 800 BCE. The events it describes happened around 1200 BCE. For four hundred years, the story existed only in trained human minds and public performance. The formulaic structure — "rosy-fingered Dawn," "swift-footed Achilles" — was not poetic decoration. It was mnemonic technology: the system by which a performer could reconstruct the poem without memorising it word for word.

The average lifespan of a URL is two years.

The oral tradition that kept Troy alive is more durable, by the measure of actual survival, than the digital culture that has existed for forty years and is already losing significant parts of itself.

The technology that seemed more fragile was not. The technology that seems permanent is not.

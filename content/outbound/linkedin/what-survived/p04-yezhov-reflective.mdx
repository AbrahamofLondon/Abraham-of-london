The most dangerous erasures are the ones you cannot detect.

We know Yezhov was removed from the photograph because the original survived somewhere. In cases where the original did not survive, we would not know there was ever a man standing on the canal bank. We would see only the smooth water.

What has been removed from your organisation's record that you do not know about?

Not deleted — removed. The decision that was never documented. The email thread that was never archived. The meeting where a different path was considered and rejected, with no minutes taken.

The removal leaves no trace of the taking. The only evidence that something is missing is the memory of someone who was there — and when that person leaves, the evidence leaves with them.

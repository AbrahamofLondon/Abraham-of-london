The most frightening thing about the Yezhov photograph is not what was added. It is what was removed so cleanly you would never know to look — unless the original survived somewhere to tell you there had been something to remove.

In 1937, Nikolai Yezhov walked alongside Stalin beside the Moscow Canal. The photograph shows three men: Stalin, Molotov, Yezhov. After Yezhov was executed in 1940, the photograph was retouched. Yezhov disappeared. The canal bank where he stood became empty water.

The retoucher did not lie. They removed. Fabrication adds something false. Removal takes something true and leaves no trace of the taking.

We know Yezhov was removed because the original survived. In cases where the original did not survive, we would not know there was ever a man standing there.

The question the series asks is how many removals left no before.

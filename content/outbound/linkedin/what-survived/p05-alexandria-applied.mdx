Your organisation's knowledge management system has the same vulnerability as the Library of Alexandria: the catalogue of what you know depends on someone knowing what to catalogue.

When the person who maintains the catalogue leaves, the catalogue becomes incomplete. When the catalogue becomes incomplete, the knowledge it points to becomes inaccessible — not because it was deleted, but because no one knows it exists.

The Library of Alexandria's catalogue burned with the books. Your organisation's catalogue may still exist on the server. But if no one knows how to navigate it, or what was supposed to be in it, the effect is the same.

What would you lose if your most important knowledge repository disappeared tomorrow — and how long would it take you to notice?

What would you lose if your most important knowledge repository disappeared tomorrow — and how long would it take you to notice?

The Library of Alexandria cannot tell us what it held because the catalogue burned with the books. We know some of what was lost only because other texts happened to reference it. The rest is silence.

Most of us have a personal version of this. The notebook that was lost. The hard drive that failed. The cloud account that was closed. The things we created that we thought were safe — and then they were gone, and we could not remember everything that was in them.

The question is not whether you have backups. It is whether you know what you would be missing if the backups failed.

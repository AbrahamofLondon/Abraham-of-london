The Library of Alexandria cannot tell us what it held because its own catalogue burned with everything else. The gap cannot be measured from inside the gap.

We know Aristotle wrote a second book of the Poetics — the one on comedy — because other writers refer to it. The book does not exist. We know it existed. We cannot read it.

This is the condition of catastrophic archival loss: the thing you most want to know — what was in the inventory — is precisely what the catastrophe took.

Your organisation's knowledge management system has the same vulnerability. The catalogue of what you know depends on someone knowing what to catalogue. What happens when the cataloguer leaves?

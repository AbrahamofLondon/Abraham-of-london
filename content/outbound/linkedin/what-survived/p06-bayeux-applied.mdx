Whose version of your organisation's history is the one that gets preserved?

Every organisation has an official account of how decisions were made, why strategies shifted, what worked and what did not. That account is usually written by the people who were in the room — which means it reflects their perspective, their interests, and their version of events.

The people who were not in the room — the junior team members, the departing executives, the departments that lost the budget battle — have a different version. That version rarely makes it into the official record.

The Bayeux Tapestry survived because the Normans controlled the cathedrals. Your organisation's official history survives because the people who made it control the archives.

Ask what is not in the tapestry. Then ask whether that absence is accidental.

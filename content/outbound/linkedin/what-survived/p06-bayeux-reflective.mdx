Which version of your organisation's history is the one that gets told — and whose version did not survive to be told alongside it?

The Bayeux Tapestry presents the Norman account of 1066. The Saxon account — if it existed in equivalent form — did not survive. We know 1066 primarily through the eyes of the people who won.

Every organisation has the same dynamic. The people who were there write the history. The people who left do not. The version that gets told is the version that serves the people who stayed.

This is not conspiracy. It is the normal operation of institutional memory. The question is whether you know which version you are reading — and whether you have looked for the one that did not survive.

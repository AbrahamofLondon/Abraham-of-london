What are you creating right now that will not exist in ten years — and does that matter to you?

The Video Game History Foundation estimated in 2023 that eighty-seven percent of classic video games are out of print and essentially inaccessible. Not pirated. Not restricted. Gone. The software exists nowhere that can run it. The hardware that would run it no longer exists in functional form.

This is not unusual. It is the default condition of born-digital work.

Most organisations create digital content with no preservation strategy beyond "it is on the server." The server will be decommissioned. The platform will be acquired. The format will become obsolete.

The question is not whether your organisation has a digital preservation policy. It is whether anyone has thought about what would be lost if the policy failed — and whether that loss would matter.

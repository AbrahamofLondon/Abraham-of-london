The inventory of what survives is compiled by the living. It is compiled now, by you, in every choice you make about what to keep and what to let go.

The Chauvet painters did not know their paintings would survive. The Griot did not know whether his transmission would outlast the next generation. Homer's performers did not know that writing would arrive in time to fix what they had kept alive.

You know. You know that the infrastructure is fragile. You know that platforms close. You know that formats become unreadable.

What you do not choose to carry will not be carried. What you do not copy will not survive the format. What you do not archive will dissolve when the server closes. What you do not tell will die with you.

We do not know what we do not know we lost. That is the condition of every era that came before this one. It does not have to be yours.

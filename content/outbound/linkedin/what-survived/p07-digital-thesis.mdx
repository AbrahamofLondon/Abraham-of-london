You have made things that will not survive.

Not because you failed to preserve them. Because the infrastructure you created them on was not yours to preserve. The blog post you wrote in 2008 on a platform that closed in 2014. The photographs you uploaded to a service that was acquired and discontinued. The community you belonged to that existed only on a platform that is now gone.

The average lifespan of a URL is two years. The Internet Archive has captured an estimated twenty to twenty-five percent of publicly accessible web content since 1996. The rest is gone.

The difference between you and every other generation that lost things is that you have been told. The Griot did not know the transmission would end. The Library's curators did not know the fire was coming.

You know the server will close. The question is what you do with that.

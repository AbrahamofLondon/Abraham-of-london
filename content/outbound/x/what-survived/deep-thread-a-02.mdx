The formulaic structure of Homeric verse — "rosy-fingered Dawn," "swift-footed Achilles," "the wine-dark sea" — is not decoration. It is mnemonic engineering.

The performer who forgot a line had a system of conventional phrases that maintained the rhythm while the right line came. The poem was reconstructed, not recalled.

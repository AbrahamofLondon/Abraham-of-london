The Soviet state sent inserts to subscribers of the Great Soviet Encyclopaedia. The inserts contained instructions: cut out pages X through Y. Paste in these replacements.

The removal of Yezhov from the official record was performed by thousands of hands, in thousands of homes, across the country. The archive was edited everywhere, simultaneously, by the people who owned it.

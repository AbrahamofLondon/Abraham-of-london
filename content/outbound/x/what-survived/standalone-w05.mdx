We know Aristotle wrote a second book of the Poetics — the one on comedy — because other writers refer to it. The book does not exist. We know it existed. We cannot read it. We cannot know what it contained.

This is the condition of catastrophic archival loss: the thing you most want to know is precisely what the catastrophe took.

# Execution Integrity Protocol

## Restore Execution Discipline Without Rewriting Strategy

---

## Core Insight

Most failures are execution failures, not strategy failures.

A brilliant strategy executed poorly will lose to a mediocre strategy delivered with discipline and clarity.

The **Execution Integrity Protocol** is a diagnostic and correction framework for teams that keep "missing the finish line" despite good intentions.

---

## Framework: Execution Integrity

The framework is built around four interlocking phases that move the team from **symptom observation** to **systemic correction**:

### Phase 1 — Integrity Audit

Establish a baseline for whether work is being done with *integrity*—aligned intents, clear responsibilities, and documented decisions.

- Identify where instructions are ambiguous, expectations are unstated, or handovers are opaque.
- Surface the "hidden overrides" and informal power structures that disrupt the agreed operating model.

### Phase 2 — Constraint Identification

Pinpoint the structural and behavioural bottlenecks that slow or distort execution.

- Separate "hard constraints" (capacity, skills, systems, gates) from "soft constraints" (communication, trust, culture).
- Map where friction arises repeatedly, not just as one‑off exceptions.

### Phase 3 — Cadence Restoration

Re‑establish a credible, predictable rhythm of work, communication, and decision‑making.

- Align meeting cadences with decision‑making needs, not convenience.
- Define clear thresholds for escalation and de‑escalation, so issues do not "sit in limbo."

### Phase 4 — Reinforcement Loop

Embed feedback and accountability so that execution integrity becomes self‑sustaining, not just a one‑off intervention.

- Introduce a simple but disciplined tracking mechanism for progress, quality, and clarity.
- Normalize the practice of "checking back" on decisions and agreements, so the system learns from its own behaviour.

---

## Protocol

### Layer 1 — Clarity

Are instructions, goals, and expectations unambiguous enough to be executed without guesswork?

#### Key questions

- Are outcomes defined in *behavioural* terms (what will be done, when, by whom) rather than vague "themes" or aspirations?
- Are deliverables documented with:
  - Success criteria.
  - Acceptance standards.
  - Completion thresholds.
- Are roles and handover points explicitly mapped, or do people operate under implied assumptions?

#### Practical levers

- Run a **"One‑Sentence Test"** on key projects:
  - "Can the owner describe the project's outcome in one clear sentence that everyone understands?"
- Require **written one‑page summaries** for all major initiatives, with section headings:
  - Goal.
  - Key outputs.
  - Criteria for success.
  - Clear owner.
- Introduce "clarity checkpoints" into project gates (kick‑off, checkpoint, close‑out).

#### Output

- **Clarity score** (0–10) per key initiative.
- List of "fuzzy mandates" needing clarification.

---

### Layer 2 — Alignment

Do teams interpret intent the same way when they leave the room?

#### Key questions

- Do different stakeholders describe the same project in materially different terms?
- Are there "received truths" that no one has written down, but everyone assumes are shared?
- Are there silent misalignments that surface only when deadlines are missed or quality drops?

#### Practical levers

- Use **"repeat‑back" validation** in meetings:
  - "Tell me what you heard; let's check for alignment."
- Conduct alignment interviews with key stakeholders to surface discrepancies in:
  - Priority.
  - Scope.
  - Quality expectations.
- Document and publish an **alignment charter** for each major initiative that includes:
  - What is in.
  - What is out.
  - What is deferred.
  - How trade‑offs will be resolved.

#### Output

- **Alignment map** showing where interpretations diverge.
- Revised alignment charters for core projects.

---

### Layer 3 — Friction

Where is execution slowing down, breaking down, or getting distorted?

#### Key questions

- Which stages of the workflow repeat delays, rework, or clarification cycles?
- Are there "hotspots" where decisions keep coming back for re‑negotiation?
- Are people spending more time coordinating than executing?

#### Practical levers

- Build a **workflow friction map**:
  - Trace a typical project from request to completion and identify:
    - Approval gates.
    - Interfaces between functions.
    - Information handovers.
- Introduce a **"friction log"** for each major project:
  - Capture:
    - When friction arose.
    - Who it involved.
    - What was missing (data, authority, clarity).
- Interview "front‑line" executors about the steps they wish did not exist.

#### Output

- **Friction index** (0–10) per project and by workflow stage.
- **Constraint map** showing where bottlenecks are concentrated.

---

## Outputs

At the end of an Execution Integrity Protocol session, the team leaves with a coherent set of outputs:

### Integrity score (0–100)

A composite metric summarising clarity, alignment, and friction across projects. Typically exposing where the team is "good‑looking on paper, but fragile in practice."

### Constraint map

Visual of key bottlenecks and friction points, across people, process, and information flows.

### Execution cadence schedule

Explicit rhythm for:
- Daily stand‑ups.
- Weekly check‑ins.
- Monthly decision‑making sessions.

Matched to decision‑making and review needs, not just calendar defaults.

### Accountability matrix

RACI or similar structure for each major initiative:
- Who is Responsible.
- Who is Accountable (decision‑maker).
- Who is Consulted.
- Who is Informed.

### Improvement backlog

Concrete, time‑boxed actions to:
- Improve clarity.
- Fix alignment gaps.
- Reduce friction points.

With owners and due dates explicitly assigned.

These outputs are not for "reporting" only; they are designed to become the team's **baseline operating documentation** going forward.

---

## Strategic Principle

A weak system cannot carry a strong strategy.

Execution integrity is a **systemic property**, not a personality trait. No amount of "trying harder" will compensate for unclear mandates, misaligned expectations, and structural bottlenecks.

By systematically restoring clarity, alignment, and cadence, the Execution Integrity Protocol transforms the operating system so that even simple strategies can be executed with discipline, predictability, and resilience.

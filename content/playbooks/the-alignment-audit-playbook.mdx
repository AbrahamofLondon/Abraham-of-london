# The Alignment Audit Playbook

## A Structured Framework for Diagnosing Organisational Misalignment Before Intervention

---

## What This Playbook Does

This playbook provides a **repeatable, evidence‑based method** for diagnosing where an organisation's stated intent has diverged from its day‑to‑day reality. It is designed for use before major decisions—such as hiring consultants, scaling operations, or raising capital—so that interventions are grounded in accurate reading, not assumption.

Specifically, the Alignment Audit surfaces:

- **Misalignment between strategy and execution:** Where the "story we tell" does not match the "behaviour we see."
- **Leadership signal distortion:** Places where leaders' intentions are misinterpreted, diluted, or contradicted in the operating system.
- **Operational clarity gaps:** Functions, roles, and decisions that lack clear definition, leading to friction and inefficiency.

By producing a clear **alignment diagnosis**, this playbook turns ambiguity and "everyone has a different view" into a structured, shared understanding of the problem.

---

## Core Framework

The Alignment Audit is built around a three‑part core framework, which unfolds inside the four phases listed above.

### Step 1 — Intent Mapping

Define what leadership believes is true.

#### Objective

Capture the **official strategic narrative** and leadership assumptions, rather than guessing them.

#### Key actions

- Review and extract key themes from:
  - Mission, vision, and values statements.
  - Strategy decks, leadership off‑sites, and board minutes.
  - Public communications and internal memos.
- Translate these into a concise **Intent Map** that includes:
  - What the organisation is for.
  - What it is explicitly *not* for.
  - What success looks like on a 3–5‑year horizon.

#### Check for quality

- Can three different senior leaders reproduce the same core intent in one sentence each?
- Are there meaningful contradictions between documents or between documents and lived behaviour?

---

### Step 2 — Execution Reality

Measure what is actually happening on the ground.

#### Objective

Ground the audit in observable behaviour, not opinion.

#### Key actions

- Map key workflows and decision‑making lines:
  - Where does work get initiated?
  - Where does it get stuck or reworked?
  - Where do decisions get re‑opened after "consensus" is reached?
- Run targeted interviews and surveys across levels:
  - Line managers.
  - Individual contributors.
  - Support functions.
- Look for recurring patterns in:
  - Delay reasons.
  - Double‑checking or "double‑approval."
  - Manual workarounds or informal rules.

#### Key question

If the organisation's "actual" operating manual were written by the frontline, what would it say?

---

### Step 3 — Dissonance Gap

Quantify the difference between intent and reality.

#### Objective

Turn qualitative misalignment into a structured, comparable diagnostic.

#### Key actions

- Align the **Intent Map** with the **Execution Reality Map** point‑by‑point, using the same domains:
  - Strategy focus.
  - Role clarity.
  - Decision‑making.
  - Performance metrics.
  - Cultural signals.
- For each domain, score:
  - Agreement on direction.
  - Consistency in interpretation.
  - Coherence between talk and behaviour.
- Produce a **Dissonance Score** per domain, and an overall **Alignment Score**.

#### Output artifacts

- A **dissonance gap table** showing where the largest deltas live.
- A visual **gap heatmap** highlighting domains with both high gap and high impact.

## Phases of the Alignment Audit

### Phase 1 — Signal Capture

Collect and structure the raw signals of misalignment.

#### What this phase does

- Captures symptoms such as:
  - Repeated strategy "re‑frames."
  - Operational inconsistency across units.
  - Widely divergent views of "what we're trying to do."
- Structures these into a coherent input set for the later stages.

#### How to run it

- Ask leadership and middle managers to describe:
  - What they believe the organisation's top 2–3 priorities are.
  - How they expect decisions to be made.
  - What behaviours they encourage versus discourage.
- Capture all responses in a standardised format (e.g., short‑answer templates).

#### Output

A **signal inventory** of misalignment indicators, ready for pattern analysis.

---

### Phase 2 — Pattern Recognition

Turn individual signals into systemic patterns.

#### What this phase does

- Identifies recurring themes such as:
  - "Our strategy sounds right, but our incentives contradict it."
  - "We say 'move fast,' but we penalise mistakes."
- Separates isolated incidents from structural problems.

#### How to run it

- Cluster similar responses and behaviours into categories:
  - Strategy storytelling vs. reward design.
  - Stated culture vs. observed behaviour.
  - Formal decision‑making vs. actual power flows.
- Map patterns back to the organisation's structure and systems.

#### Output

- **Pattern map** of key misalignment types.
- Input into the Gap Analysis step.

---

### Phase 3 — Gap Analysis

Diagnose why the system is drifting out of alignment.

#### What this phase does

- Identifies root causes such as:
  - Reward structures that contradict strategy.
  - Measurement systems that ignore the right outcomes.
  - Roles and responsibilities that are unclear or conflicting.
- Assesses which gaps are most destabilising and which are most correctable.

#### How to run it

- Review each major gap domain and answer:
  - What is the *intended* state?
  - What is the *observed* state?
  - What system element (metric, incentive, role, process, communication) causes the gap?
- Score each gap on:
  - Impact on performance.
  - Magnitude of misalignment.
  - Feasibility of correction.

#### Output

- **Gap heatmap** (visual matrix of impact vs. ease of correction).
- **Correction priority list** for the next 6–18 months.

---

### Phase 4 — Correction Mapping

Plan how to reduce misalignment without over‑engineering.

#### What this phase does

- Translates diagnosis into concrete, time‑bound actions.
- Identifies the right *levers* (process, role, metric, incentive, narrative) for each gap.
- Defines clear **intervention trigger points**—e.g., "If alignment score drops below X, we re‑run this audit."

#### How to run it

- For each high‑priority gap, define:
  - Required change (what).
  - Owner (who).
  - Target timeline (when).
  - Success indicator (how we know it's fixed).
- Think in terms of **minimal viable correction**, not total system overhaul.

#### Output

- **Correction priorities** (numbered list of high‑impact, high‑feasibility items).
- **Intervention trigger points** tied to measurable alignment metrics.

## Outputs

At the end of an Alignment Audit session, the leadership team receives a compact but powerful set of outputs:

### Alignment score (0–100, or 0–1.0)

A composite metric summarising the degree of alignment across strategy, structure, and behaviour. Designed to be re‑run periodically as a "vital sign" for the organisation.

### Alignment scorecard

A breakdown by domain (e.g., strategy, roles, decisions, communication, culture). Helps leaders see where the system is strongest and weakest.

### Gap heatmap

A visual grid showing:
- Domain of misalignment.
- Impact on value creation.
- Ease of correction.

Supports prioritisation and resource allocation.

### Domain breakdown

A short narrative per domain explaining:
- The stated intent.
- The observed reality.
- The core misalignment.

### Correction priorities

A short‑list of 3–7 high‑impact actions to narrow the gap. Each with owner, timeline, and success criteria.

### Intervention trigger points

Thresholds and rules for when to re‑run the audit or launch targeted interventions. E.g., "If alignment score falls below 65, trigger a focused alignment‑check workshop."

## Use Case

This playbook is particularly valuable **before** major organisational events or decisions, such as:

### Hiring consultants

Ensures that the external mandate is grounded in a shared, accurate diagnosis, not in competing narratives.

### Scaling operations

Identifies hidden misalignments that would otherwise create friction, delay, and rework at scale.

### Raising capital

Aligns internal leadership on story, KPIs, and risk narrative, so the external narrative is coherent and credible.

It can also be run:

- After a major strategy change.
- Following a re‑organisation or merger.
- Periodically (e.g., annually or biannually) as an "organisational health check."

---

## Strategic Principle

You do not fix what you have not properly read.

The Alignment Audit is not a substitute for action; it is a **diagnostic discipline that makes action meaningful**.

By deliberately reading the organisation's alignment state—intent, reality, and dissonance—leaders avoid pouring effort into the wrong levers and instead correct the underlying misalignment that undermines execution, culture, and growth.

You've been taught that doubt is dangerous.

That questioning means weak faith.
That uncertainty is the first step toward walking away.

But what if the opposite is true?

What if the people who never question
are the ones whose faith is most fragile?
And the ones who wrestle —
the ones who bring their honest confusion to God —
are the ones who actually stay?

---

Honest doubt is often closer to faith
than dishonest certainty.

Pretending you're sure when you're not
is not faith. It's performance.

And God is not honoured by theatre.

This week, stop performing certainty.

Write one real question you have.
Not a rhetorical one. A real one.
And bring it to God plainly.

You don't need to have it figured out.
You just need to be honest about where you are.

It used to be effort.

You had to remind yourself to be grateful.
You had to force it.
It felt like a discipline.

But something has shifted.

Gratitude is no longer something you practise.
It's something you breathe.

Not because life is easier.
Because you've learned to see differently.

---

Gratitude as a default is not denial of difficulty.
It's the recognition that even in difficulty,
there is still goodness.

And the more you look for it,
the more you find it.

This week, begin and end each day with one specific thanks.

Not generic. Specific.

The warmth of your home.
The person who checked on you.
The fact that you made it through another day.

Gratitude is not a technique.
It's an orientation.
And once it becomes your atmosphere,
everything looks different.

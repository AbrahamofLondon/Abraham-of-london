You want access.

To rooms. To people. To opportunities.
To platforms that could amplify your voice.

And there's nothing wrong with that.

But here's the question you need to answer:
What are you willing to compromise to get it?

---

Every time you soften the truth to stay in the room,
you trade integrity for access.

Every time you laugh at something you don't agree with,
you trade conviction for belonging.

Every time you stay silent when you should speak,
you trade your soul for a seat at the table.

This week, identify one place where access is making you smaller.
One room where you're paying for entry with your authenticity.

The right doors don't require you to shrink.
And the wrong ones aren't worth the price of admission.

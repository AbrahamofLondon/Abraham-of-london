You used to chase.

When someone pulled away, you pursued.
When the distance grew, you closed it.
When they stopped trying, you tried harder.

Because their leaving felt like proof
that you weren't enough.

---

But something has changed.

You're learning that someone else's withdrawal
is not always about you.
Sometimes it's about their capacity.
Sometimes it's about their issues.
Sometimes it's just the natural distance
between two people who were never meant to stay close.

This week, do not chase one disappearing person.

Let them have their space.
Let the silence sit.
Let them come back if they choose to.

You are not desperate for love.
You are worthy of consistent presence.
And chasing what runs only exhausts the chaser.

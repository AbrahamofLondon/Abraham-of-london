You've been in rooms where you were present but not seen.

Where you performed belonging
but never felt it.

Where people knew your name
but not your story.

But this is different.

The people around you now
actually see you.

They notice when you're not yourself.
They ask questions that reach beneath the surface.
They stay when it's uncomfortable.

---

Being seen is vulnerable.
But it's also healing.

And the instinct to deflect —
to make a joke, to change the subject, to hide —
is the old reflex.

You don't need it anymore.

This week, receive being seen without deflecting.

Let them notice.
Let them care.
Let yourself be known.

You are safe here.
And the people who see you
are the people who will help you become.

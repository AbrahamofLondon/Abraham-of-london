## 1. Physical Presence

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

- Iyalode was not tall, but somehow looked larger than most.
- She was fair-skinned, wrinkled in a graceful way, with delicate hands and skin that seemed to glow.
- She loved to moisturise and take care of herself.
- She wore clean, pressed clothes — no wrinkles, no stains — usually iro and buba in ankara, with gele.
- Her voice was soft but firm, slightly coarse, commanding and graceful.

---

## 2. Her Public Presence

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

- She was mostly sitting while people came to greet her.
- She was rarely, almost never, alone.
- People flocked to her house like the town's Jerusalem.
- Even when she was cordial or joking, the respect around her remained.
- Sometimes it felt like a court: her court, with her courtiers.

---

## 3. Her House

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

- She lived in Isua in the same house David Senior had built for his father's burial in the early 1980s, even after other houses were built.
- The house had a large living room for crowds and, if memory serves, about six bedrooms.

**Constraint:** Use "if memory serves" or avoid exact bedroom count if uncertain.

---

## 4. Daily Habits / Food

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

- She liked fresh food.
- She enjoyed cooking by herself.
- She mostly ate foods native to Isua.
- She liked rice and "fry" — well-fried stew. "Fry" was one of the few English words she used.
- She also liked Isua native soups and pounded yam.
- She would shower early, dress carefully, sit to receive guests all day, then retire to bed.

---

## 5. Authority / Judgement

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

- She settled arguments by pronouncing judgment.
- She was considered fair, trustworthy, and wise.
- This was part of why people kept coming to her.

**Constraint:** Do not overstate. Render through ordinary behaviour: people waiting, greeting, listening, accepting her pronouncement.

---

## 6. Stories of Youth and Abraham Adaramola

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT

- In very old age, she loved telling stories of her young days and of her handsome Abraham.
- It sometimes seemed as if she forgot she had remarried after him.

**Constraint:** Use with care. Do not turn this into gossip or melodrama. It shows memory, devotion, and the persistence of first love.

## 7. Abraham's Childhood Perception

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT

- The clearest memories were when Abraham was about eleven, just before Jumoke died.
- Iyalode had come to visit.
- It was one of Abraham's best times with her.
- It felt to Abraham, even as a child, as if her encounter with Jesus had opened her heart to finally accept him.

**Constraint:** Use carefully. This belongs in Ch.8 only if it strengthens the spiritual-covering arc.

## 8. Prayer Language

**Classification:** SPIRITUAL-SENSITIVE / USE WITH RESTRAINT

**Constraint:** Do not over-explain. Do not translate unless exact words are supplied.

## 9. Last Sight / Death

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT

- By the time Abraham last saw her in 2006, she had retired permanently to bed and could no longer recognise him.
- She had wanted to die for a while.
- Everyone she had known and grown up with had died decades earlier, and even some grandchildren were already dying.
- Her death was a loss, but most of the family were not unprepared. David Senior's younger brother was not prepared.

**Constraint:** Use with restraint. Do not turn this into a grand symbolic death. Let age and exhaustion carry it.

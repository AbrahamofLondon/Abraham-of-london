**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

## Supplemental Longevity / Family Whisper Source

**Classification:** SPIRITUAL-SENSITIVE / USE WITH RESTRAINT and ORAL FAMILY BELIEF — DO NOT ASSERT AS FACT

**Date captured:** 2026-06-23 (supplemental session)

### Background

Iyalode's parents were childless for a very long time because children kept dying shortly after birth. Her parents consulted spiritually, and rituals were performed. Family belief held that these rituals enabled Iyalode to live and not die young.

### Connection to her difficulty dying

The same ritual was later believed by some in the family to be connected to her difficulty dying in old age. There were family whispers that a nail had to be spiritually detected in the forest and removed before she could die.

### Multiple false death reports

On more than one occasion, her children were reportedly called and told that she had died, only to arrive in Isua and find that she was still alive. It was as if she was never going to die.

### Author's position

None of this has scientific explanation and it should not be explained. But the fact that she lived so long was something, not nothing.

### Prose-use rules

Do not write this as:
- Proof
- Miracle
- Magic
- Folklore spectacle
- Yoruba-culture explainer
- Horror
- Saint-making
- Supernatural certainty

**Default prose handling, if later used:**
- "Family memory said..."
- "It was whispered..."
- "Some believed..."
- "No one needed to explain it for it to matter."
- "The story belonged to the family's way of carrying what it could not settle."

### Recommended future use

This material may support a second-pass Ch.8 paragraph near the old-age/death section, not the opening. The prose should probably focus on:
- Children being called and told she had died
- Arriving to find her still alive
- The family's sense that she was almost unable to die
- The impossibility of explaining it

**Nail/forest detail:** Use only if the chapter can hold it without spectacle. It may be better retained in source than used in prose.

## 3. Nigerian Food / Mother's Concern

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

There were concerns about Nigerian foods Abraham's mother thought he would miss. Abraham thought he could do without them. There was also uncertainty about whether the food would be allowed through the airport.

He carried the food. The family agreed that if airport officials objected, his brother would return with the items. The food was allowed.

## 5. David Senior's Send-Off / Psalm 27:10

**Classification:** PUBLIC-SAFE

David Senior did not have cash to give Abraham. Instead, he gave his blessing.

David Senior's younger brother gave Abraham $250.

David Senior stood and laid his hands on Abraham while Abraham knelt. The family stood around them.

David Senior quoted Psalm 27:10 and then used it as a prayer.

**Paraphrase only (not a direct quote):**
> Unless he had ever looked away from another person in need, Abraham would never be stranded abroad; his needs would be met.

**Voice direction:** David Senior's voice should be rendered as the voice of a father sending his child into unknown territory while trusting God to provide and protect him.

**Retrospective understanding:** Abraham thought he understood the blessing at the time. He only progressively understood it in retrospect over the following months as provisions came.

**Constraint:** Use paraphrase, not a fabricated direct quote, unless exact wording is later supplied.

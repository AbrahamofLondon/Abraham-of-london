## Chapter Thesis

This is not a chapter about sex, lawyers, churches, blame, scandal, or revenge.

It is about:

> A disciplined man using undisciplined tools because the disciplined world stopped holding him.

Or, more fully:

> A man who had spent his life trying to be orderly found himself surviving on bananas, peanuts, nightclubs, dating apps, legal mistakes, religious silence, and the strange kindness of strangers — and began to see that even his search for love was being run by old wounds he had not yet named.

## 1. Precarity / Hunger / Rent Arrears

**Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT**

- Period after divorce and child-care proceedings began.
- Covid period.
- No job. No income.
- Approximately 13 months of rent arrears.
- Abraham survived at one point on:
  - £1 bananas from Hounslow Central farmers market.
  - Peanuts from Home Bargains.
- This was sometimes his only weekly food supply.
- He did not want to rely on public funds because immigration lawyers had warned him it might later count against him in future applications.
- This contributed to the collapse of normal life, dignity, and dating confidence.

## 5. Churches / Failed Expected Refuge

**Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT / INSTITUTION-SENSITIVE**

- Abraham approached churches during this period.
- Churches heard his precarious story but did not meaningfully help.
- One large Central London church used a large cinema space.
- Abraham had only around two weeks to regularise immigration status or leave the country.
- A kind usher introduced Abraham to a pastor.
- The pastor took his number but never called.
- This contributed to Abraham using nightclubs as his social space.

### Rule

Do not name the church.
Do not describe it in a way that makes it readily identifiable.
Do not accuse.

### Possible Safe Public Phrasing

> He went to places where help was supposed to live. Some listened. Some promised to call. Life continued anyway.

## 6. Femi and the Deepfake Nightclub Video

**Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE**

- Abraham's brother Femi saw a video of Abraham dancing in a nightclub.
- Femi called and said he thought it was a deepfake.
- He was almost convinced it was real.
- Abraham told him it was real.
- Femi's reaction showed the contrast between Abraham's old baseline identity and the man he had become during this period.
- Abraham was going to nightclubs almost every weekend.
- Clubs became his social space.

### Preserve as Likely Opening Image

Possible opening line:

> Femi called because he thought he had seen a deepfake of his brother dancing in a nightclub.

## 7. Dating / Casual Encounters / Longing

**Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH PRIVACY RESTRAINT**

- Abraham wanted to start dating again after divorce/child-care proceedings.
- For a long time, he could not get himself to.
- Eventually he entered a period of casual sexual encounters and short dating relationships.
- There was a long list of women from different parts of the world.
- Some names are forgotten, though nationalities are remembered.
- Some encounters were brief.
- Some lasted weeks or months.
- This came before the later Elena relationship.
- Abraham now sees that he was sometimes choosing from a dark place.
- Early-life wounds were operating beneath desire, though he did not yet understand them.
- Writing the book has opened Abraham to himself in a way he had not experienced before.

### Rule

No graphic sexual detail.
No catalogue of women.
No unnecessary nationality list.
No identifiable private women.
Use selective representative scenes only if later drafting is approved.

### Possible Safe Public Framing

> He thought he was choosing freely. Later he would wonder how much of the choosing had been done by old wounds wearing the clothes of appetite.

## 8. Relationship Patterns / Offers / Refusal / Dependence

**Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH HIGH PRIVACY / GENERALISATION RESTRAINT**

- Abraham still longed to connect with someone and build a family/business partnership.
- There was a quiet feeling that something was not right.
- He felt the dating pool was poor for marriage, while acknowledging good women exist and he has not yet found or convinced one.
- Some women may have suspected he was dating for visa, housing, or rescue because of his circumstances.
- Elena said he had huge potential, but "potential is not what she does."
- Abraham had heard women make similar points generally, but in his situation it sounded like a subtle message.
- He met women who would not date a vaccinated man because of the Covid jab.
- Some liked him until realising he was Nigerian.
- The "Nigerian men" and "Yoruba demons" stereotypes are part of the social field but must be handled carefully.
- One woman said she could not have a serious relationship with a man who already had a child because her man's first child must come from her.
- A few women asked him to move in with them after only a few weeks.
- One offered to pay off his rent arrears within two weeks.
- Abraham now recognises that dependence can sometimes be created for reasons that are not altruistic.

### Rule

Do not use "dating pool is low quality" in public prose.
Do not frame women collectively.
Do not make national/racial/cultural claims as authorial verdict.
Do not make private women identifiable.

### Possible Safe Public Phrasing

> Some offers looked like rescue. Some refusals looked like judgment. Some desires looked like kindness until he asked what they would cost.

# Source facts (verbatim from author, nothing added)

- A KPMG partner found Abraham weeks ahead of the semi-final interview and handled the initial calls and negotiations directly with him.
- The role: Commercial Manager.
- The opportunity was real — not a formality interview, a live consideration.
- After the semi-final interview, before what should have been the last stage, the partner called again.
- The partner's continued involvement showed Abraham that the partner saw him as worthy of the position and able to do the job, while still allowing the hiring managers to run their own part of the process.
- In the semi-final interview itself, Abraham did not land all of the answers in the form the room needed.
- The partner asked him directly: did he think he was able to do the job, despite that gap.
- Abraham's answer, verbatim: **"I'd kill it."**
- Correction to central thesis: the failure was not silence or omission. After "I'd kill it," the partner paused, and Abraham then talked at length, giving an account of the work he had done and was currently doing, with real evidence of capacity and capability. The failure was shapelessness — real material delivered without architecture.
- Author's own retrospective assessment, verbatim: **"That was where I lost the job, gbamsolutely."**
- This was painful, and the author states this plainly — "deeply so."
- Physical/emotional context of the second call: Abraham was in bed when the call came, exhausted from weeks of preparing simultaneously for the interview and a family court hearing. The interview had been rescheduled twice; the hearing had been rescheduled once; the dates landed close together as a result.
- He had high hopes for the interview.
- He had bought Bang & Olufsen headphones in preparation, intending to use them for a podcast side hustle he was planning at the time.
- The author states: "the failure changed everything."
- Following the loss, the author made a deliberate decision: to find something that could serve as consolation for it.
- He began writing the same day.
- The first draft "landed" that day.
- That decision became the start of writing a book: **The Architecture of Human Purpose.**
- This book is real and published — confirmed live at https://www.abrahamoflondon.org/books/the-architecture-of-human-purpose, positioned as the Prelude MiniBook / gateway to a multi-volume Canon under the Abraham of London platform.

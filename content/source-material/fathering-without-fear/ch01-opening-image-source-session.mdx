## Opening Image

February 2021.

Abraham was in his room in Hounslow, sitting on his bed.

A telephone call came in from the court. It was a voice call, not video. The call concerned a hearing he had not been informed of.

He was not prepared.

He almost protested. He told the clerk he had never been notified. The clerk did not force him to proceed.

But the silence on the other side was loud and clear.

He was told the judge was about to be brought onto the call.

This is the Ch.1 opening image.

## Abraham's Physical Action

Abraham was sitting on his bed when the call came in.

He answered a voice call from the court.

He listened as he learned that the call concerned a hearing he had not been informed of.

He told the clerk he had never been notified.

He almost protested, but the clerk's silence carried pressure. The clerk was female. He was told the judge was about to be brought onto the call.

## What the Reader Must Feel First

The reader must feel confusion before explanation.

The reader must feel the pressure of being pulled into a proceeding without preparation.

The reader must feel the imbalance between a man sitting on a bed and an institution about to bring a judge onto the line.

The reader must feel fatherhood under institutional power without being told what the book is about.

The reader must feel the demand not to disappear before the phrase is explained or named.

## What Ch.1 Must Withhold

- Case chronology.
- Detailed allegations.
- Sealed material.
- Trustpilot or public-review commentary.
- Broad institutional critique.
- "The system is broken" argument.
- Claims about what judges generally do.
- Claims about lawmakers, criminals, or the justice system as a whole.
- Title explanation.
- Memoir thesis.
- Ancestry.
- Damisi as evidence.
- Abraham as hero.
- Abraham as victim.
- A timeline opening.

## What Ch.1 Must Plant

- A present-tense or near-present pressure point.
- Abraham as a father under pressure, not a child and not a triumphant narrator.
- A room: Hounslow bedroom.
- A body: Abraham sitting on his bed.
- An object/action: phone call, voice call, court calling.
- The first institutional imbalance.
- The first unanswered demand.
- The sense that disappearance would be easier than staying present.
- The question of what a father owes when process becomes power.
- A distinct register from Ch.4's Teams hearing and Ch.17's contact-room architecture.

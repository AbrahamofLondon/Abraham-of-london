# Chapter 6 Fire Author Recall Session

## Purpose

Gather confirmed source material to expand Ch.6 — The Fire and the Deal from its current body-pass draft (~868 words) to 3,000–4,500 words (minimum 2,500). This is a recall pass only. No prose was revised in producing this document.

Ch.6 is a STRUCTURAL ANCHOR chapter (architecture gate classification). It carries the childhood covenant — the moment Abraham's later endurance becomes traceable to a promise made before he fully understood what a promise would cost. The chapter must hold four movements: (1) the ancient entrance, (2) the fall through darkness, (3) the quiet prayer and answer, (4) waking into fire. All four movements must be rendered as scene, not explained.

Session status: **COMPLETE — Groups A through G answered. 43 fragments documented (F1–F43); 40 contain confirmed source detail; F5, F11, and F38 flag absence of detail or incomplete responses.**

## Canon / Restraint Locks

These constraints hold regardless of what expansion adds.

**Canon facts (from Prose Skeleton continuity lock):**
- Abraham is one of triplets. The surviving triplet sister is **Yemisi**. One triplet did not remain to grow with them. This must never be stated directly — oblique framing only.
- **Jumoke** is a *younger sister* — NOT part of the triplet set, NOT a twin. Died at age seven. She is present in this chapter as a sleeping child in the shared room, not yet as the figure of Ch.5. Do not anticipate Ch.5 material here.
- **David** is Abraham's brother. Do not introduce, name, or imply David in this chapter unless he is the "elder brother" who arrived at the fire (see NEEDS AUTHOR CONFIRMATION, below).
- The father (David Senior) dies in Ch.21 only. Do not anticipate.
- Chapter 5 (Jumoke) is a separate chapter. Ch.6 may hold Jumoke as a physical presence — asleep in her bed — but must not carry her death, appearance, or the Ch.5 covenant.

**Chapter craft locks (from the brief and revision bible):**
- Do not explain the experience. Render it.
- Do not turn hell into a sermon.
- Do not turn the fire into metaphor.
- Do not invent angels, demons, flames, gold, trumpets, or spectacle.
- Do not add theological interpretation that the source does not supply.
- The strongest register is: ordinary detail beside unbearable fact.
- "He has never tried to renegotiate" — do not over-polish this line.
- The Hezekiah parallel is available to the prose as a felt echo, not as an announced comparison.

## Confirmed Source Fragments

All 40 fragments were supplied by the author in the recall session. Group labels match the session structure (A–G).

### Group A — The Room Before Sleep

**F1** — Room layout: The children's room was the next room from the parents' bedroom in a multi-room house. Entering the room: a bed to the left, another ahead, and Abraham's bed to the right, just behind the door. The table with the mosquito coil was just behind the door — between the entrance and Abraham's bed. After the fire, the coil's metal stand was found either on the floor or in the bed.

**F2** — Sisters' positions: Jumoke slept in the bed to the left. Yemisi slept in the bed ahead (directly opposite the door).

**F3** — The beds: Single beds, all three. Kids' room. Yemisi's bed had black metal poles decorated with gold ornaments.

**F4** — Mosquito coil use: The coil was in the room most nights.

**F5** — Pre-sleep sensory detail: The author cannot remember smell, heat, mosquitoes, darkness, generator sound, fan, curtain, or any other room objects from before the experience. No pre-sleep sensory detail available. Expansion must work from layout and inference, not from recovered sensation.

---

### Group B — The Ancient Entrance

**F6** — Entrance appearance: Not exactly like movie scenes but close. Big pillars. Massive old gate, looking aged. Human-looking creatures that he later understood to be angels.

**F7** — Entrance scale: More than ordinary size, but not too large.

**F8** — Entrance as abandoned city: Felt like the entrance to an old, possibly abandoned city.

**F9** — Figures at the gate: A few men, not more than 6 or 7 scattered around. A pair at each side of the gate. He later understood them to be angels.

**F10** — First reaction at the entrance: Shock. A sense of "where am I" or "heaven's gate." Then he was transported to another location — not by his own will.

**F11** — Light at the entrance: Author began to answer ("the") but the response was incomplete. Light quality at the entrance is unconfirmed. Do not supply.

---

### Group C — The Fall

**F12** — Fall quality: Smooth, endless, fast but not too fast.

**F13** — Falling alone: Yes.

**F14** — What was in the darkness: Noises, cries, screams. Darkness, blackout. No fire. Like a dark torture chamber, but endlessly bottomless.

**F15** — Source of the screams: Surrounding from beneath — whatever that was. Not from one direction.

**F16** — Physical sensation during the fall: None. No heat, cold, pressure, or wind. Just the horror of falling.

**F17** — How he knew it was hell: Immediate bodily knowing. Not thought, not announcement, not instruction. The body knew.

---

### Group D — The Prayer and Answer

**F18** — Prayer inspiration: The story of Hezekiah. Abraham had heard it in church a few weeks before, though they did not go regularly. (Canonical reference: 2 Kings 20 / Isaiah 38 — Hezekiah ill and near death, turned to the wall, prayed, was heard, given fifteen more years. The parallel is felt, not announced.)

**F19** — Prayer mode: All in his mind. Everything was like telepathy. Nothing spoken.

**F20** — Answer mode: Thoughts. The answer came as thoughts.

**F21** — Nature of the exchange: It felt like God heard him and agreed as he woke up. Like a deal was closed. Not one-sided. A bargaining, a surrender, a rescue, and an agreement simultaneously.

**F22** — The deal's terms (private reasoning — see classification below): The author wonders about the deal as it means he may only have a fixed number of years. The number is a private calculation. The publicly safe framing — confirmed in the Prose Skeleton — is: a promise of seventy-five years of life in service.

**F23** — Waking sequence: Instant waking. The smell of smoke came first. He then realized his mattress was on fire. He had no burns anywhere. The fire was just on the top right edge of the mattress.

---

### Group E — Waking into Fire

**F24** — Room state at waking: Still faintly lit by the fire. Not fully bright. Not fully dark.

**F25** — First action: Rushed to wake his sisters, then went to the bathroom for water.

**F26** — Mattress burn location: Top right corner.

**F27** — What burned: The bedsheet also burned.

**F28** — How sisters responded: They ran out to call the parents.

**F29** — How Abraham fought the fire: With water from the bathroom and his blanket. The blanket was grey with two red (or orange) lines at the top.

**F30** — No burns: He had no burns anywhere. When his parents arrived they were certain he was hiding a burn. He kept assuring them he was fine.

**F31** — The coil stand: After the fire was out, someone identified the mosquito coil as the cause. The metal stand was found — either on the floor or in the bed.

**F32** — Smell after: He can still remember the burnt-mattress smell now — cannot describe it in words but holds it as sense-memory.

---

### Group F — Parents and Aftermath

**F33** — Arrival order: Father arrived first. Then elder brother. Then mother. (See NEEDS AUTHOR CONFIRMATION — identity of elder brother unconfirmed.)

**F34** — Father's reaction: Surprised Abraham could fight the fire. Was checking for burns. Abraham kept assuring him he was fine.

**F35** — What was said: That he was brave to fight it; that they were glad he wasn't burned.

**F36** — Immediate aftermath actions: Making sure the fire was completely out (it still had smoke). Mattress moved outside. Windows opened.

**F37** — Household interpretation: They felt it was a miracle he woke up when he did.

**F38** — Whether the dream was disclosed at the time: Not answered directly. (See NEEDS AUTHOR CONFIRMATION.)

---

### Group G — The Lifelong Deal

**F39** — When he first understood the deal still governed him: He cannot remember a specific first moment. But each time he has had a close shave with death, the deal comes back to him. That is why he is rarely scared and takes high risks.

**F40** — Whether he ever told anyone: He does not think he ever told anyone about the dream or the deal until very recently.

**F41** — Effect on behaviour and self-image: It made him feel indestructible. Like a special force for God. Like someone with a special assignment — which means suffering AND the assurance of triumph.

**F42** — What "renegotiating" means in plain terms: Living beyond seventy-five would count as renegotiating. He is not sure he was truly of age for accountability when the deal was made. Sometimes he thinks he will live beyond 75 and, if so, perhaps to 120. He eats healthily and avoids alcohol, smoking, and womanising as part of honouring the deal.

**F43** — What the deal has cost: Reckless risks. Including marrying someone who was obviously unstable.

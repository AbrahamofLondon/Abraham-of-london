# Chapter 12 Marriage Author Recall Session

---

## Purpose

This file gathers source material for the expansion of Chapter 12 from its current ~1,050 words to the target of 4,000–6,000 words.

The chapter currently has the facts of the marriage sequence but none of the texture. The facts are accurate. They are narrated from outside the rooms in which they happened. The chapter must move inside those rooms. It cannot do that without physical detail.

Three room scenes are required:
1. The engagement room — where Abraham stood when approximately twenty people arrived on her side
2. The Lagos registry — the form, the counter, the moment the prior marriage fact appeared
3. The December ceremony — the physical space of the wedding day

A fourth scene is probable:
4. The return to Nigeria — the emotional/physical state Abraham arrived in; the room or house where decisions were being made

This is a memory-gathering file, not a drafting file. No prose is to be drafted from this material until the material is complete, classified, and risk-checked.

The chapter's central question: *What did Abraham see, minimise, explain away, or hope would become different before he said yes?*

## Remaining Questions

*These are the recall session questions. Present them to Abraham in the order and groupings below. Do not overload. One group at a time.*

---

### Group A — Return, grief, and emotional state

1. When you returned to Nigeria after David Senior's death and the burial period, what state were you in physically and emotionally?
2. Where were you staying during that period?
3. What did the house, room, or environment feel like? What did ordinary days look like?
4. Were you making decisions clearly, or were you moving from obligation to obligation?
5. What did marriage feel like at that time — hope, duty, escape, legacy, pressure, confusion?

---

### Group B — The engagement imbalance

1. Where did the engagement take place — inside or outside, which neighbourhood, whose compound or house?
2. What did the room or compound look like — chairs, layout, fabric, light, food, noise?
3. Who was visibly present on your side?
4. When you saw approximately twenty people on her side, where were you standing or sitting?
5. What did you feel in your body at that moment?
6. Did anyone say anything about the imbalance at the time, or was it unspoken?

---

### Group C — Registry discovery

1. What did the Lagos registry look like — the waiting area, the counter, the room?
2. What exactly happened when the prior marriage emerged — was it in a form, said by an official, shown in a document?
3. Who was present with you at the registry?
4. What did you feel first — shock, embarrassment, confusion, disbelief?
5. What explanation was given at the time?
6. What did you tell yourself in order to continue?

---

### Group D — The 4am cancellation

1. Where were you at about 4am when you said you wanted to cancel?
2. Was it in person or by phone?
3. What did you say, as closely as you remember?
4. Why did it feel like relief when it seemed agreed?
5. Where were you when she later called back?
6. What did she say about honouring the uncles?
7. What did you feel when you agreed to continue?

---

### Group E — Friends, isolation, and the call

1. When did you realise your friends were distant from this?
2. When you called or messaged a friend to say do not come to the engagement, what did you say? Where were you?
3. What did you expect from friends that did not happen?
4. What did you do alone during the engagement or wedding period that should ordinarily have been shared?

---

### Group F — The wedding day

1. What is the first physical image of the wedding day you carry?
2. What were you wearing?
3. What did the ceremony space look like?
4. What one image contains the whole day — the thing that, when you think of 23 December 2017, arrives first?

---

### Group G — Abraham's responsibility

1. What warning did you see but minimise?
2. What did you tell yourself that now feels untrue?
3. What did you hope marriage would settle or fix?
4. What must the chapter admit without becoming self-punishment?

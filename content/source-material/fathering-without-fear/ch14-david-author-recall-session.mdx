# Chapter 14 David Author Recall Session

---

## Purpose

This file captures source material for the expansion of Chapter 14 from its current ~960 words to the target of 5,000–7,000 words.

The chapter currently has strong *characterisation* of David — the fun-genes joke, his social brightness, his way in a room — but no *scene*. A person described is not the same as a person remembered. The chapter needs one or more specific moments in which David and Abraham were simply brothers, doing an ordinary thing together, before the chapter earns the right to ask the reader to feel the weight of his absence.

This is a memory-gathering file, not a drafting file. No prose should be drafted from this material until the material is complete, classified, and risk-checked.

---

## Canon Locks

These apply to every sentence written about David in any file.

1. David is Abraham's **brother**. Full stop. Never qualified. Never described as cousin. Never made a biological triplet.
2. David **died 5 May 2003, in Kent.** This date is fixed.
3. Friends and contemporaries knew David as part of the triplet-brotherhood / inseparable brotherhood. **That understanding is to be preserved with his memory.** It must not be contradicted in prose.
4. **Do not explain the private basis of the bond.** Do not disclose or approach the private pact's origin. Do not ask Abraham to explain it for this document. The pact exists in public form (the marriage promise) and that is the public-safe version.
5. The chapter must make David alive before it makes him absent. This is a structural rule, not a preference.
6. David must not become a symbol before he is a person.
7. The chapter must not bleed into Chapter 15 (Funke). Two separate losses, two separate chapters.
8. No date other than 5 May 2003 for David's death.

**F30 — The Kano airport road scene** *(Confirmed — Group B, 2026-06-22)*
*Source: Author recall session*

**This is F13. The chapter's critical located scene.**

> David was travelling to England. He was travelling from Kano toward the airport. On the road, Abraham felt a question rise inside him: what if he never saw David again? The question felt as if it was suggesting death. Abraham suppressed it. The question came again. Reluctantly, Abraham asked David about his relationship with Jesus. David looked at him in the usual way and burst into laughter. He said he was only going on holiday, not going to die, and would soon be back. Abraham asked again. David answered: "I am good, we are good." Then he hugged Abraham. They were quiet for a while. After that, David resumed singing along with the Celine Dion song playing.

Classification: **PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT**
**Exact line to preserve (author-confirmed):** "I am good, we are good." — do not polish unless the author later supplies a more exact remembered wording.
**Craft rule (author-confirmed):** Do not write this as prophecy. Do not write "Abraham knew." Do not make the scene prove anything. Do not overstate death. No spiritual spectacle. The register: *A question came. Abraham suppressed it. It came again. He asked. David laughed. David answered. They hugged. Then the song continued.*
Note: Celine Dion is specified — "a song-world that belonged more to Abraham than to David." This detail is important: David singing Abraham's music is a tenderness that belongs in the scene.
**Contextual question (pending):** Was this trip the one from which David did not return, or a different trip to England? The scene works either way, but the answer affects how the chapter handles the scene's position in time relative to David's death. Do not assume; clarify in Group C or later.
Expansion role: The chapter's primary David alive scene. Could function as: David alive before absence; brotherhood scene; faith-question scene; last-journey memory; ordinary music beside unbearable intuition.

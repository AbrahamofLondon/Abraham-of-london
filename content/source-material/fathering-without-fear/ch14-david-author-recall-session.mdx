# Chapter 14 David Author Recall Session

---

## Purpose

This file captures source material for the expansion of Chapter 14 from its current ~960 words to the target of 5,000–7,000 words.

The chapter currently has strong *characterisation* of David — the fun-genes joke, his social brightness, his way in a room — but no *scene*. A person described is not the same as a person remembered. The chapter needs one or more specific moments in which David and Abraham were simply brothers, doing an ordinary thing together, before the chapter earns the right to ask the reader to feel the weight of his absence.

This is a memory-gathering file, not a drafting file. No prose should be drafted from this material until the material is complete, classified, and risk-checked.

---

## Canon Locks

These apply to every sentence written about David in any file.

1. David is Abraham's **brother**. Full stop. Never qualified. Never described as cousin. Never made a biological triplet.
2. David **died 5 May 2003, in Kent.** This date is fixed.
3. Friends and contemporaries knew David as part of the triplet-brotherhood / inseparable brotherhood. **That understanding is to be preserved with his memory.** It must not be contradicted in prose.
4. **Do not explain the private basis of the bond.** Do not disclose or approach the private pact's origin. Do not ask Abraham to explain it for this document. The pact exists in public form (the marriage promise) and that is the public-safe version.
5. The chapter must make David alive before it makes him absent. This is a structural rule, not a preference.
6. David must not become a symbol before he is a person.
7. The chapter must not bleed into Chapter 15 (Funke). Two separate losses, two separate chapters.
8. No date other than 5 May 2003 for David's death.

## Remaining Questions

*These are the recall session questions. Present them to Abraham in the order and groupings below. Do not rush. One group at a time.*

---

### Group A — David's Physical Presence

1. What did David look like when you remember him most clearly? Not a description — an image. What do you see first?
2. Was there a particular way he entered a room — physically, what did he do?
3. What did his voice sound like? Loud, soft, quick, warm, teasing? What is the first word that comes?
4. How did he dress — not in general, but one specific outfit or item you associate with him?
5. What is one image of him that still comes back without effort?

---

### Group B — David in Company

1. What happened to a room when David arrived?
2. Was he the one who made people laugh, settled tension, started conversations, or noticed people standing alone?
3. Can you recall one specific gathering where his presence changed something — a dinner, a visit, a road trip, a family occasion?
4. What would friends who knew you both have said David brought into a room?

---

### Group C — One Ordinary Moment

1. What is one specific, ordinary moment between you and David — not dramatic, not the vision, not the death — just the two of you doing a small thing together?
2. Did you walk together, eat together, ride together, argue, plan something, tease each other?
3. Where were you? What was around you?
4. What did David say or do in that moment?
5. What was your usual rhythm with him when nothing was happening?

---

### Group D — The Brotherhood as Others Saw It

1. How did friends describe you both together? Was there a phrase?
2. Did anyone from school or university ever say something about the two of you as a pair — even jokingly?
3. How did the triplet-brotherhood understanding show itself publicly? Did anyone use that framing directly?
4. Did anyone joke about it? Was it acknowledged openly at any point?

---

### Group E — The Marriage Promise

1. Where were you when the pact was made? What surrounded the moment?
2. Was it spoken once, or returned to?
3. Was it said seriously, jokingly, or both at once?
4. What did David say specifically — not just the terms, but what made it sound like David?
5. When the vision came and David was absent, was the pact the first thing you thought of, or did it arrive later?

---

### Group F — David Missing From the Wedding

1. At the wedding, when did you feel his absence most sharply?
2. Was there a specific moment — a seat, a doorway, a greeting, a photograph, a silence — that made his absence visible?
3. Did anyone mention him?
4. What is the one image from the wedding that carries him in it, even by his absence?
5. What did you do with the absence at the time? Did you name it to anyone?

# Chapter 17 Contact Room Author Recall Session

## Purpose

Gather source material for the Ch.17 expansion pass. The current draft (Wave 4, 838 words) is structurally clean and legally sound but needs physical mass: rooms, bodies, waiting, toy detail, child behaviour, father's restraint, post-contact rhythm. This session provides that material. It does not draft prose. The expansion will follow separately.

Target after expansion: 3,000–4,500 words. Minimum: 2,500.

Ch.17 is not a legal chapter. It is the chapter where the reader understands the distance between what the file can record and what love actually looked like in the room. The spine sentence is:

> The file could register that contact took place. It could not register the way a boy waited to see if his father had understood the joke.

All expansion must protect that sentence. Nothing in this recall session supersedes it.

---

## Legal / Child-Protection Locks

These are non-negotiable across the entire session and any expansion that follows.

- No assessment names, no chambers references, no hearing transcripts.
- No mother/respondent framing or characterisation.
- No diagnosis or psychological claim about Damisi.
- No contact-report dispute presented as evidence — not the banana incident, not the birthday presents incident, not any characterisation of how reports are produced. These are LEGAL-SENSITIVE and must not enter public prose in any form.
- No specific court dates or case-chronology markers in prose.
- No report of Damisi's words that makes Damisi evidence of anything about his home or his other parent.
- No whispered exchange rendered in a way that makes supervision failure an accusation.
- No rendering of the Roblox / username attempt as supervision-evasion. It is a child trying to reach beyond an hour — render only that.
- Damisi must never be used as a witness. He is a child. He is the person this chapter is for.

### Group B — Arrival and Waiting

**F6 — Uncertainty of arrival**
Abraham never knows for certain, arriving, that the contact will happen. The possibility of cancellation is present at every arrival. This is not named in public prose, but it is the condition of the walk toward the building. The body must hold hope at a distance that can survive disappointment.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT (the reason for uncertainty — last-minute cancellation — is LEGAL-SENSITIVE; the uncertainty itself is public-safe and must be rendered as physical/psychological state only)

**F7 — Outside before the door opens**
Abraham always arrives early — ahead of the stated time. He waits outside. In cold weather, this means standing in the cold until the building is opened. The door is not yet open when he gets there. He stands. He waits.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (specific; physical; a concrete image of subordination to a schedule that is not his)

**F8 — Inside, in the allotted room**
Once the door opens, he waits in the room that has been allocated for the session.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F9 — The permission moment**
While waiting, he tries not to allow himself to fully expect the boy's arrival. He is managing hope — holding it back until he knows the contact is happening. The moment he knows it will not be cancelled is the moment he allows himself to expect. The body shifts at that permission. Before: hope held at a distance. After: hope permitted.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (this is a strong interior detail; the physical/psychological texture of managed hope)

**F10 — Hearing him before seeing him**
Damisi's voice arrives before his face does. Abraham can hear him on the stairs below, or in the corridor — asking where Abraham is. That question — "where is he?" — is the first signal that he is close. Abraham sometimes hides behind the door to receive him. Damisi knows to look for him. He is always looking.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (this is one of the strongest arrival fragments — the game is already in motion before the door opens)

**F11 — The hide-behind-the-door game**
Abraham sometimes hides behind the door of the contact room. Damisi comes in looking — he is always on the lookout. This is a recurring game, which means it is a recurring form of contact: the father hiding, the son seeking, the discovery, the laugh. They usually meet on the first floor. Occasionally on the ground floor. The exact room changes; the game doesn't.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F12 — Running up the stairs**
Damisi often runs up from below. Abraham hears the feet before the face arrives at the door. The sprint is part of how the boy arrives — not walking in, not being brought in, but running.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F13 — First physical moment: the kneeling**
He runs to hug Abraham. Abraham kneels down to his level — to see eye to eye. This is the first moment: the child at speed, the father at floor level, the equalisation of height, the hug.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (precise, physical, and one of the session's most important fragments — the posture of kneeling is specific and shows something about how Abraham enters the relationship)

### Group C — Damisi in the Room

**F14 — He runs to hug Abraham first, every time**
This is confirmed as the opening act of every session. He does not walk in and look around. He runs toward Abraham.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F15 — Car and dinosaur play**
During the session, Damisi often plays with cars or dinosaurs. He usually has them fight each other. This connects to the existing sports-car scene in the Ch.17 draft ("sometimes they played sports-car races").
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F16 — "I am not your bro, I am your son"**
Abraham tried once to sound cool — he called Damisi "bro." Damisi corrected him: "I am not your bro, I am your son." The correction was precise, immediate, and delivered without complaint — as a fact. Abraham is his father. That is a specific relationship with a specific name. Damisi knew the difference and said so.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is the chapter's key joke/understanding scene. It directly answers the spine sentence — "the way a boy waited to see if his father had understood the joke." The joke here is the correction: Damisi is testing whether Abraham knows what he is to him. Abraham, in being corrected, understood perfectly. This fragment is the scene the chapter has been waiting for.*

**F17 — Pokémon, YouTube, games**
Damisi tells Abraham about his world: Pokémon names, specific games, YouTube channels, TV programmes. He assumes Abraham already knows these things — the names, the hierarchies, the characters. Abraham often does not. The child occupies a life much larger than the contact hour. He brings it in anyway.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (shows the ordinary texture of a child's world spilling into the room)

**F18 — The reach-beyond moment (Roblox username)**
During one session, Damisi tried to give Abraham something — a username, a way to reach him outside the hours. He spoke quickly. The words moved fast. Abraham did not catch what was said. The attempt was the thing: a child who wanted to be reachable beyond the permitted form.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE — USE OBLIQUELY
(Do not name the platform. Do not frame it as supervision-evasion. Render only: a child who spoke quickly; something that was meant to bridge beyond the room; a name or number that didn't quite make it through; the desire to remain findable.)

**F19 — Competing, laughing, highlights of the month**
They always compete in games. They talk about the highlights of the month. Damisi tries to make Abraham laugh. The session has its own rhythm — not just the game the room provides, but the game they make.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F20 — Damisi as caretaker**
Abraham feels that Damisi is used to playing a caretaker role — toward Abraham, toward others.
Classification: PRIVATE — DO NOT USE
(This is a psychological observation about a child's coping pattern. It implies a claim about the child's home environment and must not enter prose. Do not use, do not reference obliquely.)

### Group D — What the File Could Not See

**F21 — Contact report management**
The author reports that contact reports are managed in ways he has experienced as partial. He describes specific incidents in which what was said differed from what was recorded.
Classification: LEGAL-SENSITIVE — DO NOT USE
(Any prose that characterises the accuracy or integrity of contact reports is a position statement. This material is sealed for the purposes of this memoir. Do not enter it in any form — direct, oblique, or implied. The chapter can show institutional incompleteness through scene; it cannot make a claim about document integrity.)

**F22 — Birthday presents incident**
Damisi refused to take gifts he had asked for and loved, explaining his refusal in terms of not wanting to make someone sad. Staff declined to record this.
Classification: LEGAL-SENSITIVE — DO NOT USE
(Damisi's statement, in this context, is being offered as evidence of something about his home environment. A literary memoir cannot carry this material without making the child evidence. This fragment is sealed.)

**F23 — Banana incident**
The author reports that Damisi said one thing and the contact record reflected a materially different statement.
Classification: LEGAL-SENSITIVE — DO NOT USE
(Report-distortion claim; sounds exactly like a position statement regardless of phrasing. Sealed.)

**F24 — Staff's divided attention**
Staff are not always fully present during sessions. In one room (Variant 3), the supervisor was using her phone or laptop while Damisi played on the floor. Damisi has noticed the divided attention. He sometimes tries to use it. Abraham does not encourage him to.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE — USE OBLIQUELY
(The divided attention of the room's official observer is renderable as a room condition. The child's adaptive awareness of it is obliquely usable — a boy who sometimes started a sentence and then finished it differently, who watched the room before speaking. Abraham's consistent rule-keeping — playing by the rules so that Damisi learns what consistency looks like — is PUBLIC-SAFE and important.)

**F25 — Simply being present**
What looks ordinary from outside but felt enormous: being present with your own child.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (already embedded in the existing Ch.17 draft in different form; confirms the chapter's emotional anchor)

### Group E — Leaving

**F26 — The ten-minute countdown**
When ten minutes remain in the session, Abraham begins to prepare Damisi for the transition. He counts down: ten minutes; five minutes; time to get ready to go. This is a deliberate practice — not the room's practice, but Abraham's. He is managing the goodbye before it arrives.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (specific; shows active parental attentiveness to the child's emotional readiness; gives the goodbye scene a structure)

**F27 — Damisi learning to leave without tears**
Damisi has occasionally held Abraham at the end and cried a little. He is learning to say goodbye without tears. The learning is ongoing.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT (render as physical fact; do not dwell on the crying in a way that makes it evidence of harm; do not attach it to a cause)

**F28 — The adjustment of expressed love**
Abraham has noticed that Damisi no longer initiates saying "I love you" or "I missed you" — not unless Abraham says it first. Abraham is caught between two fears: that saying it first will pressure the child, and that not saying it will leave the child to wonder whether it has stopped.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE — USE OBLIQUELY
(The causal chain behind this observation is LEGAL-SENSITIVE and must not appear. The observation itself — that a child has calibrated his expressions of love inside a supervised space — informs the chapter's theme of what the room prevents. Render it obliquely: a father who learned to say the first word so that the child could safely say the second.)

**F29 — The whisper**
On one occasion, Damisi whispered "I love you" to Abraham when the supervisor's attention was elsewhere. Abraham had to measure his response — not to make the whisper feel dangerous, not to let it go entirely unreturned.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE — USE OBLIQUELY
(This is the chapter's most tender unobservable moment. Render it obliquely: a child who sometimes spoke more quietly when the room was not quite attending. A father who learned to receive what he was given without making the receiving a performance. The word "whisper" is usable; the framing of supervision-avoidance is not.)

**F30 — Unconditional statement**
Abraham always tells Damisi, at the end of the session: he is loved; nothing he does will make Abraham love him more or less. He says it so that the child knows the love is not conditional on performance.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (this is the governing love statement; connects to Abraham's sense that Damisi tries to impress him and doesn't need to)

**F31 — Damisi trying to impress**
Abraham senses that Damisi works to impress him during the sessions. He wants Damisi to know this is not required. The love is not a performance review.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT (render without naming the psychological dynamic that underlies it — do not connect it to the caretaker-role observation in F20)

**F32 — The goodbye phrase**
At the end of the most recent session, Damisi said: "Have a nice rest of the weekend, see you next month."
The phrase was warm. It was also the goodbye of a child who has learned to be civil inside a constrained arrangement — who has absorbed the schedule and found a sentence that fits it.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT
(Do not attach this phrase to a legal argument about contact frequency. Render it as what it is: a child's goodbye that is both warm and devastating in its courtesy. The sentence is one of the session's strongest fragments.)

**F33 — The hug at arrival that does not let go**
When Damisi arrives, he hugs Abraham and holds on. You can feel, in the holding, that he wants more than the session permits. He does not say this. The arms say it.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT (physical fact; do not render the "more than permitted" as a complaint; let the arms carry it)

**F34 — The room after**
After Damisi leaves: the room is empty. Sad.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE (one word each; already implied in the Ch.17 draft's existing closing; confirms)

**F35 — The walk**
After leaving the building, Abraham goes for a walk around the area. He processes. Then he goes home.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is new and important — the chapter currently ends inside the room/session. The post-contact walk is a physical after-shape: the chapter can now follow Abraham out of the building, into the street, into the particular nowhere of a walk taken to feel something in fresh air before returning to a flat that is still the flat. This should open the expansion's closing movement.*

### Group F — Restraint and Danger

**F36 — The core existential situation**
Contact is precarious. Whether it will happen is not certain until it is happening. Abraham tries not to perform under supervision. He knows that every word can potentially be used. He knows that a child is watching to determine whether the father in the room is real or constructed for the occasion. He chooses to risk being real.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT
(This is the chapter's existential core. It informs the prose register without being stated in these terms. The phrase "I risk being real for my son" is strong and can be rendered obliquely: a father who was not interested in being legible to the room if legibility meant performing something other than what he was.)

**F37 — "Words have been twisted"**
The author reports that things he said in the contact room were interpreted or characterised in ways he did not intend and that were damaging.
Classification: LEGAL-SENSITIVE — DO NOT USE
(This would read as a position statement about the integrity of the supervision process. Sealed. The chapter shows the gap between room and record through scene — not through this claim.)

**F38 — "He is sovereign"**
The author's faith statement: God is sovereign over the situation. What has been taken, reduced, or constrained is not the final architecture of his relationship with his son.
Classification: PRIVATE
(This is an interior spiritual anchor, not a prose statement. It informs the chapter's closing register — the sense that the permitted forms are not the final form — without being quoted or paraphrased directly.)

**F39 — "He's my daddy too"**
The author's internal faith claim: God is also his Father; Damisi is also his son; the system has not removed either relationship.
Classification: PRIVATE
(This speaks to the spiritual interior of Ch.17's closing movement. Do not quote directly. May inform the register of the chapter's final lines — the distinction between what the room permitted and what it could not take.)

## High-Value Scene Candidates

These are the scenes the expansion should build. In order of priority:

**1. The bro/son joke scene** (uses F16)
Abraham calls him "bro." Damisi corrects him: "I am not your bro, I am your son." This scene belongs in the body of the chapter and directly answers the spine sentence. The existing draft says "the way a boy waited to see if his father had understood the joke" — the bro moment is that scene; the correction is the punch line; the understanding runs in both directions.

**2. The arrival / stairs / kneeling scene** (uses F7, F9, F10, F11, F12, F13)
Abraham outside in the cold before the door opens. The uncertainty of the walk toward the building. Inside: the wait. Then the voice from below — "where is he?" The game. The sprint up the stairs. The kneeling to eye level. This scene grounds the chapter's whole opening in the body and in the specific texture of arriving.

**3. The room-opening scene** (uses F1, F3, F4, F5)
Two possible rooms. Not the same every time. In one: wooden floors, Venetian blinds, natural light, baskets of toys, a leather armchair. In another: white walls, wooden floor, the supervisor against the wall with her screen, Damisi on the floor with a truck he brought in himself. The room precedes fatherhood. It does not accommodate it. The chapter can open by showing the room before the boy arrives — what the room is when it is empty, before either of them fills it.

**4. The goodbye / countdown scene** (uses F26, F27, F32, F33)
Ten minutes left — Abraham tells Damisi. Five minutes. The counting down. The hug that holds on. Then: "Have a nice rest of the weekend, see you next month." A child's voice, warm and courteous, inside a constrained schedule it has learned to live with. Abraham holding himself steady until the door closes.

**5. The post-contact walk** (uses F35, F34)
After: the street. A walk around the area. The processing that happens in fresh air. The room still in the body; the walk the way of moving the body through what the room did to it. Then: going home.

**6. The file-vs-room contrast** (uses F24, oblique F29, F25)
In the bare room: a supervisor's screen lit, attention elsewhere, while a boy played on the floor and a father sat close enough to catch him if he fell. The file would record: contact took place. It would not record the reach-beyond attempt that was swallowed before it fully arrived. It would not record the word spoken in a lower register when the room's attention lifted. The chapter already has this theme. The expansion makes it concrete — specific room, specific divided attention, specific unreachable moment.

# Chapter 18 Seven Years Author Recall Session

## Purpose

Gather source material for the Ch.18 expansion pass. The current draft (Wave 4, approximately 1,286 words) is conceptually clean and legally sound but carries no physical mass: no specific room, no journey, no recurring object, no calendar marker, no bodily detail. The boundary gate document confirms the expansion source is "one specific contact-session physical moment." This session exists to find that physical scene and the surrounding texture that makes duration visible.

Target after expansion: 4,000–5,500 words. Minimum: 2,500.

**Session update 2026-06-22:** The author provided recall responses in session. F16–F20 added from that response. FR1 (physical image) and FR2 (journey detail) are now answered — expansion unlocked. Three fragments sealed (judicial conduct, CAFCASS conduct, Damisi punishment observation). See Sealed section below.

Confirmed source fragments F1–F15 are drawn from the existing Wave 4 draft text. F16–F20 are from the author's 2026-06-22 recall response.

Ch.18 is not Ch.17 and must not become it. Ch.17 is the contact room — what the file cannot see. Ch.18 is duration — what seven years does to a person who keeps entering the next square. The distinction must hold in the prose.

Ch.18 must not become:
- legal chronology
- court complaint
- CAFCASS critique
- evidence bundle
- proof that Damisi loves Abraham
- proof that the system failed
- proof that Abraham suffered

It must become:
- seven years as lived time
- fatherhood under delay
- repeated departures
- calendars and journeys
- ordinary life held under suspended permission
- the cost of staying

## Legal / Child-Protection Locks

These apply to the existing draft and any expansion:

- No specific hearing names, case numbers, or assessment titles.
- No named legal representatives, judges, or CAFCASS officers.
- No adversarial characterisation of the respondent/mother.
- No report-dispute language.
- No evidence-bundle summary or proceeding catalogue.
- The specific date "19 July 2019" is already in the existing draft as a span marker. It is not a case marker in the prose — it marks duration only. Do not add further case-timeline dates around it.
- Do not use Damisi as proof of anything — not proof of love, not proof of suffering, not proof of alienation.
- Do not make Damisi carry the weight of why seven years was hard.
- Do not diagnose anyone, living or dead.
- "Silence as fatherhood" is the chapter's governing principle — what the chapter refuses to say is as important as what it says.
- Do not reproduce any observation about judicial conduct or CAFCASS officer conduct — not directly, not implied, not as subtext. F6 ("A structure can be necessary and still fail to see enough") is the only form available.
- Do not reproduce the author's observation about Damisi being "unable to cry because he would be punished for wanting to see his father." This specific framing cannot enter the prose in any form. It is sealed.

## Confirmed Source Fragments

*All fragments below are drawn from the existing Wave 4 draft text. They are confirmed in the sense that the author has already written them. They are not all final — some may need adjustment during the expansion pass.*

**F1 — The opening image: the calendar**
"The calendar did not look violent. That was part of the violence." The digital calendar as the chapter's governing metaphor — clean squares, one after another, as if a life could be made orderly by giving each demand its own box. A reminder appeared. A message needed answering. A document needed reading. A date moved closer. Another moved away. Nothing on the screen showed what seven years were doing to the body of the man trying to live inside those squares.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is the chapter's strongest single image. It must be the opening or near-opening. The calendar-as-violence framing is precise and original.*

**F2 — The span: 19 July 2019 to 2026**
The chapter names the span explicitly: not a vague "years" but a specific begin-date and an approximate end. The prose does not explain what happened on 19 July 2019. It uses the date only to mark duration.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT
*(The date is already in the existing draft. Do not add surrounding case context. The date marks lived time, not proceedings.)*

**F3 — Seven years as accumulation, not blow**
"Not as a single blow. Not as one date circled forever in red." Seven years arrived by accumulation — the daily, repeated, undramatic demand to keep going.
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F4 — The accumulation list**
"A form here. A hearing there. A delay. A rearranged expectation. A need to explain history again because continuity had been lost. A moment when legal help was available and another when it was not. A debt that did not care that the money had been spent in the service of fatherhood. A period when work itself was restricted, while the costs of fighting to remain present continued to grow."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE WITH RESTRAINT
*(Legal help, work restriction, and costs mentioned obliquely and without names. This is the correct level of abstraction — specific enough to feel real, not specific enough to identify a proceeding or party. Do not add specifics in the expansion.)*

**F5 — Endurance as ordinary: the repetition list**
"There were days when it did not feel like courage. It felt like opening the laptop again. Preparing again. Travelling again. Waiting again. Leaving again. Finding language again. Holding back language again. Paying what could be paid. Asking for help when help was possible. Standing alone when it was not."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This list is the existing draft's most physically grounded passage. The expansion should not replace it — it should give the abstract items (Travelling again. Waiting again.) a specific physical scene to anchor them.*

**F6 — The nature of institutional failure (not villains)**
"A structure can be necessary and still fail to see enough. It can invite participation and exhaust the person participating. It can ask a father to prove what should have been visible in the ordinary practice of fatherhood, then offer him only narrow rooms in which to keep practicing it."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is the chapter's frame for how it thinks about the system — not as a villain, as an insufficient structure. Keep this framing in the expansion.*

**F7 — Anger versus usefulness**
"He learned the difference between being angry and being useful. Anger could rise quickly. Usefulness took discipline."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F8 — The silence passage (acceptance-gate sentence)**
"Usefulness meant not saying everything he could say. It meant not turning Damisi into a witness for adult pain. It meant not making every contact session carry the weight of the whole fight. It meant accepting that some truths, even when they were his, could not be thrown into public view without making his child's world less protected."
"This was not silence as cowardice. It was silence as fatherhood."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is the chapter's acceptance-gate sentence per the Wave 4 footnote. It must be preserved. The expansion should earn it by building what precedes it, not by restating it.*

**F9 — The chapter's own restraint, named**
"There are things Abraham could explain that he will not explain here. There are records he could point toward that he will not reproduce. There are details that might satisfy a reader's appetite for certainty but would ask too much of a living child. The memoir is not less truthful because it refuses that bargain. It is trying to tell the truth without making Damisi pay for the telling."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This meta-passage names the book's own restraint as a feature of the memoir, not a defect. It should remain and is a candidate for a key position in the chapter — possibly near the end of the first movement, or at the transition to the second.*

**F10 — The refusal to vanish**
"His son could have grown around an absence and been told absence was the truth. Abraham could not allow that. So he kept entering the narrow forms available to him. He kept making the permitted life real."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F11 — Seven years of appearing**
"A single appearance can be explained away. Seven years of appearing becomes harder to erase."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F12 — Love stripped of comforts**
"It stripped love of many comforts he once associated with love. Love could not always choose the setting. It could not always extend the time. It could not always prevent misunderstanding. It could not always make evidence legible to people trained to read something else first. It could not always win in the visible way. But love could remain."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F13 — Separation of love from outcome**
"Across those years, Abraham learned to separate love from outcome. He learned to do what was right without knowing when the right thing would be recognised."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE

**F14 — What the years made: careful**
"The years did not make him soft. They made him careful. Careful with speech. Careful with documents. Careful with grief. Careful with Damisi's name. Careful not to let pain become permission. Careful not to write in a way that would win a reader and wound a child."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*The "Careful with Damisi's name" line is the chapter's most precise statement about child-protection within the memoir itself.*

**F15 — The calendar's final image**
"A calendar can make duration look tidy. It is not tidy. It lives in the person who has to keep entering the next square."
Classification: PUBLIC-SAFE
*This is the closing image of the first movement and a candidate for a recurring structural phrase through the chapter. "The next square" is specific without being legalistic.*

**F19 — What he noticed about Damisi** [CHILD-SENSITIVE]
Across the sessions, Abraham noticed things and did not always know what to do with them. The child not always able to express himself freely. Sounding sometimes too mature for his age — careful in the way a child becomes careful when he has learned that the wrong thing might cost someone pain. Being protective of his father's feelings in a way that reversed the usual direction of protection.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE — use with maximum obliquity
*(These observations cannot enter the prose as psychological claims, diagnostic statements, or evidence of anything happening at home. They can appear as things the father noticed and held — not as proof, not as accusation, not as analysis. The frame must remain entirely the father's interior: what he saw, what he carried, what he did not make Damisi responsible for. Do not name the cause. Do not interpret the child's behaviour in the prose. See also: Sealed — Damisi punishment observation below.)*

**F20 — The birthday card scene** [CHILD-SENSITIVE / PRIVATE — highest value in the book]
At a birthday session — Damisi had just turned 7. Abraham had written a birthday card containing a line to the effect that things had been difficult between them; that this would not always be so; that one day they would be able to freely see each other and be together. Damisi read the card. He paused when he reached that line. Abraham asked him to keep reading. He did. He said he loved it. He would not take it home.
Classification: CHILD-SENSITIVE / PRIVATE — use with maximum obliquity; do not quote card text; do not interpret the child's decision; do not name the cause of his refusal
*(This is the most emotionally specific moment the memoir has produced about the seven years. It must enter the prose — it is too true and too precise to lose. But the prose must render only what happened: a birthday, a card, a reading, a pause, a refusal to take it home, a boy saying he loved it. The card text cannot appear verbatim — the specific language ("things have been difficult," "freely see each other") names the restriction in a way that implicates the child's other home and makes Damisi a witness to his father's case. The prose can say Abraham wrote from love. It can show the boy reading. It can show the pause and the refusal and the "I loved it." Everything the refusal might mean stays unsaid. "He was seven years old" is the line that closes it. That is enough.)*

## Sealed — Do Not Use in Any Form

**S1 — Judicial conduct observation [LEGAL-SENSITIVE]**
The author observed that a judge was not interested in the evidence during proceedings; that the judge looked to the other side for signs to support them rather than reading the documents in front of him. This cannot enter the prose — not directly, not implied, not as subtext, not in any paraphrase. Direct judicial criticism would make the memoir a legal challenge rather than a memoir.

**S2 — CAFCASS conduct observation [LEGAL-SENSITIVE]**
The author observed that CAFCASS officers were waiting to support whatever the mother said rather than acting independently. Cannot enter the prose in any form. Same reasoning as S1. The existing draft's frame (F6) covers what can be said about the structure's insufficiency without naming officers, conduct, or institutional parties.

**S3 — Damisi unable to cry / punishment observation [CHILD-SENSITIVE — SEALED]**
The author's observation: "my son, sad and unable to cry because he would be punished for wanting to see his father." This is the most legally dangerous fragment from this session. It contains a specific claim about the child's emotional state AND a specific causal claim about why (punishment). It cannot appear in any prose form, including oblique paraphrase. The birthday card scene (F20) is the only form in which what this sentence describes can appear in the memoir — and even there, it appears without naming the cause. The gap between what F20 shows and what S3 claims is the chapter's silence. That silence is the memoir's integrity.

The uncertainty itself as physical experience: what the body carries when it does not yet know if the morning will resolve. This can appear inside the journey scene rather than as a separate scene.

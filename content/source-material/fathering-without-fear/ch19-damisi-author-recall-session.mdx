## Group F — What Must Stay Out

### Boundary Summary

Ch.19 may show Damisi. It may not make Damisi carry anything.

The chapter's child-protection rule is precise: ordinary boyhood may carry the chapter's emotional weight; Damisi's interior, pressure, psychology, and legal situation may not. Where the two overlap, show the surface and hold the interior back. Where holding it back leaves a gap, the gap is the chapter's integrity.

### Private — Do Not Use

- F49 — Highly sensitive child observation. Do not name, label, or imply "HSP" or any equivalent sensitivity designation in any form. The chapter may show sensitivity through ordinary moments; it may not name the category.
- F29 — Caretaker-role observation. The prose may protect Damisi by showing Abraham recognising and refusing to let him carry adult feeling. It may not state the observation as a claim about the child.
- F30 — Legal/report-sensitive material around contact. Ch.19 is the boy, not the case. Report disputes, professional conduct, and procedural pressure belong outside this chapter.
- F26 — Reach-beyond attempt. Do not name the platform, username, or method. Leave out of Ch.19 unless the author explicitly confirms a safer form.
- Any claim about Damisi's emotional development, psychological state, or response to restriction that goes beyond what an ordinary father notices in an ordinary hour.

### Child-Sensitive — Use Obliquely

- F6 — Alone / no one to play with. Passing line only, if at all. Do not interpret cause. Do not make it an indictment.
- F17 — Trying to stop Abraham being sad. If included, the scene must show Abraham recognising and refusing the role — not Damisi's distress as its subject.
- F27 — Whispered love. Emotionally strong but delicate. Do not make secrecy the subject.
- F48 — Crying after losing. Oblique only. If used, the scene shows Abraham's protective response; it does not analyse the child's state.
- F35 — Playground politics. About children negotiating play among themselves. Do not draw adult analogies.
- F37 — Tiger as strength. "Strength" is what Damisi said; that is the limit of what the prose may use. The inference chain behind it belongs to the father's private account.

### Refuse to Interpret

Ch.19 must not explain why Damisi is the way he is. Interpretation is what makes a child into evidence. Show the detail. Do not explain it.

Do not interpret:
- Why he prefers villains or Scar. Show the preference. Do not moralise or frame it as moral darkness.
- Why tiger replaced snake. Show the preference and the word "strength." Do not build an inference chain from it.
- Why he sometimes helps Abraham win. Show the moment of tenderness. Do not explain what it means about the child.
- Why he said "preserve, the Lord preserved me." The correction shows precision and faith-language. Do not make it a claim about his interiority or spiritual inheritance.
- Why he asked to escape through the window. The proposal is funny, childlike, and logical on its own terms. Do not frame it as legal commentary, a wish for freedom, or emotional testimony.
- Why he corrected "aunty." Show the language logic. Do not explain the authority instinct.
- Why he stopped crying abruptly. Show Abraham's response. Do not analyse what the abrupt stop means about the child's psychology.
- Why he would not take the birthday card home. This belongs to Ch.18. Do not import it into Ch.19.

### Final Expansion Rule

Show the boy.

Do not make the boy explain the father's suffering.

A child should be allowed to like tigers without becoming a symbol.

# Chapter 21 — Devotion Readiness Note

Ch.21 remains treated. The opening line remains reserved for direct author collaboration. Do not draft the opening line.

Ch.21 is the father/devotion chapter. It must carry David Senior's death with restraint. It must not repeat Ch.10's departure/provision material. It must not become sermon. It must not become elegy alone.

The chapter must show devotion as pattern, not performance. David Senior must live as father before he dies as symbol.

## Source Anchors

- David Senior died on 15 August 2017.
- Abraham returned to Nigeria on 10 September 2017 to bury him.
- David Senior's Psalm 27:10 prayer belongs structurally to Ch.10, not as Ch.21's main opening.
- David Senior had a lifetime pattern of giving and taking responsibility.
- School-fees story: after his death, a woman came to collect the second half of her son's school fees, not knowing he had died.
- He had no job or pension at the time but had still committed himself to helping.
- Ch.21 must show the cost and shape of devotion, not explain it abstractly.
- Ch.21 opening line remains the author's entry.

## Risk Note

Risk: If Ch.21 opens with death, it may become elegy too early. If it opens with virtue, it may become tribute. It must open with a concrete act or sentence that makes David Senior live before the chapter asks the reader to grieve him.

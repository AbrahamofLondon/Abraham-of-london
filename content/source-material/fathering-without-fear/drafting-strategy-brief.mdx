# FATHERING WITHOUT FEAR — DRAFTING STRATEGY BRIEF

*This brief is addressed to whoever drafts the prose — human or agent. It does not ask for more treatment. Treatment is complete. It asks for disciplined prose in a specific voice, in a specific order, with specific things held back.*

*Read this before opening any chapter file.*

---

## The Four Things This Book Must Never Become

Before any other instruction, name what the drafting pass must prevent.

### 1. A legal file
The book contains legal experience. It must not become a legal document. Every sentence that reads as: "On this date, this was filed, which was followed by this order, resulting in this outcome, which was challenged on the basis of—" is a sentence that has abandoned memoir and entered litigation. The reader came for testimony. They will leave if they receive a legal brief.

**The test:** Could a sentence from this chapter appear in a court filing? If yes, rewrite it.

### 2. A sermon
The book is unapologetically Christian. It must not instruct the reader theologically. The difference between memoir and sermon: memoir shows what faith cost and what it held; sermon tells the reader what to believe. The moment a chapter steps outside the narrator's experience and addresses the reader's soul directly — "This is what the Lord does when we trust him" — it has left the register.

**The test:** Is this a sentence Abraham would say to God, or is it a sentence Abraham would say to a congregation? Only the first belongs in the manuscript.

### 3. An ancestry brochure
The book contains ancestral material of genuine power: kingmaker lineage, founding ancestor, Isua, the walk to Lagos, the Adaramola name, Iyalode. This material is load-bearing inheritance — it explains the man. It must not become a genealogy display. The temptation in Parts One and Two is to exhibit the lineage rather than follow its pressure into the narrator.

**The test:** Does this sentence tell us who the family was, or does it show what that inheritance is doing to Abraham right now? Only the second belongs.

### 4. A revenge document
The book contains people who caused harm. Some of them are alive. Some are still inside the story. The memoir is not a counter-filing. It does not prosecute. It does not assemble evidence for a verdict the reader is being asked to render. The moment a chapter begins to feel like it is building a case — selecting details to prove, omitting details that would complicate, framing the other person as object of judgment — the register is wrong.

**The test:** Is this sentence about what Abraham experienced, understood, or chose? Or is it about what someone else did wrong? Only the first belongs.

## The Voice

**Controlled fire.**

Not rage. Not self-pity. Not legal pleading. Not spiritual exhibitionism. Not revenge.

The narrator is a man testifying under oath before God, history, his son, and himself. Every sentence earns belief through precision. The register is not angry and not broken. It is exact.

### What this means practically:

**Supernatural material** — narrated plainly, without apology and without inflation. The fire in Chapter 6, the trance before David's death in Chapter 14, Jumoke's kitchen appearance in Chapter 5 — these are narrated as things that happened, the way a witness narrates anything. No atmospheric build-up, no italics for effect, no disclaimer, no defence. The sentence is calm because the narrator is not trying to convince anyone. He is reporting.

**Legal material** — rendered through scene and consequence, not procedural overload. The waiting room matters; the docket number does not. What Abraham felt in his body during the adjournment matters; the procedural ground of the adjournment does not. One specific moment carries more legal weight than a paragraph of case history.

**Family material** — written with tenderness and restraint. The dead are people before they are symbols. The living are people before they are complications. No one in this book is merely their function to Abraham's story.

**Theological material** — embodied before explained. The verse arrives after the experience it interprets. The faith claim arrives after the cost that makes it legible. If the theology is in the chapter before the narrative has earned it, move it back.

**The narrator's self-account** — Abraham's blind spots, his naivety, his misplaced confidence, his delayed understanding are not weakness in the book. They are the book's authority. The reader trusts a narrator who does not exempt himself from judgment.

## The Prose Engine

*This section is an addendum to the voice definition above. The voice prevents failure. The prose engine produces force. A manuscript that follows the four prohibitions and the voice rules can still be disciplined, coherent, legally safe, and forgettable. The instructions below prevent that outcome.*

*Core principle: The book does not win by explaining more. It wins by placing unbearable facts in sentences calm enough to be believed.*

---

### 1. The Flat Clause Carrying the Unbearable Fact

This is the governing sentence mechanism of the manuscript.

**Structure:** Plain subject. Plain verb. The unbearable fact placed without theatrical preparation and without emotional commentary afterward.

The sentence must not beg the reader to feel. It must state the fact and move.

The narrator often did not understand the weight of the event inside the event. The prose must respect that. Do not announce significance. Do not explain impact immediately. Let delayed understanding do the work.

**Wrong mode:** "It was in that terrible moment that Abraham understood everything had changed forever."

**Right mode:** "He heard the sentence. He did not understand it then. He has been understanding it ever since."

*(These are drafting models only. Do not reproduce them as manuscript prose.)*

When a sentence carries death, spiritual terror, legal humiliation, child longing, or family rupture — strip it down. The heavier the cargo, the shorter the sentence. The more unbearable the fact, the flatter the clause.

---

### 2. Delayed Comprehension

**Rule:** If a scene explains its full meaning inside the scene, the draft is over-explaining.

Move the understanding forward. Sometimes the event belongs in Chapter 5 and the understanding belongs in Chapter 20. Sometimes the sentence should simply end and let the next scene carry the meaning. The reader's comprehension is part of the structure — it does not all need to arrive with the event.

This principle is especially important in:

- **Ch.5 Jumoke** — her death gives the book its central command; do not explain the command inside the moment of death
- **Ch.6 The Fire and the Deal** — the bargain is the memoir's covenant; its full weight only becomes clear across 22 chapters; do not front-load it
- **Ch.14 David** — the warning function of David's dream appearance must be felt before it is interpreted; do not interpret it in the same scene
- **Ch.16 Pregnant and Discarded** — the full cost of what happened here is distributed across Parts Three and Four; do not collect it all in Ch.16
- **Ch.20 Love Does Not Fear** — the theological synthesis earns its weight from everything that preceded it; do not let Ch.20 summarise; let it recognise
- **Ch.21 Devotion** — the heaviest chapter in the book; the prose must not try to carry the whole weight of 22 chapters simultaneously; trust what the reader already knows

---

### 3. Documentary Nouns Beside Sacred Nouns

This is the book's formal signature.

The memoir must not keep bureaucratic, legal, spiritual, ancestral, and theological realities in separate compartments. Abraham's life did not happen that way. A hearing date, a Teams screen, a verse, an ancestral name, a child's sentence, and a father's prayer may belong in the same paragraph — because they were happening at the same time, inside the same man.

**Required instruction:** Place institutional and sacred language side by side without hierarchy. Do not apologise for either register. Do not explain one as "real" and the other as "belief." Use the same flat witness-register for both.

**Purpose:** This is how the form of the book makes its argument. The system sees a case number. The narrator is living inside inheritance, faith, grief, and fatherhood simultaneously. The prose that holds both without explaining either makes the argument that no court document can.

---

### 4. Identical Evidentiary Discipline for Supernatural and Institutional Material

**This is critical.**

The supernatural material must not be softened — but it must be disciplined in exactly the way the institutional material is disciplined. The technique that makes a reader trust a court scene is the same technique that must make them trust a trance, a fire, a voice, a vision, or a moment of spiritual recognition.

**For supernatural material:**
- Give the concrete detail
- Avoid atmospheric inflation
- Avoid pleading for belief
- Avoid interpretation before the fact
- Do not defend the experience
- Do not exaggerate certainty beyond what was experienced

**For institutional material:**
- Give the concrete moment
- Avoid procedural overload
- Avoid advocacy
- Avoid case-file structure
- Avoid arguing with an invisible opponent

The same rule governs both: **specificity over assertion; restraint over conviction-stating; fact placed and left alone.**

A reader who does not share Abraham's faith should be able to read the fire in Chapter 6 and the Teams screen in Chapter 4 with equivalent trust — because the narrator applies the same evidentiary honesty to both.

---

### 5. The Corrective Second Sentence

This is the memoir's recurring prose fingerprint.

A sentence makes a claim. The next sentence narrows, corrects, or complicates it. This is already natural to Abraham's thinking — the way he arrives at harder truths by first stating the easier one and then pulling it back. It should become a deliberate recurring technique.

**Model:** "He thought it was faith. It was partly fear wearing a better suit."

*(Drafting model only. Do not reproduce as manuscript prose without author clearance.)*

**Instruction:** Use this roughly once per chapter. Do not overuse it. It is a sharpening tool, not a tic. Applied too often it becomes ironic commentary; applied at the right moment it is the most honest sentence in the chapter.

---

### 6. Single-Line Paragraph Restraint

**Hard rule:** Single-line paragraphs are powerful only if rare.

Use no more than two or three per chapter. Reserve them for the heaviest moments — a death confirmed, a court decision announced, a child's sentence remembered. Do not isolate ordinary emphasis. Do not create motivational-fragment prose. The book is memoir, not a sequence of standalone declarations.

**The test:** Could this single-line paragraph appear in a social media post as inspiration? If yes, it is either in the wrong place or the wrong register. The single-line paragraph in a literary memoir earns its white space from the weight of what surrounds it, not from the weight it announces for itself.

---

### 7. Vocabulary Without Apology

The manuscript should allow *Iyalode*, *Isua*, *Adaramola*, *Jesus*, *1 John 4:18*, *Microsoft Teams*, *Section 3C*, and *family court* to exist in one world — because they do exist in one world, in this man's life.

**Do not:**
- Italicise Yoruba terms merely to mark them as foreign
- Over-translate into explanatory asides
- Interrupt narrative rhythm with cultural footnotes unless the reader genuinely cannot follow without them
- Treat Christian vocabulary as requiring disclaimer
- Treat legal vocabulary as requiring humanisation

**Do:**
- Trust context to carry meaning
- Let unfamiliar words do work — the reader learns without being taught
- Allow the register to shift across a single paragraph the way a man's interior life shifts

The reader who cannot sit with *Iyalode* beside *Teams* beside *Psalm 27:10* is not this book's reader. Do not write for that reader.

---

### 8. Direct Address to the Dead

A rare structural device. Used correctly, it is among the most powerful moves available to the memoir. Used incorrectly, it is sentimentality.

**Rule:** Use direct address to the dead exactly once in each major death chapter, if the draft naturally supports it.

**Applicable chapters:** Ch.5 (Jumoke), Ch.14 (David), Ch.15 (Funke), Ch.21 (Devotion).

**Placement rule:** Not at the opening. Not at the closing. Place it at the structural turn — the moment where the narrator's witness mode briefly breaks and the grief speaks directly, before returning to the third-person or first-person witness register. One sentence, or at most two. Then back.

The direct address acknowledges what the flat-clause mode cannot hold without strain. It does not explain the grief. It does not ask the dead to explain themselves. It speaks to them as people the narrator still addresses, briefly, before the memoir's discipline returns.

Do not make it sentimental. Do not make it a sustained lyric passage. One turn. Then back to the record.

---

### 9. Escalation Architecture

The prose must not remain at identical pressure across all 22 chapters. A controlled book can still be flat. Controlled fire must be managed across the full arc.

**Wave 1–2 (Chapters 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10)**
Use the flat clause at its calmest. Short, declarative, almost reportorial. This is the register that trains the reader to trust the narrator. Every unusual fact is delivered plainly. No preparation. No build-up. The reader learns: when this narrator says something extraordinary, he means it.

**Wave 3 (Chapters 11, 12, 13, 14)**
Allow slightly longer accumulation. More clauses may stack before the full stop. The relational entanglement of this section — romance, warning signs, friendship, loss — has a different rhythm from the ancestral material. The prose can move more slowly, more densely, before it stops. Do not become ornate.

**Wave 4 (Chapters 4, 17, 18, 19)**
Return to shorter, colder sentences. The legal and systemic material should feel colder than the ancestral material. That coldness is itself the argument: the system strips warmth; the prose performs the stripping. A reader who feels the temperature drop from Chapter 13 to Chapter 17 has understood something the text did not have to explain.

**Wave 5 (Chapters 16 and 20)**
Permit one or two chosen breaks from the flat-clause rule — moments where the control briefly strains because the material has earned the strain. Only one or two per chapter. Mark these planned breaks in the drafting notes before drafting the chapter. Do not let them happen by accident.

**Chapters 21 and 22**
Return to calm. The prose must be still at the heaviest point. Stillness at the end is not restraint imposed from outside. It is the form the book arrives at because the narrator has kept his promise. The discipline of the final chapters is the final proof of the book.

---

### 10. The Unfinished Sentence and Held Material

Chapter 1 is held. Chapter 21's opening line is reserved. These are not merely legal or editorial restrictions. They are formal elements of the book's shape.

**Required instruction:** In chapters adjacent to the held material, allow occasional controlled approach-and-withdrawal. A sentence may move toward something the narrator is not ready to say — and stop.

Not overused. Not announced. Not apologised for. The reader should understand, by the time they reach Chapter 21, that this narrator has been practicing a specific discipline with what can and cannot be said. The approach-and-withdrawal is not evasion. It is a form of fidelity to what language can and cannot hold.

Chapter 1's absence should feel chosen, not missing. Chapter 21's opening should feel like a door the reader has been approaching since page one.

---

### 11. The Bestseller-Risk Test

Before any chapter is accepted as draft-complete, put it through all seven questions. All seven must be answered yes.

1. Does this chapter contain at least one sentence only Abraham could have written?
2. Does the chapter carry a scene rather than a summary?
3. Does the chapter avoid all four prohibited registers — legal file, sermon, ancestry brochure, revenge document?
4. Does the chapter make the reader feel trusted rather than instructed?
5. Does the chapter withhold enough to create force without becoming evasive?
6. Does the chapter end with movement, not resolution?
7. Would a reader who disagrees with Abraham's theology still trust the discipline of the witness?

If the answer to any of these is no, the chapter is not ready. Do not submit it. Return to the prose and find what is failing the test.

## Drafting Order

The order below is a recommendation, not a rigid sequence. It is organised on three principles: (1) build drafting confidence in low-risk chapters before entering high-risk ones; (2) draft the structural spine first so the book's architecture is established before complex chapters are filled; (3) legal/protected material is last, to be reviewed by the legal pass.

### Wave 1 — Establish the ancestral and childhood spine (low risk)
Draft these first. They establish the world the reader enters and the man who enters the rest of the book from it.

- **Ch.5 (Jumoke)** — the book's emotional foundation; lowest structural risk; a perfect first draft
- **Ch.9 (The Boy Who Became a Father at Fifteen)** — establishes the paternal pattern the rest of the book inherits
- **Ch.3 (The Boy Who Was Already Old)** — Abraham's origin and rejection; clean gates
- **Ch.2 (Isua to Agege)** — the founding ancestor; use "lineage" framing throughout

### Wave 2 — The bargain and its inheritance (medium complexity)
- **Ch.6 (The Fire and the Deal)** — the memoir's covenant; hinge file treatment applies; draft with the highest supernatural care of the whole manuscript
- **Ch.7 (The Name That Had to Be Powerful)** — Iyalode and Jesus; straightforward
- **Ch.8 (The Spiritual Covering)** — Iyalode in full contradiction; restrained dual frame
- **Ch.10 (Leaving Lagos)** — departure, provision, David Senior's death; all gates confirmed

### Wave 3 — Love, death, and collateral damage (complexity rising)
- **Ch.14 (David Was Missing From the Wedding)** — David as brother; 5 May 2003 locked
- **Ch.11 (A Post on Facebook)** — light chapter; doorway chapter
- **Ch.13 (Stephanie)** — dignity restraint throughout; draft carefully
- **Ch.12 (Married by December)** — legal caution; draft the velocity and the consequence; do not draft the evidence

### Wave 4 — The fatherhood fight (legal and child-protection restraint)
Draft only after Waves 1–3 are established and the voice is fully in hand.

- **Ch.4 (A Hearing Date)** — 27 July 2023 Teams hearing; one moment of waiting; legal caution throughout
- **Ch.17 (What the System Sees)** — systemic texture through scene; legal caution throughout
- **Ch.18 (Seven Years)** — 19 July 2019–2026; duration as formation; legal caution throughout
- **Ch.19 (Damisi)** — child-protection restraint throughout; draft from confirmed Damisi material only

### Wave 5 — Synthesis and close (highest craft demand)
- **Ch.20 (Love Does Not Fear)** — journaling as ordinary moment; 1 John 4:18 earned not cited; no cheap resolution
- **Ch.16 (Pregnant and Discarded)** — do not draft legal specifics; draft emotional register only; legal review before publication version

### After drafting is otherwise complete — author collaboration
- **Ch.21 (Devotion)** — the opening line is reserved; the rest of the treatment is in the hinge file; begin structural drafting once the opening line is confirmed
- **Ch.1 (Something Always Happens)** — HELD; do not attempt until author initiates

## What to Draft First in Any Given Chapter

Every chapter obeys the same internal sequence:

1. **The ordinary texture first.** Before the grief, the fight, the miracle, the hearing — find the specific mundane detail that grounds the stakes. Draft that.
2. **The event or confrontation second.** Once the reader is inside the ordinary world, the disruption of it carries weight.
3. **The ending movement last.** Do not draft toward resolution. Draft toward continuation or changed understanding. The book does not end because the battle is won. It continues because the promise is kept.

## How to Treat Held and Reserved Chapters

**Chapter 1 — HELD**
Do not write an opening sentence. Do not write a placeholder. Do not draft the chapter at all. When Chapter 1 eventually opens, it will arrive from the author, not from the drafting pass. The only preparation the drafting agent can make: when Chapter 4's opening image is drafted, note that it must be in a distinct register from whatever Chapter 1 will eventually hold.

**Chapter 21 — Opening line reserved**
The chapter's treatment is complete and in `hinge-chapter-treatments.mdx`. A drafting agent may study the treatment and understand the chapter's architecture. The first sentence may not be written without the author. Do not write a first sentence and call it a placeholder. The reserved line is the author's entry into the chapter, not an empty slot.

**Chapters 15 and 22 — CLOSED**
Treatment complete. Do not return to them. Do not append material. Do not reopen the question of whether they need more.

## The Mundane-Before-Grief Rule — Applied to Drafting

Every chapter that carries a death or institutional confrontation requires ordinary texture before the weight arrives. This is not an aesthetic preference. It is a structural rule derived from the book's central argument: the dead must live before they die; the fight must be recognised as a fight for something ordinary before the fight itself is registered.

**In practice:**
- Ch.5 opens in Jumoke's living presence (her clinginess, her attachment) before it opens in her absence
- Ch.4 opens in the texture of waiting (Teams screen, ten minutes) before it opens in what was at stake
- Ch.19 opens in Damisi's playfulness (window-escape suggestion) before it opens in what the restriction costs him
- Ch.14 opens in David as a brother (present, attending a wedding in some form) before it opens in his death
- Ch.18 opens in the rhythm of contact sessions (ordinary presence) before it opens in the duration of the fight

**If a chapter draft opens with the grief or the fight, it is wrong.** Find the ordinary moment first.

## Preventing Four Specific Failures

### Genealogy as display
When writing Chapters 2, 3, and 9, the temptation is to show how much is known about the Adaramola lineage. Do not exhibit the lineage. Carry it into the narrator. Every ancestral fact should arrive as pressure on Abraham, not as historical information offered to the reader. Use "lineage" when speaking of the founding ancestor's relationship to David Senior and Abraham — do not specify a count of generations.

### Procedural overload in legal chapters
When writing Chapters 4, 17, and 18, the narrator has access to a large body of procedural experience. Most of it should not appear. Select the moment that carries the pattern. The 27 July 2023 Teams hearing is not a representative sample of seven years of proceedings — it is the chapter's concrete anchor. The seven years are felt in the accumulated texture, not catalogued in it.

### Theological announcement
When writing Chapters 6 and 20, the theological material is earned by what precedes it. Chapter 6's bargain must be felt as desperate and specific before it becomes covenantal. Chapter 20's verse must arrive as recognition — the reader understanding Abraham's past in a new light — not as a lesson the chapter has decided to teach. If 1 John 4:18 appears in Chapter 20 before the reader has lived through the chapters that make it interpretive, move it later.

### The adversarial register
At no point in the drafting of Chapters 12, 16, 17, 18, or 19 should the prose position itself as a counter-argument to another person's account. The narrator testifies to his own experience. He does not refute. He does not pre-empt. He does not assemble evidence against. If a sentence sounds like it is written in response to something said in a court room or a legal document, it is in the wrong register.

## The Five Governing Questions — Active in Drafting

Track which question governs each chapter and let it govern the drafting:

- **Q1 — "What does a man inherit before he understands himself?"** Governs Parts One and Two. The ancestral chapters are not backstory. They are active inheritance pressure on the narrator.
- **Q2 — "What does death demand from the living?"** Governs the three death chapters (5 Jumoke, 14 David, 15 Funke). Each death commands something specific from Abraham. The chapter earns the command by first earning the grief.
- **Q3 — "What does faith mean when God preserves your life but not your comfort?"** Under the surface of Parts Two and Four. Most acute in Chapter 20. Do not let Q3 resolve before Chapter 20 has earned it.
- **Q4 — "What does a father owe his child when the system rewards disappearance?"** Governs Part Four primarily. The answer is not stated; it is shown in the accumulated fact of Abraham's continued presence.
- **Q5 — "What does it cost to tell the truth without destroying the people still inside the story?"** Governs Parts Three and Four absolutely. Every omission in these parts is a moral choice. Name the silence when the reader might otherwise feel cheated.

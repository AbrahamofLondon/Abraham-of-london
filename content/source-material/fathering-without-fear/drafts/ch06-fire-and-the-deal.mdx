# Chapter 6 — The Fire and the Deal

Before the fire, while he slept, there had been a dying.

Not a dream in the ordinary sense. He was in a state that had the quality of finality — the particular stillness of something being concluded. He was not afraid in it. That is the part he has noticed in the remembering: that he was not afraid, that he was present to something enormous, and that something spoke.

He asked for time. The specific amount: seventy-five years. He was a child. The number did not arrive through calculation. It arrived from wherever such things arrive from in a state like that, and he offered it. Something — he calls it God, and that is what he means, not a placeholder for something he cannot name more precisely — agreed.

Then the room was on fire.

He woke into it already burning. Not the gradual accumulation of signals — the fire was present before comprehension arrived. The body moved first.

There were two sisters in the room with him. He does not remember deciding to move. He remembers only that he was already moving, that his body had begun the work before his mind had caught up to what the work required. He reached them, or they were already near him — the sequence is not stored in language. What is stored is the physical fact of getting them out, of being outside, of the fire being behind them, and of his sisters beside him.

AUTHOR DETAIL NEEDED: the room — where was it in the house? What time of day or night? Were the sisters asleep or awake? What did the fire look like, sound like, smell like?

Their parents reached the room after. By then the children were already out. Abraham does not know exactly how he did it. He knows only that when he came to himself fully, they were outside, and the fire was behind them, and his sisters were beside him, and the bargain he had made in the dying state was now attached to a world he could feel with his hands.

The bargain was this: seventy-five years. In service. All of it. A child made that deal, in a dying state, before a fire that was real. He has been living inside it ever since. He has never tried to renegotiate.

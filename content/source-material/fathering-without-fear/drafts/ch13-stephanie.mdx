# Chapter 13 — Stephanie

They were walking.

That is the safest place to begin, because everything else that later gathered around Stephanie can make the story too heavy too quickly. Before the journal, before the warning, before the voice, before the years that would make Abraham look back at this period with a different kind of attention, there was a road in Anambra and two young people walking during national service.

Awka South LGA. Approximately 2002 or 2003. The kind of place national service makes temporary and serious at the same time. You are not home, but you must live there. You are not yet settled into adulthood, but adulthood has already begun asking for proof.

Stephanie had come from Ayamelum into Abraham's station because the conditions around her posting had become unsafe. Civil-war conditions is the phrase the source carries. Use it carefully. It is enough to say that she came from danger into the orbit where Abraham was serving, and she needed a transfer to Awka South.

One morning she asked him to walk with her.

They walked. Someone stopped them and asked whether they were service members. The person offered help. Stephanie asked about the transfer. The thing that had needed process suddenly had a path. It was effectively confirmed there and later formalised.

This is one of those moments that can become too symbolic if handled badly. In the moment, it was a walk and a person stopping them. A problem received help. A young woman stayed near the place where Abraham was posted. The chapter should not pretend he understood more than that at the time.

Their life there had ordinary commerce in it. Abraham travelled to Aba for fabrics and made limited-edition shirts. They sold quickly — sometimes within three hours. Stephanie sold female underwear and jeans, and her stock also moved quickly. They were young and industrious in the way people become when they are given very little structure and decide to build one. Fabric, travel, customers, quick sales, the small confidence that comes when an idea works in public.

This matters because Stephanie must not enter the chapter as a spiritual case. She was a person in a place, selling, moving, asking for help, receiving visitors, occupying a social category that Abraham could feel but could not easily name.

Not girlfriend.

Clearly something.

There was closeness. There was cuddling. There was no sex. The distinction is not defensive. It is the truth, and the truth matters here because this chapter can easily be pulled into a register it does not deserve. Abraham and Stephanie were close enough for the closeness to matter. They did not cross the line the reader might assume. The memoir does not need to borrow heat from what did not happen.

She had a long-term boyfriend. Approximately fifteen years. Abraham knew enough to understand that whatever category he occupied was not simple. He did not yet know how to read that complexity well. The chapter is not written to punish her for it. It is written because Abraham's education in spiritual and emotional ambiguity passed through her.

There were incidents where Stephanie appeared to know what people had said about her when she was not present. The chapter does not ask the reader to accept an explanation for this. It reports Abraham's observation. Things were said. She seemed to know. The ordinary world around them did not stop being ordinary because of that. The fabrics still sold. People still came and went. Service still had its rhythms.

Then there was the journal.

It had been written years before they met. That mattered to Abraham. It did not arrive as something composed for him or around him. It existed before him. When he encountered it, the journal contained a disclosure about a spirit husband. The phrase belongs to a spiritual world many readers will not know. The chapter does not need to translate it into a category more comfortable for them. It needs only to place it with discipline: this was what Abraham encountered, and this was how the warning entered the story.

The next day, a voice threatened to kill Abraham for his wife.

His wife.

The phrase did not fit Abraham's category for Stephanie. That was part of the disturbance. The warning came in language that claimed a relationship Abraham had not claimed in that way and did not control. Two near-death experiences followed. The chapter must not inflate them here. Their function is formation: Abraham was inside something he did not yet have the full discernment to read.

He underestimated the warning.

That sentence is not accusation. Not of Stephanie. Not of Abraham. It is the plainest description of what happened. He was young. He was drawn. He had already been formed by spiritual seriousness, but not every kind of spiritual danger announces itself in the register one already understands. Sometimes the warning is clear and still not absorbed, because the person receiving it has not yet become the person who can receive it fully.

Years later, in 2013, after redundancy, Abraham reconnected with Stephanie. She tried to help him with work or business. She mentioned him to her husband. Her husband told her to cut ties and not mention it again. The chapter does not make him a villain for this. A husband can recognise a boundary from inside his marriage. Abraham may not have liked the sentence. He can still write it without prosecuting it.

That is the ethic this chapter requires. Stephanie remains protected by dignity. Her husband remains protected by dignity. Abraham's memory remains honest without converting her life into spectacle for the reader.

What he carried from Stephanie was not a romance story. It was an education in proximity, spiritual warning, desire, restraint, and the difficulty of reading a situation while one is inside it. He had not yet gone to London. He had not yet entered the later relationship. He had not yet become a father. But a part of him had already learned, and not learned, what it means to stand near danger and call it closeness because closeness is the part you can feel.

The warning was given.

He did not know how seriously to take it until later.

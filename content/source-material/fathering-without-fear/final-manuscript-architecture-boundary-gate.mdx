## Section 1 — Executive Verdict

**Structural verdict: STRUCTURALLY READY BUT UNDER-BODIED.**

The manuscript's architecture is coherent. The four-part structure holds. The interwoven timelines are established. The voice is consistent and has not drifted. The canon is clean. The boundary chapters are correctly protected. The legal and privacy locks are holding.

The manuscript is not near final length.

The 19 drafted chapters average 1,017 words each. The target is approximately 4,100–4,500 words per chapter across 22 chapters. Three chapters — Ch.1, Ch.15, and Ch.22 — are either held or closed and contribute zero words at present.

This is not a failure. The body-pass cycle was never intended to produce final-length chapters. It produced clean, structurally sound scene-spines. What they need now is not correction but mass — rooms, physical detail, duration, social consequence, memory, and time.

**Word-count verdict:** The manuscript currently holds approximately 19,323 words across 19 drafted chapters. The target is 90,000–100,000 words across 22. The gap is approximately 70,000–80,000 words.

**Expansion verdict:** The gap is closeable. It requires a staged plan: author-detail recall sessions, a scene-first expansion pass applied chapter by chapter, and eventual decisions about the three boundary chapters. Expansion must come from scene and memory, not from commentary, explanation, or repetition.

> The body-pass cycle made the chapters clean and safe. It did not make them final-length chapters. The next phase must now do what only memory and scene can do.

## Section 7 — Full Manuscript Expansion Strategy

The expansion phases are sequential. Do not enter a later phase until the earlier one is substantially complete.

### Stage A — Author Detail Recall Sessions

**Purpose:** Fill the 12 active AUTHOR DETAIL NEEDED markers, beginning with Ch.14.

**Sequence:** Follow the priority order in Section 6. Ch.14 is first and cannot be deferred. All other markers can be gathered across multiple sessions.

**Method:** Per recall session — the agent presents the specific anchor request, the author supplies the detail, the agent records it in the chapter footnote as source-confirmed material, and the AUTHOR DETAIL NEEDED marker is converted to a sourced anchor. No prose is written in the recall session itself unless the author supplies a full passage.

**Output of Stage A:** 12 markers converted; Ch.14 unblocked; all chapters have their sensory anchors confirmed or parked.

**Estimated added words from Stage A alone:** 4,000–8,400 (from the anchors themselves; does not include the scenes those anchors enable).

---

### Stage B — Scene-First Expansion Pass (chapter by chapter)

**Purpose:** Expand each drafted chapter from its current scene-spine into a full literary-memoir chapter. Follow the priority order in Section 4.

**Governing rules (from revision bible):**
- Scene first, always. No chapter expands by adding explanation. It expands by rendering the scene fully — the room, the bodies in it, the objects, the duration, the social consequence, the silence.
- A declaration may remain only if the scene has already earned it.
- Do not invent. If source detail is missing, mark the gap and continue with what is confirmed.
- One chapter per pass. Do not expand two chapters simultaneously.

**Heavy chapters requiring full scene construction:**
- Ch.14: 4,000–6,000 words added; requires David alive before his absence in the first half; trance and journey in the second
- Ch.12: 3,000–5,000 words added; three distinct room scenes (registry, engagement, ceremony)
- Ch.6: 2,800–4,300 words added; the fire room must be rendered in physical terms; the dying state; the bargain
- Ch.17/Ch.18: 2,800–4,300 words each; the fatherhood sequence rendered scene by scene
- Ch.21: 2,750–4,750 words added; the interview room across a full day; the Monday morning in Norwich

**Standard chapters:** Each gains 2,000–3,500 words through scene-first rendering of their existing spines.

**Short literary hinges (Ch.4, Ch.5, Ch.11):** Restrained expansion only — 1,000–2,000 words maximum; these chapters are deliberately compact by design.

**Estimated added words from Stage B:** 25,000–40,000.

### Stage D — Whole-Book Compression and Flow Pass

**Purpose:** After expansion, cut repetition. Read the full manuscript as a continuous text for the first time.

**What to look for:**
- Repeated ideas across chapters that were not visible at the chapter scale
- Summary passages rendered redundant by newly expanded scenes
- Tone shifts between chapters at the Part boundaries
- The motif of rooms — does it recur naturally or has it become too visible?
- Direct addresses to the dead — one per chapter in the four death chapters only; check that none have drifted into other chapters
- Escalation architecture — does the prose work harder in Waves 3–5 than in Waves 1–2?

**Important:** Stage D is a compression pass after expansion. Do not cut before the manuscript is at scale. Cutting a scene that is the only body in a chapter is a mistake that Stage B will have to undo.

## Section 9 — Risk Warning

> The risk is not that the manuscript cannot reach full length.
>
> The risk is reaching full length through explanation rather than scene.
>
> Every chapter in this manuscript has enough material to grow by explanation — to tell the reader what the scenes meant, to summarise what the chapters already show, to add theological interpretation, to layer legal context. That path produces a longer manuscript and a worse book.
>
> The book must grow by recovering rooms, bodies, objects, silences, and remembered sequences — not by explaining what those scenes mean.
>
> A chapter that doubles in length through scene is twice the book it was. A chapter that doubles in length through commentary is half the book it was trying to be.
>
> The test is the revision bible's Rule 1: every major claim must be supported by a physical scene. If the expansion adds words but no new physical scene, the pass has failed.

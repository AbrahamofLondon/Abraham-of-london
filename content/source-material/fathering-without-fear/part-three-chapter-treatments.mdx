PART THREE CHAPTER TREATMENTS — TRACK B

Part Three is *Love, Death, and Collateral Damage*. Its function is
to move Abraham from the London arrival of Chapter 10 through the
full arc of attraction, marriage, warning, grief, and collapse —
arriving at the threshold of Part Four's legal and systemic fight.
The danger throughout is heat. This section must not become
accusation, melodrama, litigation, or therapy transcript. Its job
is to preserve emotional truth while keeping the narrator morally
credible.

Chapter 15 (Funke) is the grief/premonition hinge inside Part Three.
It is fully treated in `hinge-chapter-treatments.mdx` and is not
re-treated here. This file covers Chapters 11, 12, 13, 14, and 16.

Chapter numbers follow the final 22-chapter sequence. The Five
Governing Questions are quoted exactly from "The Five Questions" in
`fathering-without-fear-source.mdx`:

1. What does a man inherit before he understands himself?
2. What does death demand from the living?
3. What does faith mean when God preserves your life but not your comfort?
4. What does a father owe a child when the system rewards disappearance?
5. What does it cost to tell the truth without destroying the people still inside the story?

PART THREE STRUCTURAL ARGUMENT

Part Three covers the period from Abraham's London arrival (late in
Chapter 10, Part Two) through the collapse of his marriage and the
beginning of the fatherhood fight. The timeline is not strictly
linear. Chapter 13 (Serena) is placed after the marriage chapters
(11–12) as a retrospective, functioning as earlier-formation material
that the reader needs to understand why Abraham was vulnerable in the
London relationship. The insertion follows the book's existing
structural logic — the same interweaving of ancestral/prior material
with present-day events used throughout the book.

The section must pass four tests throughout:

**1. No legal contamination.** Do not import court documents, Home
Office records, transcripts, or third-party legal records. Part Three
can point toward the legal fight ahead, but it must not become a
legal bundle. The legal material is Part Four's territory.

**2. No revenge prose.** Where relational harm appears: state what
happened; avoid theatrical judgment; avoid name-calling; avoid proving
too much; do not write as if arguing before a judge. The reader must
feel the damage without feeling manipulated.

**3. No saint/villain flattening.** Abraham must not appear as an
innocent hero. The other parties must not appear as cartoon antagonists.
Where Abraham was confused, hopeful, naïve, stubborn, spiritually
pressured, or self-deceiving, the treatments must leave room for that.
The memoir's authority comes from this restraint.

**4. No grief spectacle.** Chapter 15 is the Part Three grief hinge.
Do not dilute it by spreading the same grief logic across adjacent
chapters. Each loss in Part Three — David, Funke — must have its own
distinct weight and its own distinct register.

The reader who finishes Part Three should understand, without being
told: that the relational collapse was not a clean story of victim
and villain; that every major death and departure arrived in a man
already carrying an enormous prior load; that the fatherhood fight
now beginning in Part Four has been earned by everything the previous
fifteen chapters established; and that the bargain with God made in
Chapter 6 will now be tested in its hardest terrain.

CARRY-FORWARD NOTE — Q5 GOVERNS PART THREE

Question 5 — "What does it cost to tell the truth without destroying
the people still inside the story?" — operates as the governing
craft constraint throughout Part Three. All factual material about
the marriage, the relational collapse, and the people involved must
be derived from Abraham's own memory and approved source material.
No court documents. No Home Office records. No third-party legal
accounts. No speculation about other people's internal states or
motivations beyond what Abraham directly perceived.

This constraint is not weakness. It is the source of the memoir's
authority. The reader who is given the consequence without the
accusation arrives at their own verdict with far more conviction
than the reader who has been argued at.

### Chapter 11 — A Post on Facebook — Revised Premise

**Opening image:** Abraham is back in Nigeria after the MBA period and after burying David Senior. The country is familiar, but he is not returning as the same man who left. His father is dead. The MBA has ended. The refinery possibility and the next phase of life appear to be opening, but grief is still fresh enough to make recognition feel like rescue.

**The post:** Abraham posts about his father's death. The post is not a strategy. It is not a search for romance. It is a statement of loss.

**The reply:** A woman from his past responds with a specific point of shared grief: her own father, she says, died in a plane crash. This is not generic comfort. It presents itself as understanding purchased by pain. That is why it lands.

**The invitation:** She invites Abraham back to the UK. The chapter ends near the invitation and what it felt like to receive it. The consequences belong to Chapter 12.

**Register instruction:** Chapter 11 must not contain the later recognition that real pain can be used to paint a false picture. That understanding is true, but it belongs later. This chapter must remain inside what the exchange felt like at the time.

**Craft risk:** Dramatic irony. Do not warn the reader. Do not wink. Do not write a sentence that tells the reader to distrust what Abraham did not yet distrust. If the prose begins to prosecute the future from inside the past, cut it.

**Primary governing question:** What does it cost to tell the truth without destroying the people still inside the story?

**Restraint:** Render what was offered and what Abraham experienced. Do not diagnose motive. Do not name her. Do not flatten the plane-crash detail. Do not turn the chapter into accusation.

### Chapter 12 — Married by December

**Opening image:** Not the wedding. Not the ceremony. The specific
moment when Abraham understood — or did not yet understand — the
velocity at which everything had moved. The Chapter 11 reconnection,
the later UK return, and the December 2017 marriage in Lagos. The image is
the speed itself, made physical: a date on a form in a Lagos registry,
the particular feeling of having made an irreversible decision before
the full nature of what he has entered has resolved. Not judgment —
scale. A man measuring the distance between where he was and where
he now is, and discovering the distance was covered faster than he
knew.

**Chapter function:** To show how quickly an entire future can be
rearranged before a man fully understands what he has entered. The
chapter moves in three beats: speed → ceremony → warning. First: the
velocity (Facebook reply to invitation back to the UK to marriage,
his MBA finished, no stable ground yet). Second: the ceremony and
what it revealed (a prior marriage discovered at the registry; an
engagement ceremony from which his friends were excluded while her
twenty came; a friend turned back from Nigeria; the chapter shows
how much Abraham didn't know before he committed). Third: the warning
seen and not taken (friends who bought December tickets and refused
to come after hearing what happened; friends who saw the danger
before Abraham accepted it). The chapter must make the reader
understand why Abraham kept walking even as each beat revealed more
— not because he was foolish but because the inheritance drove him
and the conviction had already moved faster than his capacity to
question it.

**Governing question:** Q1 runs beneath — "What does a man inherit
before he understands himself?" The helper/provider pattern
inherited from David Senior's fifteen-year-old formation now operates
in a context it was not built for. Q5 governs: the truth told here
must not destroy the people still inside the story.

**Emotional centre:** The specific difficulty of distinguishing love,

something. That belief moved with velocity. The chapter's emotional
ground is what happens when the instinct to commit, inherited from
a lineage of people who committed without full information, runs
into a context it cannot correctly read.

**Theological pressure:** The inherited pattern of stepping up and

### Chapter 13 — Serena

**Opening image:** Not Serena. Not desire. Walking. One morning
during national service, Abraham and Serena walked together —
just walking, the particular kind of purposeless walking that
national service generated, two young people in a place neither
fully chose, moving through it. The chapter can open in that
walk: the road, the time of day, the specific quality of proximity
before meaning was attached to it. Awka, Anambra, approximately
2002/03. Not romance. The room before romance. The shirt trips to
Aba. The work of selling. The fabric. The life around two people
who are not yet in a category.

**Chapter function:** To illuminate, through a formative earlier
encounter, the romantic and spiritual inheritance Abraham brought
to London. The chapter is placed after Chapters 11 and 12 not
because it happened after them — it happened earlier, during national
service in Nigeria — but because the reader needs this prior
formation to understand the London vulnerability more fully. It
is a retrospective chapter: the book steps back to show where
Abraham's patterns in romantic territory were first shaped. After
the speed of Chapters 11–12, Chapter 13 gives the reader the earlier
experience that makes that speed legible.

The chapter also establishes the specific kind of spiritual pressure
Abraham encountered in intimate territory: spirit-husband disclosure,
warning, the gap between what he felt and what was actually present.
This is the first time the book places spiritual danger inside
romantic attachment. It will not be the last.

**Governing question:** Q3 primarily — "What does faith mean when
God preserves your life but not your comfort?" — because the
Serena encounter placed Abraham inside a form of spiritual danger
he underestimated, and the discomfort of that encounter was part of
his formation. Q1 runs beneath it: what does a man inherit before
he understands himself? The patterns of how Abraham moved in
romantic territory — what he was drawn toward, what he dismissed,
what he underestimated — were formed before London.

**Factual spine:**
- NYSC (Nigerian Youth Service Corps) posting: Awka South LGA,
  Anambra, approximately 2002/03
- Abraham and Serena first met in the dark during national service

  texture
- She had been evacuated to Abraham's station from Ayamelum because

- One morning she asked Abraham to walk with her; they were flagged

  help; Serena asked for her transfer, and it was effectively

- Ordinary national-service life: Abraham started a shirt business,

- Abraham and Serena never had sex; there was closeness and

- The warnings that followed the journal encounter: a voice threatened

- The author notes Serena may claim not to remember certain details;
  he doubts this but wants her dignity respected; the chapter must
  handle everything within that ethic
- *Note on Chapter 13 / Chapter 14 geography:* Abraham was stationed
  in Awka during this national-service period. When David died in

  all material is from Abraham's own memory

All factual material comes from Abraham's own memory and approved
source material. No speculation about Serena's internal states
or motivations. No construction of her as a villain. Serena's
marriage and dignity are protected throughout.

**Emotional centre:** The specific quality of youthful misreading —
not stupidity, but the blindness that comes from desire, inexperience
in spiritual discernment, and the instinct to rescue rather than to
protect oneself. Abraham was drawn toward something and did not
yet have the vocabulary to read what was in it. The chapter's
emotional ground is the experience of being inside an encounter
whose real nature you cannot yet see — and the specific texture
of understanding, years later, what the warning had been and how
completely you missed it.

**Theological pressure:** The spirit-husband disclosure and the
warnings that follow are this chapter's theological territory.
Abraham encountered, in the Serena relationship, a form of
spiritual interference his earlier formation had not prepared him
for. The book handles this the way it handles all spiritual material:
narrate what happened, what was believed, what was warned, what
was later understood. Do not adjudicate between spiritual
frameworks. Do not dismiss what was reported. Do not make it
theatrical.

**Inheritance thread:** The Serena encounter is where a blind
spot in Abraham's spiritual discernment was first formed — or first
revealed. What he failed to read here, he brought forward to London.
The inheritance thread in Chapter 13 is negative formation: the
specific gap in Abraham's readiness for what romantic territory
could contain. He arrived in London already carrying this gap.

**Fatherhood thread:** Absent at this stage. This is a young man's
story from a time before fatherhood was a category in his life.
The chapter does not reach forward.

**Mundane-before-rupture gate:** FILLED. The national-service

coming and going, the particular social category of "not girlfriend
but clearly something." These are the chapter's grounding details.
The supernatural material arrives into an ordinary life already
established.

**Craft risk:**
(a) Spiritual overclaiming — the spirit-husband material is specific
and serious; the chapter reports what Abraham understood to be
happening, not what the book pronounces as metaphysical fact. The
reader is not asked to agree; they are asked to understand what
Abraham encountered and how he processed it.
(b) Gossip register — the chapter handles material (journal contents,
long-term boyfriend, husband's instruction to cut ties) that carries
a risk of reading as private revelation about someone who did not
consent to this account. The chapter must handle all of this as
Abraham's experience and formation, not as information about
Serena. She must exist as a person. Her husband must not become
a villain. Her fifteen-year boyfriend is context, not backstory.
(c) Sexualising the relationship — the chapter must not imply more
than the author states. Abraham and Serena never had sex. The
closeness and cuddling are held as what they were: something real,
something that mattered, something that the chapter does not need
to inflate.
(d) Over-drawing the line to London — the reader will draw the
connection from Serena to the London relationship without
assistance. The chapter must not draw it for them. State what
happened. Let the resonance travel forward without annotation.
(e) Spectacle — keeping the supernatural claims grounded in ordinary
NYSC life is the structural defence: walking, transfers, business,
visitors, fabric, selling quickly, shared proximity, things said
when someone was not present. The supernatural material arrives
into that life, not above it.

**Prohibited material:** Serena's internal states or motivations
beyond what Abraham directly knew or perceived. Court records or
legal material of any kind. Any direct connection named between the
Serena story and later legal proceedings. Any construction of
Serena's journal contents as confirmed spiritual diagnosis rather
than as what Abraham encountered at the time. Any framing that
treats the spirit-husband disclosure as proof of any theological
position about spiritual marriage.

**Ending movement:** The Serena chapter closes on what Abraham
carried away from it: what he understood, and — more important —
what he did not yet understand. The warning was given. It was held
lightly, not fully absorbed. The reader feels the resonance forward
without the chapter naming it.

### Chapter 14 — David Was Missing From the Wedding

**DATE CORRECTION (canon):** David died **5 May 2003, in Kent**.
This date is fixed. Do not use any other year. Do not use 2023 or
any date connected to the marriage arc. David's death preceded
Abraham's UK marriage by fourteen years.

**Opening image:** Not death. Not the vision. David alive — his
quality in a room. Parties, girls, friends, well-dressed, truly
kind, temperamentally the opposite of Abraham in almost every
particular. When people remarked that David and Abraham looked or
behaved nothing alike, David joked that all the fun genes had gone
to him and Abraham had received all the serious ones. That is the
opening key: the laughter in it, the love in it, the particular
brightness of the person who was the companion of Abraham's entire
early life. The chapter cannot open on his absence. It opens with
his presence.

**Chapter function:** To render David as the brother, full stop —
and to hold the mechanics of their pact as the lens through which
Abraham understood a vision before he consciously understood what
the vision meant. The chapter is not primarily about grief, though
grief is present. It is about brotherhood as a form of covenant —
and about how a covenant can carry more information than either party
intended when they made it. The chapter must make the reader feel
who David was before it asks the reader to feel what his absence
meant.

**Governing question:** Q2 primarily — "What does death demand from
the living?" — but refracted through a specific structure: the pact.
David's death demanded that Abraham understand what he had lost
before he consciously knew it was lost. That is a different kind of
demand from ordinary grief. Q1 runs beneath: the man who was given
all the serious genes now carries the full weight alone.

**Factual spine:**
- David: Abraham's brother — full stop (canon: never qualified,
  never "cousin," in any file under any circumstance)
- **David died 5 May 2003, in Kent.** This is confirmed. Do not
  use any other date or year.
- David was the companion: childhood, school, university, every
  private crossing of Abraham's early life — David was there
- The pact: neither would say "I do" without the other's approval
  and presence — a covenant made between them; its logic depended
  on both of them being alive and present to each other
- Abraham had a vision in which Abraham's wedding was shown to him —
  and David was absent from it
- This was not a real scheduled wedding in 2003; the vision used
  the pact's logic to convey what could not be said plainly
- Because of the pact, David's absence from the vision could mean
  only one thing: he was already dead
- Abraham travelled to Lagos the next day to confirm — he had been
  at his NYSC posting in Awka, Anambra (the same period as Chapter 13)
  when the vision came; the journey from Awka to Lagos is the night
  between the vision and the confirmation
- The confirmation scene — confirmed by author: approaching his
  father's house in Lagos, Abraham saw a crowd gathered; he knew
  immediately that something was wrong; approaching the gate, his
  uncle — Uncle Dejo — came to him; Abraham asked directly "is David
  dead?"; Uncle Dejo's response (paraphrased from Yoruba): "Triplets,
  strange children, he already told you"
- Abraham did not ask "what happened" or "is something wrong" — he
  arrived already knowing from the vision/pact what he was confirming,
  and asked the specific question; the family was not surprised that
  he already knew
- Uncle Dejo's response carries the family's existing belief: they
  had always believed something inherently special about Abraham as
  first of the triplets and as grandpa reincarnate; his knowing
  before being told confirmed what they already expected of him
- This connects to Chapter 3's recognition thread (the reincarnation
  belief David Senior carried) — the family's framework for Abraham's
  foreknowledge is not new to Chapter 14; it is an extension of
  what has already been established about how the family understood
  his birth and nature
- David had died 5 May 2003, in Kent
- Mundane-before-grief detail: FILLED — David as "jolly good fellow"
  — parties, girls, friends, well-dressed, truly kind; the fun-genes
  joke; opening material confirmed
- No legal records, court documents, or third-party accounts enter
  this factual spine

**Emotional centre:** Brotherhood as covenant — and the specific
strangeness of arriving at a confirmation you already hold. Abraham
did not travel to Lagos to find out. He traveled to confirm what
the vision had already told him. Standing at his father's gate,
seeing the crowd, asking Uncle Dejo directly "is David dead?" —
he was not asking a question he did not know the answer to. He was
asking for the world to catch up with what he already knew. Uncle
Dejo's response ("triplets, strange children, he already told you")
confirmed both the death and the mechanism: the family was not
surprised Abraham already knew. That is the chapter's emotional
ground — not the shock of discovery, but the weight of confirmation
that comes when the supernatural already knew and the world is just
now arranging its words to match.

**Theological pressure:** The trance/vision pattern appears again
here — Jumoke appeared in the kitchen on the day of her death; now
Abraham has a vision in which David is absent from Abraham's wedding,
and the pact becomes the key that unlocks the meaning. The dead do
not always speak plainly; they leave structures for the living to
interpret. The book handles this the way it handles all supernatural
material: narrate plainly. State what happened, what was seen, what
was inferred. The reader is not asked to adjudicate whether the
vision was "real." It is stated as the thing that happened. The pact
is what made the interpretation possible.

**Inheritance thread:** David's death removed the last peer-witness
of Abraham's formation in 2003 — fourteen years before the marriage
and the fatherhood fight ahead. That gap — fourteen years of
carrying the inheritance without its closest human witness — is the
chapter's inheritance thread. The reader who reaches Part Three
carrying Chapter 14 will understand something about Abraham that
the surrounding chapters cannot supply: that he has been the last
one who knew it from inside for a very long time.

**Fatherhood thread:** Absent in the direct sense — David died in
2003, years before Damisi. But the pact carries a structural echo
forward. Abraham will later face a legal and systemic pressure built
precisely on erasing his presence from his son's life. The man who

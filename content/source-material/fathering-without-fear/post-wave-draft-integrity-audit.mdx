## SECTION 6 — UNIVERSAL WRITING STANDARDS

Applied to all 18 chapters:

**Truthmode** — Abraham's blind spots, naivety, and delayed understanding are present in every chapter where the narrator could have flattered himself. Ch.12: "He saw enough to be troubled." Ch.16: "He stayed past warnings." Ch.20: "He had been careless." The memoir's authority depends on this and it is holding.

**Redteam** — The most dangerous moment in the collection is Ch.8's Iyalode chapter. The dual-spiritual frame could collapse into either exoticising traditional faith or treating conversion as correction. The draft holds both without resolving either. Redteam pass: ✓

**Hormozi (value density)** — The weakest chapter on this standard is Ch.13 (Serena). Too much explanatory narration in proportion to scene. The value-per-line ratio is the lowest in the collection. Needs second-pass reduction.

**Human** — The acceptance gate sentences across all chapters are written from inside human experience, not above it. Ch.19 in particular passes this at the highest level: the father who has to tell his son "you do not need to make Daddy happy" because the son already tries to is one of the most human sentences in the collection.

**/Futureyou** — The memoir's discipline holds: Abraham's future understanding is consistently withheld from moments where the younger Abraham did not have it. Ch.11 does not insert later knowledge into the Facebook exchange. Ch.12 does not prosecute from hindsight. Ch.16 explicitly names the restraint: "Abraham's later understanding must not be allowed to bully the past."

**Unlearn** — Ch.20 (Love Does Not Fear) is the strongest example: the verse is unchanged; the man reading it has been corrected by the cost of his earlier misreading. The book does not arrive at easy answers. The Iyalode chapters do not arrive at a tidy spiritual resolution. This standard is maintained.

**80/20** — The 20% producing 80% of this memoir's weight: Ch.6 (the bargain), Ch.8 (Iyalode's irreducibility), Ch.14 (David), Ch.17 (what the file cannot see), Ch.19 (Damisi), Ch.20 (love corrected). These six chapters are the book's spine. All six currently pass.

**Socrates** — The memoir asks but does not answer: What does a man inherit? What does faith mean when God preserves your life but not your comfort? What does a father owe a child when the system rewards disappearance? These questions are not resolved in the drafts. That is correct. Ch.20 makes the closest pass at an answer and immediately qualifies it: "That was as far as Abraham could go without lying."

**Lindymode** — The prose engine (flat clause, declarative, mundane-before-grief, faith carried by cost) is built for durability, not trend. These chapters will not read as dated in ten years. The legal-caution architecture means the book can go to print without becoming a liability document. This standard is maintained.

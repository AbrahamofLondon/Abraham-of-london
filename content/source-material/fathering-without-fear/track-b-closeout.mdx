## Standing Craft Rules (Active Across All Chapters)

1. **The dead must live before they die.** Jumoke, David, Funke, Iyalode, and David Senior must each be rendered with ordinary human detail before death or symbolism enters.
2. **Mundane-before-grief gate.** Every chapter that carries a death or institutional confrontation must ground the reader in ordinary texture first.
3. **No abstract openings.** Every chapter opens in a scene, object, sentence, place, date, gesture, or remembered physical fact.
4. **Faith must be carried by story.** Theology emerges through lived cost, not sermonising.
5. **The son must remain protected.** Damisi is not evidence. He is a child.
6. **The narrator must remain self-accountable.** Abraham's blind spots, naivety, and delayed understanding are part of the book's authority.
7. **Q5 governs Parts Three and Four absolutely.** "What does it cost to tell the truth without destroying the people still inside the story?" Every omission in Parts Three and Four is a moral choice, not a failure of honesty. Name the silence where the reader might feel cheated.

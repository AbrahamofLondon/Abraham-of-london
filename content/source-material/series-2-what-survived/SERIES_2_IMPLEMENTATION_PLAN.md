## PHASE 2 — PART WRITING SEQUENCE

**Writing begins:** 2026-10-06 (after source kit complete)
**First part deadline:** 2026-10-20 (2 weeks before launch for review)
**Cadence:** One part per week, written 2 weeks ahead of publication

### Writing Order

Parts must be written in order. Each part's closing hook references the next part, and the device sentence accumulates. Part 7 cannot be written until Parts 1–6 are complete because its closing depends on the accumulated weight of all preceding device sentences.

### Writing Standards Applied

Every part is written against:

1. **Narrative Discipline Standards** (from Series 1):
   - Begin in the scene. Always.
   - Do not state the argument. Demonstrate it and stop.
   - Remove section headers. Replace with transitions.
   - Show character through action, not biography.
   - Make one strong claim late.

2. **Universal Writing Standards**:
   - Truthmode — defensible from evidence
   - Redteam — tested against counter-arguments
   - Hormozi — every sentence earns its place
   - /Human — legible to a general reader
   - Futureyou — leaves the reader changed
   - Unlearn — challenges an assumption
   - 80/20 — weight on highest-impact moments
   - Socrates — proceeds by inquiry, not assertion
   - Lindymode — built to last beyond the moment

3. **Series 2 specific:**
   - One device sentence per part (the inventory sentence), placed in the body
   - No section headers — continuous prose with transition sentences
   - Escalating proximity: Parts 1–3 ancient/remote, Parts 4–5 closer, Parts 6–7 present
   - Part 7: second person, present tense (the formal shift)

### Quality Gates

Each part passes these checks before it is filed:

- [ ] Opening sentence puts the reader somewhere specific
- [ ] No section headers — continuous prose
- [ ] Device sentence present in the body, not opening or closing
- [ ] No declared thesis sentences in the first half
- [ ] Every character introduced through action, not biography
- [ ] Closing hook connects to the next part
- [ ] Word count within target range
- [ ] Read aloud — no sentence requires a second reading
- [ ] Truthmode check — every claim defensible
- [ ] Redteam check — strongest counter-argument addressed

## PHASE 6 — SOURCE KIT UPDATE INSTRUCTIONS

### Chauvet Cave — New Entry

**Required components:**
- The Fact: Discovery date, location, age, sophistication, sealed cave, lost context
- The Story: Three people in 1994, the torchlit animals that moved, the gap in the rock face
- The Payload: Claim 1 (Medium decides) primary, Claim 4 (Oral cultures) secondary
- The Emotional Register: Awe shading into epistemological vertigo — the discovery is also a loss
- The Hook: "The most sophisticated art in Europe for 35,000 years was made 36,000 years ago. We found it in 1994. We have no idea what it means."
- The Turn: The paintings are described as a discovery. What they are is a partial survival. The interpretive layer — the meaning-carrying context — survived nothing because it was made of breath and voice and memory, and those leave no mark.
- Format Fit: Long read, editorial, chapter opening
- The Question It Leaves: What are you mistaking the body for the person?

### Library of Alexandria — New Entry

**Required components:**
- The Fact: Multiple destruction events, contested history, 40,000–700,000 scrolls lost, no inventory survived
- The Story: The catalogue burned with the books. We know Aristotle wrote a second book of the Poetics because other writers refer to it. The book does not exist. We know it existed. We cannot read it.
- The Payload: Claim 1 (Medium decides) primary, Claim 4 (Oral cultures) secondary
- The Emotional Register: Vertiginous grief without sentimentality — the gap that cannot be measured from inside itself
- The Hook: "We do not know what was lost at Alexandria because the record of what was there did not survive either."
- The Turn: The Internet Archive estimates it has preserved less than a quarter of what has been created digitally. The digital Library of Alexandria is dissolving silently, incrementally, without witnesses.
- Format Fit: Long read, editorial
- The Question It Leaves: What are you creating now that will not exist in twenty years?

### Digital Fragility — New Entry

**Required components:**
- The Fact: URL lifespan ~2 years, Internet Archive ~20-25% coverage, 87% of classic video games out of print, platform closures (Geocities, MySpace, Vine, Google+)
- The Story: Second person, present tense. "You have made things that will not survive."
- The Payload: Claim 1 (Medium decides) primary, Claim 3 (Distribution controls history) secondary
- The Emotional Register: The cold recognition that the loss is already underway and the reader is inside it
- The Hook: "You have made things that will not survive. Not because you failed to preserve them — because the infrastructure you created them on was not yours to preserve, and it is already gone."
- The Turn: This is not a historical problem. It is your problem, now, in the present tense. The difference between you and everyone in this series is that you have been told.
- Format Fit: Long read, editorial, closing chapter
- The Question It Leaves: What will you do now that you know?

## PHASE 7 — RISK REGISTER

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Part 7 second-person shift feels unearned | Medium | High | All six preceding parts must build escalating proximity. Part 7 writer reads all parts before beginning. |
| Device sentence feels repetitive by Part 4 | Low | Medium | Device sentences escalate in specificity and proximity. Part 1 is about ancient paintings; Part 4 is about a photograph from 1937. The device is the same structure; the material is not. |
| Source kit updates not complete by writing deadline | Medium | High | Begin source kit updates immediately. Prioritise Chauvet and Alexandria (new entries) over updates to existing entries. |
| Outbound content not ready for launch | Medium | Medium | Outbound content can begin production after Part 2 is written. The 4-week LinkedIn lag provides buffer. |
| Series 1 audience does not carry over | Low | High | Series 2 is designed as a standalone argument. No prior knowledge of Series 1 required. The relationship is thematic, not sequential. |

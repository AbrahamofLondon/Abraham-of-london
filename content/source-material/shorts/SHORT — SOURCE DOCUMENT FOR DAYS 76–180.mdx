# SHORT — SOURCE DOCUMENT FOR DAYS 76–180

## Project Purpose

`short` is a daily 2–3 minute reflective writing series by Abraham of London.

It exists to say what people are thinking but rarely say aloud: the private contradictions, emotional fatigue, spiritual tension, relational disappointments, ambition traps, identity fractures, and moral evasions of ordinary life.

The tone must be frank, intelligent, emotionally precise, accessible, sometimes confrontational, sometimes tender, and never shallow.

The writing should feel secular-accessible but spiritually aware. It may reference God, faith, prayer, obedience, grace, and conviction where appropriate, but it must never sound like church pamphlet writing. It should feel like philosophy, therapy, moral clarity, and lived wisdom meeting in public.

The aim is not motivation. The aim is recognition, interruption, and return.

Readers should feel:

* “That is exactly what I’ve been feeling.”
* “I didn’t want to admit this.”
* “This is uncomfortable, but true.”
* “I need to come back tomorrow.”

## Core Writing Pattern

Each short should follow this general rhythm:

1. **Opening recognition**
   Start with a short, piercing observation. No long introductions.

2. **Naming the hidden pattern**
   Describe what is really happening beneath the surface.

3. **The uncomfortable truth**
   Include one clear, quotable line that confronts the reader.

4. **Concrete audit**
   Ask 2–4 practical questions or name specific symptoms.

5. **This week / tonight / today practice**
   Give one small action, not a vague inspirational instruction.

6. **Closing line**
   End with a strong sentence or short paragraph that lands emotionally.

## Style Rules

* Use short paragraphs.
* Use line breaks generously.
* Use bullets sparingly but effectively.
* Avoid academic explanations.
* Avoid cliché motivational language.
* Avoid excessive religious language.
* Avoid sounding like therapy Instagram.
* Avoid “you are enough” softness unless earned.
* Never be cruel.
* Never flatter the reader.
* Tell the truth with moral seriousness.

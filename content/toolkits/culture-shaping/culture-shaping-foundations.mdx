# CULTURE SHAPING — FOUNDATIONS

**(A+ Standard — Institutional Culture Design Model)**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Culture is not what leadership says it is.
Culture is what the system rewards — consistently, repeatedly, and without exception.

Most culture initiatives fail because they target **declared values** rather than **operating incentives**. This toolkit addresses the structural layer beneath behaviour: the reward systems, authority patterns, and accountability mechanisms that actually determine how people act.

It establishes:

```txt
Diagnosis of current operating culture
Design of target culture architecture
Embedding mechanisms that survive leadership change
Measurement systems that detect drift before it compounds
```

---

## 2. Strategic Context

Culture operates across three layers:

```txt
Declared Culture   — what leadership says (mission, values, principles)
Operating Culture  — what the system rewards (promotions, bonuses, status)
Deep Culture       — what is assumed (unwritten rules, taboos, sacred cows)
```

Failure occurs when these layers diverge. The most dangerous condition is **Declared Culture diverging from Operating Culture** — because leadership believes the message is working while the system proves otherwise.

Three illusions sustain this divergence:

```txt
1. Values posters = culture
2. Engagement surveys = truth
3. Leadership behaviour = cultural ceiling
```

In reality:

```txt
Values without enforcement are decoration
Surveys without consequence are theatre
Leadership behaviour without accountability is permission
```

## 3. Core Model — Culture Architecture

### 3.1 The Four Culture Dimensions

```txt
Authority Pattern    — who decides, how, and with what accountability
Reward Structure     — what behaviour actually gets promoted or paid
Information Flow     — what is known, by whom, and how it moves
Consequence System   — what happens when standards are violated
```

Every organisation has a culture defined by these four dimensions. Changing culture requires changing at least three of them simultaneously.

### 3.2 Culture States

```txt
ORDERED        — declared and operating culture align; standards enforced
DRIFTING       — declared culture intact; operating culture diverging
MISALIGNED     — declared and operating culture in active contradiction
DISORDERED     — no coherent culture; survival behaviour dominates
```

## 4. Diagnostic Signals

### Primary Signals

```txt
REWARD_MISALIGNMENT    — behaviour rewarded differs from behaviour stated
EXCEPTION_PATTERN      — high performers exempt from standards
INFORMATION_HOARDING   — critical knowledge held by individuals, not systems
CONSEQUENCE_VACUUM     — standards violated without response
```

### Secondary Signals

```txt
MEETING_THEATRE        — high meeting volume, low decision output
BLAME_DISPERSAL        — failures attributed to system, not owners
HERO_DEPENDENCE        — specific individuals irreplaceable
CYNICISM_GROWTH        — stated values met with visible scepticism
```

---

## 5. Failure Modes

```txt
1. Values theatre — stated values are aspirational, not operational. No one is held accountable for violating them.

2. Founder shadow — culture is dependent on a single individual's presence and cannot survive their absence.

3. Scale fracture — culture that worked at 20 people breaks at 200 because informal enforcement no longer reaches everyone.

4. Acquisition contamination — acquired entities bring incompatible operating norms that erode the host culture.

5. Performance exception — high performers are permitted to operate outside cultural standards, signalling that standards are optional.

6. Silence as consent — leadership tolerates behaviour they claim to oppose, signalling that opposition is rhetorical.
```

## 6. Application Playbook

### Step 1 — Diagnose Operating Culture

Map the four culture dimensions as they currently operate, not as declared.

**Deliverable:** Operating Culture Map

### Step 2 — Identify Divergence Points

Compare declared culture against operating culture. Name specific contradictions.

**Deliverable:** Culture Divergence Report

### Step 3 — Design Target Architecture

Define the desired state for each of the four dimensions. Ensure they are internally consistent.

**Deliverable:** Target Culture Blueprint

### Step 4 — Align Reward Systems

Audit promotion, bonus, status, and recognition mechanisms. Remove rewards for behaviour that contradicts target culture.

**Deliverable:** Reward Alignment Plan

### Step 5 — Install Consequence Systems

Define what happens when cultural standards are violated. Apply consistently, starting with leadership.

**Deliverable:** Consequence Protocol

### Step 6 — Embed Measurement

Install drift detection: quarterly culture signal scan, annual full diagnostic.

**Deliverable:** Culture Monitoring Cadence

## 7. Board-Level Questions

```txt
What behaviour is actually rewarded in this organisation — not what do we say is rewarded?

Which individuals are exempt from our cultural standards, and why?

If our founder left tomorrow, what would the culture become in 6 months?

What is the one behaviour we tolerate that we claim to oppose?

When was the last time someone was held accountable for a cultural violation — and was it a senior person?
```

---

## 8. Metrics and Measurement

```txt
Culture Integrity Score   — alignment between declared and operating culture (0-100)
Consequence Consistency   — % of violations that receive proportional response
Reward Alignment Index    — % of promotions that reflect stated values
Exception Count           — number of individuals operating outside standards
```

## 9. Case Application (Modelled)

### Situation

Scaling organisation (80→200 people) experiencing behaviour degradation. Leadership believes values are clear. New hires are not absorbing them.

### Signals Detected

```txt
REWARD_MISALIGNMENT — aggressive individual contributors promoted over collaborative ones
CONSEQUENCE_VACUUM — senior leader known for bullying behaviour not addressed
HERO_DEPENDENCE — three individuals hold critical institutional knowledge
```

### Classification

```txt
DRIFTING — declared culture intact, operating culture diverging
```

### Intervention

- Promoted collaborative behaviours in next review cycle
- Addressed bullying behaviour with formal consequence
- Documented critical knowledge into operating systems

### Outcome (90 days)

```txt
Culture Integrity Score ↑ from 52 to 71
Voluntary turnover ↓ 22%
New hire integration time ↓ 35%
```

## 12. Final Position

Culture is not what you say. It is what you tolerate.

If you tolerate exception, you have encoded exception as standard.

If you tolerate silence, you have encoded silence as consent.

This toolkit removes the gap between declared and operating culture — not by changing what you say, but by changing what the system rewards.

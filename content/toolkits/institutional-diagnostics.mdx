# INSTITUTIONAL DIAGNOSTICS TOOLKIT

## 1. Executive Summary

Most institutions do not fail because effort disappears.
They fail because contradiction is tolerated until it becomes structural.

This toolkit explains what the platform examines across culture, governance, systems, leadership alignment, crisis readiness, and growth capacity.
It does not disclose the operating model behind the diagnosis.

Public outputs are expressed as institutional states:

```txt
ORDERED
DRIFTING
MISALIGNED
DISORDERED
```

Each state carries:

* a clear structural reading
* a practical directive
* an intervention path proportionate to the condition

---

## 2. Strategic Context

Every institution operates across four layers:

```txt
Declared intent
Operational reality
Structural design
Human behaviour
```

Breakdown begins when those layers stop reinforcing one another.

Leadership teams usually monitor performance.
Far fewer can name:

```txt
where authority is leaking
where contradiction is compounding
where execution no longer reflects declared intent
where growth is revealing hidden weakness
```

The toolkit exists to make those conditions visible early enough to act.

---

## 3. What The System Evaluates

The platform examines six domains:

```txt
1. Culture
2. Governance
3. Systems
4. Leadership alignment
5. Crisis readiness
6. Growth strain
```

Each domain is interpreted through a proprietary evaluation model.
The public layer shows the state, the explanation, and the next move.
The private layer retains the internal calibration, routing, and decision policy.

---

## 4. Public Reading Model

### ORDERED

The institution still exhibits coherence between intent, authority, and execution.
Correction is light-touch and preventive.

### DRIFTING

Early divergence is present.
The system can still be corrected before the condition hardens.

### MISALIGNED

Contradiction is no longer isolated.
Leadership, governance, or execution are pulling in different directions.

### DISORDERED

Breakdown is distributed across the institution.
Containment and structural intervention become urgent.

---

## 5. Domain Interpretation

### Culture

The question is whether real behaviour matches declared values.
If standards exist only in language, the institution is already paying for it.

### Governance

The question is whether decision authority is clear, durable, and enforceable.
If authority is assumed rather than held, delay and shadow control follow.

### Systems

The question is whether execution holds under pressure or depends on workarounds.
If informal fixes become normal, fragility is already embedded.

### Leadership Alignment

The question is whether senior actors operate from the same reality.
If alignment is performative rather than real, decisions degrade before results do.

### Crisis Readiness

The question is whether the institution can stay coherent when conditions tighten.
If pressure changes who decides, the institution was never stable.

### Growth Strain

The question is whether scale strengthens the system or exposes its contradictions.
If growth amplifies confusion, the constraint is structural rather than commercial.

---

## 6. Intervention Logic

The public process is simple:

```txt
Diagnose the condition
Name the structural pressure
Determine the directive
Escalate only when the condition justifies it
```

The private system uses internally governed scoring, routing, and narrative assembly.
Those mechanics are intentionally non-public.

---

## 7. Board-Level Questions

```txt
Where are we tolerating contradiction?
Who truly holds final authority?
What fails when pressure rises?
Where is growth exposing weakness instead of strength?
What are we calling stable that is only temporarily quiet?
```

If these questions cannot be answered plainly, the condition is already material.

---

## 8. What Good Evidence Looks Like

Strong diagnostic evidence usually includes:

* repeated decision delay
* recurring execution friction
* mismatched leadership narratives
* authority confusion during live pressure
* operational patterns that contradict stated priorities

The public toolkit names these evidence categories.
The non-public decision model governs how they are interpreted together.

---

## 9. Modelled Outcome

A mid-sized institution can appear commercially healthy while internally drifting.
After governed analysis, the useful outcome is not a public formula.
It is a directive such as:

```txt
clarify decision authority
remove duplicated approval paths
stabilise leadership interpretation
contain execution drag before scaling further
```

That is what the user should leave with: clarity, sequence, and action.

## 10. Product Integration

```txt
Diagnose  -> entry assessment

```

## 11. Final Position

This toolkit is public doctrine, not a reproducible worksheet.

It explains:

```txt
what is being examined
why it matters
what state the institution is in
what action follows
```

It does not expose the private evaluation model that produces the final governed decision.

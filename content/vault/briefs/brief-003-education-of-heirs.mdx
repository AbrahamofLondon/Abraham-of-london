### The Three Fractures of Educational Decay

#### 1. The Institutional Transfer
When a [Father](/lexicon/father) outsources the formation of his children to the state or a corporation, he is surrendering his **[Sovereignty](/lexicon/sovereignty)**. He allows a hostile narrative to write the "Source Code" of his heirs. By the time they inherit the [Ledger](/lexicon/legacy), they no longer share the [Lore](/lexicon/legacy).

#### 2. The Weightless Gap
Modern education delays **[Responsibility](/lexicon/responsibility)** until the mid-twenties. This creates a "fragility gap" where heirs have knowledge but no [Integrity](/lexicon/integrity) under pressure. A Canon heir is given a "weight-bearing" role in the [Oikos](/lexicon/family) as soon as they reach maturity.

#### 3. The Narrative Voids
Without the **[Sisterhood](/lexicon/sisterhood)** weaving the history and culture of the family, children become "Cultural Orphans." They look to the algorithm for their identity, becoming [Fragmented Consumers](/lexicon/family) rather than generational builders.



---

### The Strategic Pivot: The Formation Protocol

To transition your heirs from **Decay** to **Rise**, we implement the **Three Prongs of Mastery**:

1. **Repatriation of the Lore**: The family history, mission, and [Covenant](/lexicon/covenant) must be the primary curriculum. The heir must know "Who we are" before they learn "What the world thinks."
2. **Economic Apprenticeship**: Heirs must manage small portions of the [Economic Fortress](/vault/briefs/brief-002-economic-fortress) early. Wisdom in handling the [Ledger](/lexicon/legacy) is a prerequisite for total inheritance.
3. **The Rite of Passage**: A clear, high-friction transition from "Child" to "Steward." This requires a public commitment to the [Standard](/lexicon/integrity) of the house.

---

### Intelligence Conclusion

You cannot build a 100-year [Legacy](/lexicon/legacy) with children who have been trained to seek "permission" and "safety." The education of an heir is a combat mission; it is the engineering of the strength required to carry the weight of the family's future.

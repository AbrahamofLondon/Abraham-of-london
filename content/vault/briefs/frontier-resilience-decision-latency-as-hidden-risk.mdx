<BriefAlert variant="strategy" icon="⚠️">
**Frontier Rule**: The cost of slow judgment rarely appears on the first delay. It compounds silently across handoffs, trust, and missed sequence until the institution realizes it has been paying for hesitation all along.
</BriefAlert>

## I. The Pressure Pattern

Some risks arrive as shocks. Others arrive as accumulated delay. Decision latency belongs to the second category. A choice takes too long, then another does, and eventually the institution is governed less by strategy than by the drag of its own unresolved questions.

## II. What This Pattern Actually Does

Latency creates cost in multiple layers. Work stalls. Teams hedge. Local initiative shrinks. Market windows narrow. And people learn that upward judgment is slow enough to route around or stop expecting altogether.

## III. Diagnostic Lens

The practical lens is straightforward: identify which decisions routinely exceed their natural window of usefulness. Those are the ones generating hidden risk. The problem is not merely time. It is the mismatch between decision speed and consequence horizon.

## IV. Strategic Implication

Resilient institutions reduce latency by clarifying who decides, what must be reviewed, and what can move locally without ceremonial permission. They know that speed without law is chaos, but law without speed becomes its own failure mode.

## V. Closing Judgment

Frontier resilience depends not only on good decisions but on decisions made while they can still shape reality. When latency becomes normal, fragility has already entered the operating model.


## What Failure Looks Like

Decision Latency as Hidden Risk becomes a recurring pressure pattern rather than an exception requiring command attention.

## Primary Fracture

The institution lacks a governed response before pressure changes behaviour, sequencing, and truth flow.

## Pressure Indicators

- Pressure changes who owns the decision.
- Signals arrive late or lose force before reaching authority.
- Recovery depends on effort rather than designed cadence.

## Implementation Horizon

14 days to name the pattern; 60 days to assign authority and cadence; 6 months to test whether the correction holds under renewed pressure.

# Governing Principles

## 1\. Clarity before atmosphere

Premium does not mean dim, murky, gold-tinted, or overly cinematic.

Premium means:

* strong hierarchy
* excellent reading comfort
* disciplined spacing
* quiet confidence
* restrained motion
* stable surfaces

If a user strains to read a heading, subtitle, metadata line, or body paragraph, the design has failed.

## 2\. One system, not one giant component

The target is not one component to rule them all.

The target is:

* one foundation system
* one semantic token layer
* one family of primitives
* thin surface-specific compositions

A giant universal component becomes a switch statement with branding. Avoid it.

## 3\. Surface identity must be real but controlled

Canon, Vault, Essays, Shorts, Books, Inner Circle, and related surfaces must feel distinct, but the distinction must come from governed surface contracts, not page-level improvisation.

Each surface should vary by:

* tone
* density
* imagery ratio
* metadata emphasis
* hero treatment
* reader rhythm
* semantic token overrides

Not by arbitrary per-page styling.

## 4\. Semantic tokens over raw styling

Raw values belong in the foundation layer.

Page and component code should primarily consume semantic meaning such as:

* background
* panel
* text
* text-muted
* border
* accent
* hero-wash
* hero-scrim

Surface-level overrides should override semantic tokens, not raw color literals.

## 5\. Gradients are decorative, not structural

Gradients may support mood, but they must never carry primary reading responsibility.

Text must sit on a stable reading plane. If text overlays a gradient, a scrim or stabilized luminance zone is required.

No dense body copy may sit directly on decorative uncertainty.

## 6\. Contrast is a product requirement, not an aesthetic preference

Minimum targets:

* body text: at least 7:1
* secondary text: at least 4.5:1
* interactive text: at least 4.5:1
* hero headings: at least 4.5:1 against effective rendered background

No meaningful text may be styled into faintness for mood.

## 7\. Migration must be parallel and reversible

Do not delete old systems first.

New foundations and primitives should be built in parallel and proven on pilot surfaces before legacy removal begins.

Every migration step should preserve rollback safety.

## 8\. Home page is not the first proving ground

The home page is the most visible and easiest place to create impressive-looking mistakes.

Migrate it after the rules are proven on:

* Canon
* Vault

## 9\. Editorial reading quality outranks visual ornament

Typography is not a font-size table. It is a reading system.

The system must define:

* line length
* line height
* paragraph spacing
* heading rhythm
* quote treatment
* callout treatment
* metadata hierarchy
* caption styling
* code/pre formatting
* link behavior

## 10\. Performance is part of the design system

Blur, gradients, image treatments, motion, overlays, and smart covers must operate within budget.

A luxury surface that is slow, unstable, or janky is not premium.

## 11\. Governance must outlive the migration

The system is not complete unless it is enforceable.

That means:

* linting or policy against raw color drift
* visual checks on pilot surfaces
* contrast review discipline
* explicit ban list for unsafe patterns

## 12\. The benchmark

The benchmark is not “looks expensive in screenshots.”

The benchmark is that the platform feels calm, authoritative, readable, and coherent for sustained use.

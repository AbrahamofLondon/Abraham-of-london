# Minimum Sellable Corridor

Primary buyer: COO / operating founder of a B2B services, manufacturing, or regulated-growth company facing one costly delayed decision with named stakeholders and a consequence window under 90 days.

Why this wedge: high decision cost, clear owner, governance pressure, need for continuity, and a controlled fulfilment path that does not require selling the whole estate.

Commercial sequence:

1. Trigger: delayed material decision with cost of inaction.
Product: Decision Signal.
Time to value: 2 minutes.
Result: pressure band, contradiction/evidence gap, next admissible move.
Access mode: free.
Price: free.
Fulfilment: automated engine.
Evidence created: input-sensitive reading and recommendation ID.
Next move: Corridor.

2. Trigger: buyer asks "what should we do next?"
Product: Corridor.
Time to value: immediate after Signal.
Result: established facts, unresolved blockers, one admissible action.
Access mode: free/controlled/manual/self-serve according to recommendation.
Price: canonical commercial resolver where applicable.
Fulfilment: persisted recommendation context.
Evidence created: action intent and continuation event.
Next move: Operator Pilot if material and controlled.

3. Trigger: live decision qualifies for governed intervention.
Product: Operator Pilot.
Time to value: same-day intake, operator review SLA target 2 business days.
Result: qualification state, requested information or acceptance/decline/scoping.
Access mode: controlled.
Price: manual proposal; no checkout inference.
Fulfilment: admin queue + human authority.
Evidence created: intake, status transitions, acceptance/decline reasons, checkpoints.
Next move: scoped commercial continuation.

4. Trigger: pilot accepted and scope defined.
Product: controlled commercial continuation: Executive Reporting / Strategy Room / retained oversight depending on evidence.
Time to value: scoped.
Result: governed decision record, checkpoint, outcome review.
Access mode: manual billing / controlled.
Price: canonical/manual quote only after scope.
Fulfilment: existing fulfilment contracts and admin operation.
Evidence created: longitudinal decision history and outcome checkpoints.

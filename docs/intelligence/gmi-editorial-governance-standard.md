**Purpose:** Make market judgement inspectable over time. GMI does not compete by having the most data; it competes by maintaining a governed record of what it said, what happened, what changed, and what must be decided next.

---

## 0. Governing Proposition

> **GMI makes market judgement inspectable over time: it scores prior material calls, states what would prove current theses wrong, distinguishes evidence from judgement, and converts market signals into owned, time-bound decisions.**

The immediate wedge is **accountable judgement**. The moat is the accumulated governed longitudinal record: timestamped original calls, immutable wording, original confidence, evidence state at issuance, review windows, falsification criteria, subsequent evidence, scored outcomes, learning, weighting changes, version history, and unresolved-call inventory.

Do not claim that nobody else scores its work or that GMI is the only scorecard in the market. The defensible public claim is:

> **GMI makes recurring call-by-call scoring, falsification conditions, and revision discipline part of the quarterly product itself.**

## 2. Edition Invariants

Every edition must contain, in substance:

1. Prior-edition material-call review: original wording, original confidence, review window, score, evidence, learning, and next action.
2. Current-edition material calls: immutable ID, exact statement, confidence, evidence posture, expected review window, falsification condition, and next review date.
3. Falsification register: variable + threshold + duration + source + consequence where analytically supportable; qualitative adjudication rule where numerical thresholds would be fake precision.
4. Decision Integrity Index (DII): process-integrity score, not forecast hit rate.
5. Cumulative track-record record: denominator disclosed; unresolved and too-early calls excluded from success denominator.
6. Governed actions: decision, reason now, owner, activation trigger, deadline, success metric, review date.
7. Source appendix: hard numbers have source, source date, observation period, evidence class, as-of date, and confidence posture.
8. Not-investment-advice boundary visible in the product and matched by behaviour.

---

## 3. No Disappearing Calls

Every material call entered into an edition must subsequently be one of:

1. resolved and scored;
2. explicitly carried forward with a next review date;
3. superseded with a stated reason; or
4. withdrawn with a stated reason.

A material call may never disappear from the longitudinal record. A future edition that omits a prior material call without one of these statuses fails market-readiness.

---

## 4. Immutable Originals

Once an edition is published:

- original call wording cannot be silently edited;
- original confidence cannot be silently changed;
- original falsification conditions cannot be retrospectively relaxed;
- original review window cannot be moved without an amendment record.

Corrections are additive and visible. The original remains inspectable.

---

## 5. Amendment Discipline

Every post-publication factual correction must record:

| Field | Required |
|---|---|
| edition | yes |
| affected claim or call ID | yes |
| original text | yes |
| corrected text | yes |
| reason | yes |
| date | yes |
| affects score/thesis/action/DII | yes, explicit |

No silent historical rewriting. If a correction affects a score, thesis, action, or DII component, the amendment must state the retest required.

---

## 6. Anti-Denominator Gaming

Track-record calculations must disclose:

- calls issued;
- calls resolved;
- calls unresolved;
- calls carried forward;
- too-early-to-assess count;
- weakly supported and disconfirmed calls;
- withdrawn and superseded calls;
- scoring methodology version.

Rules:

- Do not publish a single success percentage without its denominator.
- Do not count unresolved calls as successes.
- Do not count score-2 too-early calls as successes.
- Keep withdrawn/superseded calls visible with reason.
- Attach every aggregate to a methodology version and as-of date.

## 7. DII v1.0 — Decision Integrity Index

DII measures the integrity of the intelligence process. It is not a disguised forecasting hit-rate.

**DII v1.0 is frozen for the first three editions in which it is used.** During that period there are no dimension changes, weight changes, band changes, or retrospective score rewrites. Any later methodology becomes DII v2.0 or later and applies prospectively only. Historical scores remain attached to their original methodology version.

| Function | Final DII v1.0 dimension | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Evidence integrity | Evidence integrity | 20 | Source quality, evidence class clarity, hard-number traceability |
| Falsifiability and trigger precision | Falsifiability and trigger precision | 20 | Whether theses can be disproved by observable triggers |
| Calibration and accountability discipline | Calibration and accountability discipline | 20 | Prior-call review, scoring honesty, denominator discipline |
| Decision actionability | Decision actionability | 20 | Owner, trigger, deadline, success metric, review date |
| Revision and uncertainty discipline | Revision and uncertainty discipline | 20 | Uncertainty labelling, amendment discipline, carried-forward calls |

**Bands:**

- 85-100: Strong decision integrity
- 70-84: Sound, with material uncertainties
- 55-69: Conditional; elevated integrity risk
- Below 55: Not publication-grade

The paid report must show a compact DII evidence table: dimension, weight, score, evidence basis, key weakness/deduction, methodology version.

---

## 8. Edition Doctrine Record

Every edition must define one named regime doctrine. The doctrine record must state:

- thesis;
- supporting evidence;
- counter-evidence;
- material uncertainties;
- falsification conditions;
- decision implications;
- review window.

The doctrine is an accountable analytical record, not branding copy.

---

## 9. Public/Paid Track-Record Architecture

The public assurance surface may show aggregate accountability: edition count, calls issued, resolved/unresolved counts, carried-forward count, scoring methodology, and as-of date.

The paid product retains richer detail: full call evidence, review notes, falsification register, methodology implications, and board action logic.

The cumulative track-record banner is mandatory from Edition 3 onward.

---

## 10. Release Gate

Market readiness fails closed when any structural invariant fails:

- all prior material calls accounted for;
- no disappearing call IDs;
- all new material calls registered;
- resolved calls scored using valid rubric values;
- carried-forward calls have next review dates;
- unresolved/too-early calls excluded from success denominator;
- DII methodology version present;
- DII component scores reconcile to headline score;
- cumulative track-record math reconciles;
- falsification register present;
- actions contain required fields;
- source appendix clears blockers;
- hard numbers have source or method treatment;
- scenario probabilities reconcile;
- not-investment-advice boundary present;
- paid and public editions materially differ;
- lifecycle state is correct;
- Q-next record exists;
- quality threshold passes;
- render and access route work as intended.

Automate structural invariants. Require human sign-off for analytical judgement.

## 1. Working thesis

**Provisional:** The Q2 2026 operating environment is defined by the persistence of fragmentation as the dominant structural condition, with a secondary question around whether financial stress begins to compound the trade/tariff shock that drove Q1 market repricing.

The Q1 thesis — that markets are pricing survivability within fragmentation rather than growth within globalisation — holds into Q2. The key question for Q2 is whether this represents an extended repricing of risk premia, or whether a second-order stress cycle begins in credit, FX, or emerging market exposure.

**What would change this:**
- Material tariff de-escalation below ~50% effective rate on US-China
- Coordinated G7 policy response that substantively stabilises inflation expectations
- Systemic credit event that forces non-linear scenario reassessment

## 11. What would change the Q1 view

The Q1 thesis remains operative unless:

| Trigger | Implication |
|---|---|
| Tariff effective rate falls below ~50% | De-escalation becomes the operative scenario; upside risk for equities and trade-exposed sectors |
| US credit spread widening exceeds Q1 levels materially | Escalation spiral moves to higher probability; financial stress becomes primary framing |
| Fed emergency action or pivot signal | Q2 monetary environment repriced; rate-sensitive sectors reframe |
| China stimulus exceeds $1trn equivalent | Domestic demand offset may stabilise commodity demand and reduce EM stress |
| Dollar reserve status questioned in observable market pricing | Confidence fracture scenario moves to primary, not tail |
| Geopolitical de-escalation in secondary theatres | Political risk premium reduces; capital flow to EM recovers |

# Market Intelligence Board Summary Standard

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Effective from:** Q2 2026

Every paid institutional edition must include a one-page Board Summary as section 2, immediately before the Executive Summary. It must be self-contained — usable as a standalone briefing extract without reference to the rest of the report.

---

## Required structure

The Board Summary must contain all ten of the following elements in order. Omitting any element causes `BOARD_USABILITY` to score below the release threshold.

---

### 1. Core thesis

One paragraph. No hedging. States the structural position of the quarter in plain language readable at board level.

> **Q1 2026:** Markets are no longer pricing growth within globalisation. They are pricing survivability within fragmentation. Operators who continue to plan around pre-2025 supply chain, capital allocation, and monetary assumptions are taking structural risk disguised as continuity.

---

### 2. Current decision window

States the quarter this report is operative for, not the quarter it covers.

> **Current decision window:** Q2 2026. This report covers Q1 2026 conditions and remains active for Q2 operating decisions until superseded by the Q2 2026 report.

---

### 3. Five non-optional operating decisions

Numbered. Action-oriented. Each begins with a verb. Written at board level, not management level. These are not suggestions — they are the minimum operating responses to the conditions described.

> **Q1 2026 non-optional decisions:**
>
> 1. Map tariff exposure at supplier-node level, not country level. Country-level analysis understates exposure where goods cross multiple borders before assembly.
> 2. Shift supply chain strategy from optimisation to optionality. Resilience is now a capital markets variable.
> 3. Review capital allocation posture under elevated monetary constraint. Assumptions built before Q1 2026 tariff escalation may no longer hold.
> 4. Assess revenue model durability under prolonged trade friction. Identify which revenue lines are structurally exposed to tariff transmission.
> 5. Assign a board-level owner to geopolitical scenario monitoring. This is no longer a management discretionary task.

---

### 4. Three watch signals

Specific, observable, time-bound. These are the signals that would trigger a material update to the report's view. Each includes what to watch, why it matters, and what it would indicate.

> **Watch signals — Q2 2026:**
>
> 1. **Dollar risk-off behaviour:** Continued dollar weakness during equity market stress would signal reserve currency credibility stress rather than normal safe-haven dynamics reversal.
> 2. **Tariff trajectory:** Any reduction in effective US tariff rate below ~50% on China would require reassessment of the base case toward de-escalation.
> 3. **Credit spread widening:** Material widening in IG and HY credit spreads beyond Q1 levels would indicate that financial stress is beginning to compound the trade shock — raising the probability of the Escalation Spiral scenario.

---

### 5. Base-case scenario

Names the operative scenario, states its probability, and gives a one-sentence description.

> **Base case:** Managed Fragmentation — 43% probability. Elevated tariffs persist, supply chain restructuring proceeds slowly, monetary constraint is extended, and markets continue repricing to a new normal over multiple quarters.

---

### 6. Biggest downside scenario

Names the tail risk, states its probability, and gives a one-sentence description of what it would require to materialise.

> **Tail risk:** Confidence Fracture — 12% probability. A non-linear systemic event — dollar reserve status questioned in observable market pricing, coordinated institutional response required. Would require a significant secondary shock beyond the current tariff regime.

---

### 7. What would change this view

A bullet list of specific triggers that would cause the structural thesis or base-case scenario to require material revision.

> **Triggers that would change this view:**
>
> - Tariff effective rate falls below ~50% on a sustained bilateral basis (de-escalation becomes operative)
> - US credit spread widening exceeds Q1 levels materially (escalation spiral probability increases)
> - Fed pivot signal or emergency action (Q2 monetary environment repriced)
> - Dollar reserve status question emerges in observable institutional selling of US Treasuries
> - China domestic stimulus exceeds ~$1 trillion equivalent (EM stress and commodity demand framing changes)

---

### 8. Board-level instruction

One sentence. The single most important action the board should take in the current decision window. Distinct from the five operating decisions — this is the governing instruction.

> **Board instruction:** Treat Q2 2026 as a structural optionality-building quarter, not an execution quarter. Decisions that foreclose strategic flexibility are high-cost in this environment.

---

### 9. Confidence posture summary

Three lines. States which elements of this report are HIGH, MEDIUM, and MONITORING confidence. No more — the detailed posture is in the body of the report.

> **Confidence posture:**
> — HIGH: Structural fragmentation thesis; observed tariff escalation; market repricing direction.
> — MEDIUM: Inflation pass-through range; recession probability clustering; capital-flow interpretation.
> — MONITORING: Dollar weakness under risk-off; policy de-escalation pathway; confidence fracture risk.

---

### 10. Not investment advice boundary

One line. Must appear at the bottom of the Board Summary. Must use the exact phrase or a direct equivalent.

> This report is decision-support intelligence. It is not investment advice. All decisions remain the responsibility of the reader's organisation and its advisers.

---

## Formatting requirements

- The Board Summary must fit on one page (A4 or letter) when typeset at 10–11pt in the institutional PDF edition.
- Section headers must be bold.
- Numbered lists must be used for the five decisions.
- Bullet lists must be used for watch signals and view-change triggers.
- The confidence posture and disclaimer must be typographically subordinated (smaller or lighter) to signal their supporting role.

---

## Board Summary and the paid/public separation

The Board Summary is a paid-edition element. The public surface may include the core thesis and base-case scenario but must not reproduce the five non-optional decisions, watch signals, or board-level instruction verbatim. These are part of the institutional product value.

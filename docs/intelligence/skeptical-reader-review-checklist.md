# Skeptical Reader Review Checklist

**See also:** `docs/intelligence/market-intelligence-release-standard.md` · `docs/intelligence/gmi-buyer-assurance-pack.md` · `lib/intelligence/gmi-release-candidate-checklist.ts`

This checklist is used before a quarterly Market Intelligence report is moved from draft preparation into publication review.

1. Does every hard number have a source or method note?
2. Are scenario probabilities labelled as assumptions unless measured?
3. Are regional claims classified as confirmed, directional, or monitored?
4. Are FX and credit claims tied to observation windows?
5. Is there a clear "what would prove us wrong" section?
6. Are internal governance labels hidden from the external-facing body?
7. Does the report avoid sounding more certain than the evidence allows?
8. Is the paid edition still commercially strong after tightening?
9. Does the report remain useful without pretending to forecast?
10. Would a skeptical institutional reader know what is evidence and what is judgment?

## Release Discipline

- A strong thesis is allowed. Unsupported certainty is not.
- Draft reports may contain source-pending rows, but active releases may not.
- Scenario probabilities must show their method basis.
- Regional opportunity claims must remain directional or monitored until investment, trade, or project-flow evidence supports stronger classification.
- FX and credit stress claims must include observation windows and must not be converted into regime claims without repeated supporting evidence.

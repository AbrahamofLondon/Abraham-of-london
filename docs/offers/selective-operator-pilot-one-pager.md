# Selective Operator Pilot - Decision Infrastructure Trial

Status: ACTIVE_OFFER_DRAFT  
Commercial posture: selective operator outreach only

## Offer

Bring one decision your organisation cannot afford to keep circling. The system will test it, identify the contradiction, issue a required move, schedule accountability, and show what changes.

## Who It Is For

- operators carrying a live decision under pressure
- founders or executives with real authority, deadline, and consequence
- teams stuck in repetition, drift, or unresolved escalation

This is not for:

- casual exploration
- generic ideation
- buyers looking for a software tour
- organisations unwilling to submit a real decision

## What They Submit

One real decision with real stakes.

Typically:

- the exact decision being delayed
- the constraint environment around it
- relevant authority and ownership context
- optional supporting evidence if they have it

## What The System Returns

- a governed finding
- the contradiction or exposure holding the decision in place
- a required move
- a checkpoint or accountability condition
- a next-step architecture where escalation is earned

## What Gets Remembered

- the submitted decision record
- the result and contradiction
- the required move
- checkpoint state
- relevant follow-up surfaces such as Return Brief, counsel, proof, or retained oversight where justified

## What Follow-Up Happens

The system does not stop at the first output.

Follow-up may include:

- Decision Centre continuity
- Executive Reporting
- Strategy Room
- Return Brief if the condition remains unresolved
- proof and evidence surfaces where outcomes are later verified

## What Is Not Promised

- no promise of instant transformation
- no promise that every decision will be cleared to proceed
- no promise of automated oversight where cadence is manual
- no promise of verified improvement without verification
- no promise of general £50k retained readiness

## What Success Looks Like After 14-30 Days

- one real decision has been tested under evidence, authority, and consequence
- the contradiction has been named clearly
- the required move has been issued
- accountability has been scheduled
- the organisation can see whether anything actually changed
- the buyer can judge whether governed continuity is valuable enough to retain

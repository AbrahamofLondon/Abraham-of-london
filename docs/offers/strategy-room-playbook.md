# Strategy Room Playbook

Status: PARTIAL
Live surface truth: Strategy Room exists as a live surface, but this playbook is an operator/design standard, not proof that the full delivery model is implemented end-to-end.

## Objective

Make Strategy Room a decision-grade intervention rather than a branded call.

## Session Standard

The room exists to force disciplined movement under constraint.

It is not:

- coaching
- brainstorming
- therapeutic processing
- prestige theater

## Five-Phase Session Model

### 1. Context Collapse

Compress the situation to one real decision.

Outputs:

- exact decision statement
- relevant stakeholders
- decision deadline

### 2. Constraint Exposure

Force reality into the room.

Expose:

- money constraints
- time constraints
- political constraints
- legal or regulatory constraints
- execution capacity limits

### 3. Strategic Truth Articulation

Name what is actually true even if it is unwelcome.

This includes:

- what the buyer is avoiding
- which options are performative rather than real
- which trade-off is already being paid implicitly

### 4. Intervention Mapping

Map the viable options only.

For each option define:

- upside
- downside
- reversibility
- exposure
- owner

### 5. Decision Forcing

The room ends with:

- a decision
- or a disciplined deferral with explicit criteria and deadline

No open-ended philosophical close is allowed.

## Operator Rule

If the real authority-holder is absent, the room should not pretend it can complete the decision.

## Output Artifacts

Every Strategy Room should produce:

- decision memo
- trade-off map
- execution owner list
- cadence checkpoints
- risk watchpoints

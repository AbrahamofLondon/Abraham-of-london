# Memory Overclaim Risk Register

## High Risk

### Decision Centre `Case memory`

- Risk: user may infer robust case-specific governed memory
- Actual state: merged inherited evidence without strong case-level dating or lineage
- Why risky: premium users may assume the system knows what is current, superseded, or verified when it may only know what was previously reported
- Recommended action: add source label, date, and case-specific lineage before stronger market claims

### Strategy Room session `Unresolved execution memory`

- Risk: `unresolved` implies active downstream validation
- Actual state: usually field-presence-driven inherited memory from persisted canonical snapshot
- Why risky: unresolved can sound stronger than carried-forward
- Recommended action: add freshness/supersession rules and explicit source phrase

## Medium Risk

### Executive Reporting `Evidence carried forward`

- Risk: report may sound more institutionally grounded than the visible provenance supports
- Actual state: inherited prior evidence, often self-reported, sometimes merged from ladder context
- Recommended action: source-label as inherited prior governance evidence

### Strategy Room entry `Execution memory`

- Risk: carries authority through execution prep but without visible evidence-strength distinction
- Actual state: mixed inherited evidence from thread, executive result, and canonical payload
- Recommended action: mark as inherited unresolved constraints from prior assessment history

## Low Risk

### Return Brief `Against your prior standard`

- Risk: low
- Actual state: proportionate language and server-side comparison against later state
- Remaining issue: no visible date/source label

### Oversight Brief `Continuity source`

- Risk: low to medium
- Actual state: client-safe structured actions sourced from governed signals
- Remaining issue: not all continuity sources are equally strong

### Control Room `Governance Evidence Coverage`

- Risk: low
- Actual state: aggregate-only, suppressed when unsafe
- Remaining issue: could still be misread as condition truth rather than coverage quality if badly introduced elsewhere

## Labels That Need Guardrails

- `memory`
- `unresolved`
- `carried forward`
- `tracked`
- `verified recurrence`

## Suppress / Soften / Source-Label Recommendations

Suppress:

- Any escaped raw accusation or respondent-sensitive phrasing that survives unsafe-content matching

Soften:

- Decision Centre memory claims
- Strategy Room session unresolved claims

Source-label:

- Executive Reporting
- Strategy Room entry
- Strategy Room session
- Decision Centre

## Market-Use Warning

The system is close to premium-grade continuity, but some surfaces still rely on **confidence by presentation rather than confidence by trace**. That is acceptable for controlled entry with disciplined language, but not yet for maximal market claims.

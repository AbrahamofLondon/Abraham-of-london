# Oversight Brief Efficacy Standard

**Date:** 2026-05-08
**Rule:** No brief may be delivered unless it reaches at least STRONG, is client-safe, and is explicitly approved through a governed review decision.

---

## Grades

| Grade | Score | Auto-deliver | Operator review |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| FORMIDABLE | 75+ with 2+ actions, 4+ signals, and real continuity movement | NO | REQUIRED |
| STRONG | 60+ with 1+ action and clean client-safe output | NO | REQUIRED |
| ADEQUATE | 40+ | NO | REQUIRED |
| WEAK | <40 | NO | REQUIRED |
| WITHHOLD | Any score with withhold reason | NO | REQUIRED |

---

## Dimensions (9)

### Signal Density
Brief must contain multiple real signals — cost, recurrence, commitment, boardroom, counsel, credit, outcome. Score: 15 points per live signal. Minimum 3 signals for substantive brief.

### Evidence Strength
Score based on evidence tiers. Self-reported only caps score. Outcome-verified lifts. Multi-case lifts. Need active cases and verified commitments.

### Decision Specificity
Cases must be named with specific decisions and actions. Generic language scores low. Each named case with a next action contributes 25 points.

### Actionability
**WITHHOLD if no required actions exist.** A brief without actions wastes credibility. 2+ actions needed for FORMIDABLE.

### Retainer-grade threshold

A brief without historical comparison cannot be FORMIDABLE unless a first-cycle exception is manually recorded with an operator note.

FORMIDABLE requires at least four of:

- cost movement
- commitment movement
- recurrence movement
- decision credit movement
- counsel trigger
- boardroom threshold
- outcome movement
- irreversibility movement
- option decay or loss
- dependency conflict

STRONG requires:

- at least one required action
- at least one consequence
- at least one evidence basis
- at least one continuity or verification marker
- no unresolved sensitive suppressions
- enough live signals to justify delivery

### Consequence Clarity
Must name cost, exposure, breaches, counsel triggers, or risk. Vague consequence scores low.

### Continuity Value
Score high when comparing this cycle against prior state. First-cycle briefs inherently score lower — this improves with repeated oversight.

### Executive Relevance
Score high when boardroom/counsel/retainer-level signals are present. Operational noise alone scores low.

### Suppression Safety
**WITHHOLD if unresolved sensitive content remains** in client-safe version. Correct suppressions score 70. No suppressions needed scores 100.

### Retainer Value Proof
Must clearly show what the client would have missed without oversight. Score high when cost, breaches, boardroom threshold, counsel trigger, or credit movement are present.

## Section-level minimums

| Section | Must include |
|---------|-------------|
| Executive Summary | One strongest signal, one consequence, one required action |
| Active Cases | Case name, current state, evidence tier, unresolved issue, next action |
| Cost of Inaction | Amount, basis, elapsed period, confidence boundary |
| Pattern Recurrence | Status, prior count, explanation, confidence boundary |
| Commitment Verification | Commitment, status, due/overdue/verified/blocked, next step |
| Boardroom / Counsel | Trigger reason, threshold, escalation or reason not escalated |
| Required Actions | What, owner, timing, consequence of non-action |

---

## Delivery rule

A brief is not retainer-grade unless it is:

- reviewed
- scored
- client-safe
- approved, revised, withheld, or escalated through a recorded review decision
- tied to the next governed cycle

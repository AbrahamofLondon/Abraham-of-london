# Personal Assessment Product Standard

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Surfaces:** Fast Diagnostic, Purpose Alignment, Constitutional Diagnostic entry
**Doctrine:** Decision Intelligence as Infrastructure
**Standard:** Personal decision infrastructure, not self-help.

---

## Positioning principle

The personal assessment is the naturally strongest low-friction entry point into Decision Infrastructure by Abraham of London.

It must feel:

- **Personal but not sentimental.** The system reads your decision structure. It does not affirm your feelings.
- **Useful but not generic.** Every output is specific to the inputs provided. No templates. No archetypes.
- **Premium but not cold.** The tone is institutional authority, not clinical detachment.
- **Challenging but not hostile.** The system confronts weak logic. It does not judge the person.
- **Low-friction but not shallow.** Free entry, substantive output, earned progression.

---

## Current surface audit

### Fast Diagnostic (`/diagnostics/fast`)

**Strengths:**
- Headline is strong: "You don't have an execution problem. You have a decision structure problem."
- Three interrogation questions are specific and confrontational.
- Live hints flag vague language ("grow," "improve," "fix").
- Commitment gate: "If this identifies the real blocker, will you act on it within 48 hours?"
- Refusal moment: "Then this is not yet a decision."

**Weaknesses:**
- "Then this is not yet a decision" is condescending to exploratory users. Rewrite to: "The system is designed for decisions that are ready to move. You can still view the analysis, but the system will classify this as unresolved."
- No mention of "Decision Infrastructure" anywhere on the page.
- The commitment gate appears before the result, which may deter users who are curious but not yet committed. Consider moving to post-result.

**Recommended language direction:**
"Test whether your stated direction can survive evidence, pressure, contradiction, and consequence."

### Purpose Alignment (`/diagnostics/purpose-alignment`)

**Strengths:**
- "Your decisions reveal your real mandate" is a strong headline.
- "This is not a personality test" disclaimer is correct positioning.
- Dual-axis scoring (resonance x certainty) adds rigour.
- 14-day re-evaluation framing positions this as tracked infrastructure, not one-off assessment.
- "The move required to restore order" is prescriptive, not exploratory.

**Weaknesses:**
- "This is a personal decision surface" disclaimer is buried at the bottom of the hero in small grey text. Move higher.
- The page says "Personal Analysis" in the tag — should say "Personal Decision Infrastructure" or "Personal Layer."
- No explicit mention of what happens if patterns don't improve at 14-day re-evaluation.
- The difference between Purpose Alignment (personal) and Constitutional Diagnostic (institutional) is implied, not stated clearly at entry.

### Constitutional Diagnostic (`/diagnostics/constitutional-diagnostic`)

**Strengths:**
- "Before strategy, the constitution" is a strong institutional claim.
- Routes to STRATEGY / DIAGNOSTIC / REJECT — hard gate is visible.
- Instrument specification box (questions, scoring, routes, output) is transparent.

**Weaknesses:**
- REJECT route is mentioned but not explained. Users don't know what rejection means until they receive it.
- Add upfront copy: "REJECT means the identified condition prevents escalation to the strategy phase — other pathways remain open."

## Required changes

### Copy corrections

1. **Fast Diagnostic commitment decline:** Change "Then this is not yet a decision" to "The system is designed for decisions ready to move. You can still view the analysis — the system will classify this as unresolved."

2. **Purpose Alignment tag:** Change "Personal Analysis" to "Personal Decision Infrastructure" or remove the tag and let the headline carry positioning.

3. **Purpose Alignment disclaimer:** Move "This is a personal decision surface" from bottom of hero to directly below the headline, styled as a visible institutional label.

4. **Constitutional Diagnostic REJECT explainer:** Add below the routes list: "REJECT means the current evidence does not support escalation. Other assessment pathways remain open."

5. **All personal surfaces:** Add micro-line near entry CTA: "Part of Decision Infrastructure by Abraham of London."

### Tone calibration

The personal surfaces should use this vocabulary:

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| decision structure | mindset |
| operating pattern | personality type |
| structural reading | assessment result |
| evidence of contradiction | area for improvement |
| required move | recommended next step |
| decision infrastructure | personal growth tool |
| the system reads | we analyse |
| consequence | impact |

### Progression language

After any personal assessment result, the progression prompt should feel earned:

**Good:** "Your evidence is strong enough for deeper structural analysis. The Constitutional Diagnostic reads the organisation."

**Bad:** "Upgrade to unlock deeper insights."

## Standout product standard

For the personal assessment to become a standout product:

1. **First value in under 2 minutes.** Already achieved by Fast Diagnostic.
2. **Visible restriction.** Already achieved — commitment gate and vague-input flags.
3. **Specific output.** Already achieved — condition narrative, cost of inaction, required move.
4. **Memory signal.** Already achieved — 14-day re-evaluation framing.
5. **Earned progression.** Needs work — progression copy should reference evidence strength, not product tiers.
6. **Clear product identity.** Needs work — "Decision Infrastructure" naming absent from personal surfaces.

**Date**: 2026-05-08  
**Purpose**: Map each question to its real strategic purpose

## Purpose Categories

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|

## 1. Fast Diagnostic

| Question | Primary Purpose | Secondary Purpose | Why This Matters |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| "What decision has been sitting unresolved longer than it should?" | DECISION | PRESSURE | Names the core unit of value. Without a named decision, nothing else matters. The "longer than it should" creates temporal pressure. |
| "Who can actually make this decision binding?" | AUTHORITY | IDENTITY | Separates decision-owners from decision-influencers. "Actually" and "binding" are precise — excludes nominal authority. |
| "What becomes more expensive if this stays unresolved?" | COST | PRESSURE | Forces consequence articulation. The word "more" implies existing cost is already being paid — creates urgency to act. |

**Purpose coverage**: DECISION, AUTHORITY, COST — 3 of 13 categories. Appropriate for a 3-question entry point.

### Gaps

1. **VERIFICATION** (0 questions across all surfaces): No surface asks "How will you verify this was done?" or "What evidence will prove this was executed?" This is a genuine gap — the product has verification logic downstream (enforcement cycles, outcome verification) but never asks the user to define what verification looks like.

2. **HISTORY** (1 question across all surfaces): Only Constitutional Diagnostic q9 asks about history ("Past attempts to correct the issue have failed..."). The Fast Diagnostic and Purpose Alignment don't ask what's been tried before. This means the system often produces recommendations without knowing what has already failed.

3. **FAILURE_PATTERN** (4 questions, all in Constitutional Diagnostic and Strategy Room symptoms): The Fast Diagnostic and Purpose Alignment don't ask why previous attempts failed. Users entering through these paths get recommendations that may repeat failed approaches.

### Recommendations

1. **Add a VERIFICATION question to the Fast Diagnostic**: After Q3 ("What becomes more expensive..."), add "How will you know this has been resolved?" This would feed the enforcement cycle and outcome verification systems.

2. **Add a HISTORY question to the Purpose Alignment context**: After C3 ("What becomes worse..."), add "What have you already tried that hasn't worked?" This would prevent the system from recommending approaches the user has already attempted.

3. **Add a FAILURE_PATTERN question to the Enterprise Assessment**: After the 4 blocks, add "What previous attempts to address these issues have failed, and why?" This would feed the pattern recurrence detection.

---

## Strategic Verdict

The questioning system covers **11 of 13 purpose categories** across the product ladder. The two gaps (VERIFICATION, HISTORY) are real but narrow — they affect specific surfaces, not the entire system.

The purpose distribution is appropriate:
- **Entry points** (Fast Diagnostic, Purpose Alignment) focus on DECISION, AUTHORITY, COST, IDENTITY
- **Organisation assessments** (Constitutional, Enterprise) focus on CONTRADICTION, FAILURE_PATTERN, EXECUTION_CAPACITY
- **Institutional intake** (Strategy Room) covers the broadest range (11 categories)

No question is asking something that another question already covers better. No purpose category is over-represented. The system is well-balanced.

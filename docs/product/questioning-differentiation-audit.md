# Questioning Differentiation Audit

> Audit date: 2026-05-08
> Central question: Do our questions prove this is a governed decision infrastructure, not a survey, coaching form, or diagnostic quiz?

---

## Category Separation Test

The question layer must prove this product is NOT:

| Competitor Category | How Our Questions Must Differ |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| A survey | Surveys measure sentiment. We extract decision intelligence. Surveys are optional. Our questions gate access. |
| A coaching form | Coaching forms invite reflection. We demand specificity. Coaching forms accept vagueness. We reject it. |
| An AI prompt box | AI prompt boxes accept anything. We constrain input to produce evidence. |
| A management consulting intake | Consulting intakes gather context. We price consequences and surface contradictions. |
| A personality test | Personality tests classify the user. We classify the decision. |
| A dashboard onboarding flow | Onboarding flows collect preferences. We collect commitments. |
| A diagnostic quiz | Quizzes produce a score. We produce an enforceable decision record. |

## Where Questions Already Create Category Separation

These questions are already proprietary-grade and no competitor would produce them:

1. **"If this identifies the real blocker, will you act on it within 48 hours?"** — commitment before results. No survey, coaching form, or diagnostic does this.
2. **"Who can actually make this decision binding?"** — "binding" is not a word competitors use. It implies governance, not management.
3. **"If someone asked me right now what my actual job is — not my title, but my real function — I could answer in under ten seconds."** — the ten-second constraint is visceral and memorable.
4. **"The five people I spend the most time with are making me sharper, not softer."** — specific, confrontational, unforgettable.
5. **"You committed to act. No action has been recorded."** — the system remembers and confronts. This is governance, not feedback.
6. **"Where does ownership of this decision collapse? Name the handoff point where accountability disappears."** — (adaptive bank) names the structural failure with surgical precision.
7. **"Name the person who benefits most from this decision NOT being made."** — (adaptive bank) no consulting intake asks this.
8. **"readyForUnpleasantDecision" / "willingAccountability"** — hard gates. The product refuses access if you won't commit. This is the opposite of every SaaS product.

---

## Differentiation Verdict

**The product's best questions are category-defining.** The Fast Diagnostic, the adaptive question bank, the return brief prompts, and the commitment/admission gates create an experience no competitor replicates.

**The product's weakest questions are category-eroding.** The enterprise respondent set, the generic Purpose Alignment statements, and the productivity-language questions make the product feel like something it is not.

**The ratio is currently 83% differentiated / 17% generic.** The goal should be 95%+ differentiated. The 17 generic questions identified above must be rewritten or removed to maintain category separation.

**No rewrites should expose engine mechanics.** All proposed replacements maintain the user-facing confrontation without revealing scoring formulas, thresholds, weighting, or internal architecture.

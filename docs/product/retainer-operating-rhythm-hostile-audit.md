# Retainer Operating Rhythm Hostile Audit

Retainer System Classification: `RETAINER_GRADE_MINIMUM_PLUS`

Brief Efficacy Grade: Strong when cadence, client-safe output, comparison, and cancellation-loss are present. Formidable still requires prior-cycle comparison or manual first-cycle exception.

Client-Safe Delivery Grade: Controlled. Sponsor-safe and board-safe outputs now differ, but delivery is still portal-state rather than scheduler or email automation.

Review Workflow Grade: Strong. Operator can review, approve, withhold, revise, escalate, archive, and move delivery state without touching raw APIs.

Cycle Comparison Grade: Strong. Archived cycles now support practical delta comparison across cost, commitments, recurrence, credit, boardroom, counsel, irreversibility, option decay, losses, and dependency risk.

Counsel Workflow Grade: Partial-Strong. Counsel is now a governed workflow with auditable history, but not yet a full staffed counsel pipeline.

Indispensability Grade: Strong. The brief now states what visibility would be lost if oversight stopped, grounded in real preserved signals.

£5k Readiness: Yes, in controlled conditions with operator review discipline.

£15k Readiness: Not yet. Missing richer organisation divergence depth, stronger sponsor-safe enterprise posture, and deeper operating cadence beyond individual cycles.

£50k Readiness: No. Enterprise Control Room, role model hardening, scheduler-backed cadence, broader boardroom memory, and deeper counsel history are still incomplete.

Biggest Remaining Debt: Scheduler-backed monthly execution and richer enterprise-safe multi-user divergence memory.

Highest-Leverage Next Move: Move cadence from derived status to scheduler-backed execution, then deepen organisation divergence and boardroom/counsel archive depth for enterprise retained accounts.

## Questions

Is the £5k retainer now operationally defensible?

Yes. A reviewed, archived, client-safe brief can now be composed, scored, approved, and viewed through a controlled surface.

Can a client safely receive the brief?

Yes, through the governed client-safe brief surface, subject to access and suppression rules.

Can an operator review, approve, withhold, and deliver?

Yes. The operator bench supports governed review and delivery-state changes.

Is cadence now product-enforced or still manual?

Partially product-enforced. Cadence status is derived from real timestamps and archive state, but job scheduling is still intentional rather than automated.

Is boardroom memory archived?

Yes, at minimum viable level via archive entries tied to oversight cycles.

Is organisation divergence sponsor-safe?

Yes, at sponsor-safe summary level. Depth is still conservative.

What still blocks £15k?

- richer organisation divergence memory
- stronger enterprise sponsor-safe surface depth
- more durable cadence execution

What still blocks £50k?

- enterprise Control Room
- operator role hardening
- scheduler-backed cadence
- broader boardroom and counsel longitudinal history
- cross-organisation retained intelligence

What would a hostile enterprise buyer still reject?

- cadence without scheduler enforcement
- limited divergence depth
- partial boardroom/counsel memory compared with a mature institutional archive

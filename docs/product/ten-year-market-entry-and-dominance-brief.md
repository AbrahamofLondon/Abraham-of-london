# Ten-Year Market Entry & Dominance Brief

**Date:** 2026-05-08
**Authority:** Decision Infrastructure by Abraham of London

---

## The One Sentence

Decision Infrastructure is the system that remembers what you decided, what you avoided, what it cost, what repeated, what worsened, and what must now be governed.

---

## Category Position

We are not entering the market to compete with dashboards, AI copilots, assessments, coaching platforms, advisory firms, BI tools, or consulting retainers.

Those categories observe, summarise, advise, or report.

Decision Infrastructure governs decision quality over time, remembers what the client avoids, prices what delay costs, detects repeated contradiction, verifies execution, escalates unresolved pressure, and preserves institutional memory that becomes more valuable every cycle.

---

## The Moat

Competitors can copy language. They cannot easily copy:

- accumulated decision history
- evidence ledger
- contradiction memory
- cost accumulation
- recurrence patterns
- decision credit
- outcome verification
- boardroom archive
- counsel history
- organisation divergence
- oversight cycle comparison

The moat is not secrecy. The moat is accumulated intelligence.

---

## Market Dominance Through Five Ideas

1. **Refusal as product** — most systems always answer. We refuse when evidence is insufficient.
2. **Delay as measurable cost** — most systems track activity. We track the cost of not acting.
3. **Repetition as evidence** — most systems treat each case separately. We detect recurring contradiction.
4. **Execution reliability as capital** — most systems track tasks. We track decision credit.
5. **Oversight as strategic visibility** — most retainers sell access to people. We sell continuity of governed intelligence.

---

## Retainer Lock-In

The client must eventually understand:

> "If we leave, we do not merely lose a supplier. We lose accumulated decision visibility."

Retainer tiers:

- £5k/month — Oversight (monthly brief, cost clock, commitment verification, recurrence, boardroom alerts)
- £15k/month — Executive Command (+ irreversibility, options, losses, counsel, dependency)
- £50k/month — Institutional Command (+ Control Room, portfolio, cross-org memory, strategic twin)

## Emotional Target

Not fear. Recognition.

> "This system just named what I have been avoiding — and it is now tracking whether I do anything about it."

---

## Non-Negotiable Standard

Every primary output must feel bespoke. If a sentence could appear unchanged for another user, challenge it. If a result does not make the user feel recognised, it is not good enough. If a brief does not tell the client something they would have missed, it is not retainer-grade.

## Core Experience Arc

1. **Entry** — "Name the decision. The system will test whether it can be governed."
2. **Discovery** — "You said X. You also said Y. Both cannot govern the same decision."
3. **Consequence** — "Delay compounds. The system is counting."
4. **Admission** — "Progression is earned by evidence, authority, and readiness."
5. **Execution** — "This is now under governance."
6. **Return** — "You are not starting again. The system remembers."
7. **Oversight** — "This is what oversight preserved that ordinary review would have missed."

We entered as a governed decision institution. We made users feel the cost of delay. We made recurring contradiction visible. We remembered what they committed to.

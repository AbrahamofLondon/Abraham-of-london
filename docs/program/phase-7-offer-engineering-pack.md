# Phase 7 Offer Engineering Pack

Status: PLANNED
Live surface truth: Strategy Room is live as a surface, but this pack is a future-state offer and operator standard, not proof that every described intervention artifact is implemented.

## Objective

Engineer offers that convert serious principals without degrading trust, seriousness, or operator discipline.

This pack governs:

- the Strategy Room structure
- the offer stack around Executive Reporting
- operator scripts
- objection handling
- intervention packaging

## Core Offer Logic

The system should close by increasing decision clarity, not by increasing pressure.

That means:

- the buyer must feel more accurately seen
- the next step must feel more disciplined, not more sales-driven
- each offer must have a crisp boundary

## Offer Stack

| Offer | Core promise | Buyer condition | Primary output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Reporting | Convert scattered signal into decision-grade interpretation | Serious but not yet advisory-ready | Written interpretation and next-step architecture |
| Strategy Room | Resolve a high-stakes decision under constraint | Decision gravity, authority, urgency | Decision architecture, trade-offs, owners, cadence |
| Advisory Engagement | Govern execution after a serious decision | Need sustained implementation support | Ongoing intervention, reviews, standards, governance |
| Membership | Preserve access to doctrine and tools | Wants continuity and restricted assets | Ongoing archive, briefings, frameworks |

## Strategy Room Session Structure

### Stage 1. Admission check

Confirm:

- a real decision exists
- the decision-maker is present or represented with authority
- delay already carries measurable cost

If any of the above is false, do not open the room.

### Stage 2. Reality framing

Establish:

- what is actually being decided
- what cannot change
- what pressure the system is under
- what false options need to be removed

### Stage 3. Constraint map

Force clarity on:

- cash
- time
- people
- legal or compliance realities
- political or reputational exposure

### Stage 4. Option architecture

Reduce to a maximum of three real options.

For each option, define:

- upside
- downside
- reversibility
- time to value
- failure mode

### Stage 5. Decision and trade-off

The room should end with one of two outputs:

- a decision
- a disciplined deferral with explicit criteria and deadline

### Stage 6. Execution lock

Before the room closes, define:

- owner
- first actions
- review cadence
- risk monitoring points

## Operator Playbook

### Opening frame

Use:

`This room exists to produce a decision architecture that survives scrutiny. We are not here to generate more conversation than the decision requires.`

### Mid-room correction frame

Use:

`That is an observation, not yet a decision variable. Bring it back to authority, trade-off, or execution consequence.`

### Close frame

Use:

`The room is only complete when ownership, cadence, and failure conditions are explicit.`

## Objection Classification Model

| Objection class | What it usually means | Correct response |
|---|---|---|
| Price resistance | The buyer has not yet quantified the cost of drift | Re-anchor on delay cost and decision consequence |
| Time resistance | They do not yet believe this is decision-critical | Re-clarify irreversibility and near-term exposure |
| Trust resistance | They want proof of seriousness before access | Use authority assets, sample logic, and intake discipline |
| Scope resistance | They fear being sold into indefinite consulting | Clarify offer boundaries and decision-specific outputs |
| Authority resistance | The real decision-maker is absent | Refuse or reschedule around actual authority |

## Non-Salesy Close Framework

Use a three-part close:

1. Name the reality.
   Example: `You do not have an information problem. You have a decision-governance problem under time pressure.`
2. Name the correct container.
   Example: `Executive Reporting is the right next step if you need disciplined interpretation before intervention.`
3. Name the consequence of not acting.
   Example: `If this remains unresolved, the cost is not uncertainty. The cost is drift under pressure.`

## Intervention Matrix

| Buyer condition | Best next offer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Signal present, authority unclear | Executive Reporting | Creates clarity without wasting advisory capacity |
| Authority present, decision active, urgency high | Strategy Room | The scarce chamber is justified |
| Decision made, execution environment weak | Advisory Engagement | The problem has moved from choice to implementation |
| High trust, recurring need, archive appetite | Membership | Best continuity layer |

## Session Deliverables

Every Strategy Room should produce a minimum artifact set:

- decision statement
- option trade-off sheet
- execution owner list
- 30/90 day cadence outline
- explicit risk watchpoints

## Operator Standards

- never oversell access
- never promise transformation without governance
- never leave the room without named trade-offs
- never convert seriousness into theatrical language
- never use generic coaching language

## Offer Risks to Avoid

- collapsing Executive Reporting and Strategy Room into one blurred offer
- using Membership as a substitute for decision-specific value
- letting a Strategy Room become a free-form brainstorm
- offering retained work before the decision layer is genuinely complete

## Decisions Locked by This Pack

- Strategy Room is a decision chamber, not a generic session
- the system closes by sharpening reality, not by adding pressure
- objections are usually qualification problems, not persuasion problems
- offer boundaries are a trust mechanism

# Pricing Strategy

Status: PLANNED
Live surface truth: Pricing discipline and positioning logic only. No payment or checkout implementation is live in this document.

## Objective

Price by consequence, authority, and intervention depth rather than by feature count.

This system should never sound like software packaging or agency bundling.

## Core Rule

Price escalation should follow seriousness:

- low-friction qualification
- paid interpretation
- premium intervention
- high-ticket implementation

## Price Anchoring Logic

### 1. Anchor on consequence

The commercial frame is:

- what bad decisions cost
- what delay compounds
- what drift does to the institution

Do not anchor on:

- page length
- number of deliverables
- time spent
- feature comparison

### 2. Charge more when authority and consequence rise

Pricing should rise when:

- the decision is irreversible or costly to reverse
- the buyer has real authority
- more stakeholders are exposed
- the recommendation carries larger execution consequence

### 3. Preserve the difference between interpretation and intervention

`Executive Reporting` should remain materially cheaper than bespoke intervention while still premium enough to filter unserious demand.

`Strategy Room` should carry premium pricing because it consumes scarce decision-grade attention.

`Advisory` should be the highest-priced tier because it governs implementation burden over time.

## Escalation-Based Pricing

Use this sequence:

1. `Diagnostics`
   Purpose: qualification
   Price logic: free or minimal-friction
2. `Executive Reporting`
   Purpose: monetize seriousness through interpretation
   Price logic: premium fixed fee
3. `Strategy Room`
   Purpose: decision-forcing intervention
   Price logic: premium chamber fee
4. `Advisory`
   Purpose: govern implementation
   Price logic: high-ticket scoped engagement

## Refusal Logic

Do not accept:

- buyers who want prestige access without decision stakes
- buyers who cannot identify the real decision
- buyers who lack authority and cannot bring authority into the process
- buyers seeking therapy, validation, or generalized motivation under strategic language
- buyers whose requested outcome requires deception, coercion, or legitimacy laundering

## Commercial Discipline

- never sell Membership as a substitute for strategic consequence
- never collapse Executive Reporting and Strategy Room into one blurred offer
- never lower price by adding vagueness around the actual stakes
- never justify price using quantity when consequence is the real variable

## Decision Instruments

Decision instruments sit below premium escalation.

- `Decision Exposure Instrument`
- `Mandate Clarity Framework`
- `Intervention Path Selector`
- `Operator Decision Pack`

Principle:

- these are not replacements for `Executive Reporting`
- these are not replacements for `Strategy Room`
- these are lower-stakes paid instruments for users whose evidence does not yet justify premium escalation

Commercial rule:

- they may be monetised later
- they must not bypass earned progression
- they should be recommended only when the evidence supports that instrument

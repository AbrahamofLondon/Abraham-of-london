## A-List Agent Detailed Notes

### Kate Evans — PFD (UK)

**Why she might respond:** Kate Evans has articulated an interest in nonfiction that "says something about the way we live" — not issue-led journalism but writing grounded in life at the level of experience. *Fathering Without Fear* is exactly that: a book that examines institutional pressure, family law, faith, and inheritance through the specific grain of one life. Her openness to "extraordinary personal experiences" and "passionate manifestos from experts in their field" makes her a natural target. The manuscript's compression and its refusal of easy resolution would appeal to an agent who prizes narrative nonfiction with a strong voice.

**What to emphasise:** The ancestral lineage — the Isua-to-Lagos movement, the tailoring, the first Christians. The father-and-son layering. The way the book refuses to become a legal argument. The refusal to disappear as a fatherhood posture.

**What NOT to emphasise:** Family court proceedings in any detail. Do not lead with the legal material. Do not pitch it as a story of injustice.

**Angle:** Literary / family memoir / institutional endurance / faith-adjacent.

**Submission materials:** Cover letter + full chapter outline + three sample chapters.

### Nicola Chang — David Higham (UK)

**Why she might respond:** Chang explicitly cites Janet Malcolm and Annie Ernaux as touchstones — both writers known for moral seriousness, formal compression, and the refusal of sentimentality. The book's architecture (close-grained prose, compressed chapters, deliberate withholding) matches that aesthetic. Her interest in "the boundary between fiction and nonfiction" is relevant: this manuscript operates in that boundary zone — it is prose memoir that moves with the precision of literary fiction. Her stated interest in power, class, and culture adds another entry point through the institutional and legal material.

**What to emphasise:** The prose quality. The compression. The way the book withholds as much as it reveals. The Iyalode chapters. The father's death and what it costs.

**What NOT to emphasise:** The court proceedings as victimhood narrative. The specific legal claims.

**Angle:** Literary memoir / prose benchmark / moral compression.

**Submission materials:** Cover letter + first 50 pages in Word format. Email nicolasubmissions@davidhigham.co.uk.

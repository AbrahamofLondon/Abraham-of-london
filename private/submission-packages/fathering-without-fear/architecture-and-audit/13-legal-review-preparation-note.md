# Legal Review Preparation Note — Fathering Without Fear

## Purpose

Prepare for specialist legal review of Chapter 16 ("Fatherhood Began Outside") without exposing unnecessary protected material. This note is for internal use only. Do not share with the legal reviewer as a brief — use it to prepare the materials they will receive.

## Scope of Required Review

Only Chapter 16 requires mandatory legal review before wide circulation or submission to agents/publishers. Other chapters have been edited to exclude protected material and do not require review at this stage.

## What the Manuscript Intentionally Excludes

The manuscript has been edited with the following principles:

- No names of protected parties (mother, child's full name, extended family members involved in proceedings)
- No court findings, orders, or assessments quoted or paraphrased
- No allegations narrated — only the fact that proceedings existed and the author's experience of them
- No sealed material, transcript material, or chambers material
- No medical, psychological, or diagnostic claims about any party
- No adversarial framing — the other parent is not named, described, or characterised
- The child is rendered as a person, not as evidence or proof of any claim

## What the Legal Reviewer Should Be Told

The reviewer should be informed that:

1. The manuscript intentionally excludes protected detail. The restraint is deliberate, not a gap to be filled.
2. Chapter 16 is the only chapter requiring review at this stage.
3. The review should focus on defamation, privacy, family-court confidentiality, and sealed-material risk.
4. The reviewer should not recommend expanding the chapter with additional allegations or protected material.
5. The reviewer should assess the chapter as it stands — not as it could be if more detail were added.

## Review Questions

The legal reviewer should be asked to address:

1. **Is Chapter 16 publishable in its current restrained form?** Does the chapter as written create any defamation, privacy, or confidentiality risk?

2. **Does any wording imply an allegation or finding not safely stated?** Even if no explicit allegation is made, could a reader infer a specific claim about a protected party?

3. **Does any wording risk identifying protected parties beyond what is necessary?** Are there any indirect identifiers (dates, locations, descriptions) that could allow a motivated reader to identify individuals who should remain protected?

4. **Does the chapter respect the privacy of the child?** Could any passage in the chapter cause harm to the child if read by him now or in the future?

5. **What is the required timeline for changes?** Are there changes required before the manuscript can be shown to agents or publishers, or only before publication?

## Material to Provide to the Reviewer

- Chapter 16 only (clean copy, no annotations)
- This preparation note (for context, not as a brief)
- A brief summary of the legal context (proceedings type, jurisdiction, approximate timeframe) — without naming parties or providing specific case details
- Confirmation that pseudonyms are used where needed

## Material Not to Provide

Do not provide:

- Full manuscript (not needed for this review)
- Names of protected parties
- Specific court orders, findings, or assessments
- Correspondence or documentation from proceedings
- Any material that could be considered part of the court record
- Drafts of Chapter 16 that include material that was cut for legal reasons

## Timeline

- Legal review to be completed before agent submission
- If changes are required, they will be made before submission
- A second review may be needed before publication if the manuscript changes substantially after agent/publisher input

# Positioning Note — Fathering Without Fear

## Category

Compressed literary memoir / short memoir-in-fragments.

The book sits in the literary nonfiction space alongside formally compressed memoirs that use brevity as a structural principle rather than a limitation. It is not a conventional linear autobiography. It is not self-help. It is not devotional. It is not legal nonfiction.

## Word Count

49,981 words across 27 chapters, rounded outward-facing to approximately 50,000 words.

## Why the Brevity Is Intentional

Path A is now the chosen positioning: compressed literary memoir. The book's compression is its formal strategy, not a sign of incompleteness. Most chapters are between 800 and 3,800 words. Each carries a single weight — a name, a fire, a hearing date, a death, a boy's favourite animal — and sets it down before moving to the next.

This structure reflects three deliberate choices:

1. **Privacy.** A living child stands inside the story. The book's restraint is not evasion — it is protection. Chapters that could be longer are kept short because the material they would expand into is legally sensitive, involves protected individuals, or would compromise the child's right to grow up without his childhood being publicly narrated in detail.

2. **Formal discipline.** The book treats compression the way poetry treats lineation — as a meaning-bearing constraint. The short chapters do not summarise. They land a single image, a single scene, a single sentence that could only have been written by this author, and then stop. The white space between chapters carries weight.

3. **Reader trust.** The book respects the reader's intelligence. It does not explain what the reader can feel. It does not build emotional arguments across long chapters. It deposits evidence and moves on.

## Why the Book Should Not Be Padded

Expanding to 60,000–80,000 words would require either:

- Adding material that is legally sensitive and cannot be published without specialist review;
- Inflating existing chapters with explanatory prose that would undermine the book's discipline;
- Restoring material that was deliberately cut during the editorial process because it weakened the manuscript.

None of these options improves the book.

## On the Restored Chapter

One chapter — Ch.20, "The Available Tools" — was added during the editorial process because it solved a demonstrable structural gap between institutional endurance and adult dislocation. It carries hunger, rent pressure, spiritual silence, work exhaustion, nightclubs, and the return of desire without presenting nightlife as collapse or dating as validation.

Another chapter — Ch.21, "The Version in His Head" — restores the texture of Abraham's ordinary adult life: social world, romantic desire, community, and the attempt to rebuild a life while the system still held his son. It carries the fatherhood red line — the book's strongest non-institutional proof of Abraham's refusal to disappear.

A third chapter — Ch.6, "A Year Without Home" — was added to fill an early-formation gap: the year Abraham spent in Akure after Jumoke's death, displaced from home, refusing class while still passing, committing to faith, and choosing a school because he hoped to find friends who had already gone. No further new chapters should be added unless they solve a demonstrable structural absence. The 27-chapter, 49,981-word manuscript is the complete book.

## Suitable Agent / Editor Interest Profile

- Agents and editors working in literary memoir and literary nonfiction
- Interest in formally compressed or fragmentary nonfiction
- Comfort with international context (Nigerian/British)
- Interest in fatherhood, family, inheritance, grief, faith, institutional pressure
- Appreciation for restraint and negative space as literary tools
- Willingness to position a short memoir without requiring it to be padded

## Comp Titles

Comp titles to be researched. Any comp-title selection must be based on fresh market research and should reflect:

- Formally compressed memoirs (short chapters, fragmentary structure)
- Literary memoirs dealing with fatherhood, inheritance, or institutional pressure
- Faith-inflected literary nonfiction that is not devotional genre
- Nigerian/British or diaspora memoir
- Books in the 30,000–50,000 word range that were successfully published and positioned as complete works

Do not use comp titles that position the book as trauma memoir, legal exposé, or prescriptive parenting.

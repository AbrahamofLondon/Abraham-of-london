# Agent Targeting Criteria — Fathering Without Fear

## Target Profile

Agents who represent literary memoir and literary nonfiction, with openness to:

- **Formally compressed nonfiction.** The book is approximately 49,000 words across 25 short chapters. The agent must be comfortable positioning a short memoir without requiring it to be padded to a conventional word count.

- **Faith-inflected work without devotional genre.** The book treats Christianity as a family inheritance and a lived reality, not as a genre. The agent should be comfortable with faith as subject matter without categorising the book as religious or inspirational nonfiction.

- **International / Nigerian / British context.** The book moves between Nigeria and London. The agent should have experience with or interest in diaspora memoir, African literary nonfiction, or cross-cultural family narrative.

- **Fatherhood as literary subject.** The book is about fatherhood — not as a prescriptive topic but as a practice rendered through restraint, presence, and institutional pressure. The agent should recognise fatherhood memoir as a viable literary category.

- **Family, inheritance, grief, and institutional pressure.** The book deals with family law, supervised contact, and systemic process without becoming a legal memoir. The agent should be comfortable with this material without expecting the book to become an exposé or a campaign text.

- **Restraint and negative space as literary tools.** The book's compression, its refusal to narrate certain material, and its protection of a living child are structural choices. The agent should read these as strengths, not as gaps.

## Agents to Avoid

- Agents who represent only celebrity memoir or platform-driven nonfiction
- Agents who require a large social media following or established public profile
- Agents specialising exclusively in trauma memoir or "survivor narrative"
- Agents seeking prescriptive parenting or self-help nonfiction
- Agents who would position the book as legal exposé or campaign journalism
- Agents uncomfortable with faith as a presence in literary nonfiction
- Agents who would require the manuscript to be expanded to 60,000+ words before submission

## Agent List

Agent list requires fresh web research before use.

No agents should be contacted using stale lists, assumed preferences, or AI-generated agent recommendations without verification. Each agent on the final list must be confirmed through:

- Current agency website or verified submission guidelines;
- Recent (within 12 months) deals, interviews, or manuscript wish-list posts;
- Confirmation that the agent is currently open to unsolicited queries in literary memoir / nonfiction.

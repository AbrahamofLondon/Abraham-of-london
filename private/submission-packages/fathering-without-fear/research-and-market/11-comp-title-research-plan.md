# Comp Title Research Plan — Fathering Without Fear

## Purpose

Identify 3–5 commercially published titles that can serve as comps for *Fathering Without Fear* in agent submissions and pitch materials. Comp titles must be verifiable through current market data, not guessed or inferred.

## Criteria

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Genre** | Literary memoir or formally compressed literary nonfiction |
| **Form** | Short chapters, fragmentary or compressed structure (under 50,000 words preferred) |
| **Themes** | Fatherhood, family, inheritance, grief, faith, institutional pressure, migration, cross-cultural identity |
| **Publication window** | Within the last 10–15 years (2011–2026) |
| **Publisher** | Major trade publisher or respected independent press |
| **Exclusions** | Not celebrity memoir, not self-help, not devotional/religious genre, not legal exposé, not prescriptive parenting |
| **Faith handling** | Faith may be present as lived experience or family inheritance, but the book must not be categorised as Christian/inspirational genre |
| **Cultural context** | Nigerian/British diaspora or cross-cultural family narrative preferred but not required |

## Comp Titles Requiring Fresh Web Research

The following placeholders require fresh web research. Do not use any title without verifying publication date, publisher, word count, form, and thematic fit through current sources.

### Placeholder 1: Nigerian/British diaspora literary memoir

- **Target profile:** A formally compressed memoir by a Nigerian or Nigerian-British author dealing with family, inheritance, grief, or faith
- **Research needed:** Search for Nigerian-British memoirs published 2011–2026 by literary presses; verify word count and form
- **Status:** ⬜ Not researched

### Placeholder 2: Fatherhood / family literary memoir (short form)

- **Target profile:** A short literary memoir (under 50,000 words) about fatherhood, family, or the experience of being a parent under pressure
- **Research needed:** Search for short-form fatherhood memoirs; verify word count, form, publisher
- **Status:** ⬜ Not researched

### Placeholder 3: Compressed / fragmentary literary memoir

- **Target profile:** A memoir that uses short chapters, fragments, or compression as a formal strategy rather than a limitation
- **Research needed:** Search for memoirs with formally compressed or fragmentary structures published 2011–2026
- **Status:** ⬜ Not researched

### Placeholder 4: Faith-inflected literary nonfiction (non-devotional)

- **Target profile:** A literary work where faith is present as lived experience or family inheritance without being categorised as Christian/inspirational genre
- **Research needed:** Search for literary memoirs that engage with Christianity as cultural or family inheritance, not as devotional genre
- **Status:** ⬜ Not researched

### Placeholder 5: Institutional pressure / family law literary memoir

- **Target profile:** A literary memoir dealing with family court, supervised contact, or institutional pressure without becoming a legal exposé
- **Research needed:** Search for memoirs that address family court or child contact systems from a literary rather than campaigning perspective
- **Status:** ⬜ Not researched

## Research Method

For each placeholder:

1. Search publisher catalogues, literary awards shortlists, and agent wish-lists
2. Verify publication date, publisher, word count, and form
3. Read sample pages or reviews to confirm thematic and formal fit
4. Record source URL and date checked
5. Assess fit against criteria above (score 0–5)
6. Select best 3–5 for use in submission materials

## Verification Required Before Use

Each comp title must be confirmed through:

- Publisher or author website with verified publication details
- Word count confirmed (not estimated from page count alone)
- Form confirmed (memoir or literary nonfiction, not self-help or prescriptive)
- Thematic fit confirmed through reviews or sample reading
- Source URL and access date recorded

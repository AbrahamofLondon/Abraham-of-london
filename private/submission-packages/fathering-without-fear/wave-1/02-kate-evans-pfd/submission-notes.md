# Submission Notes — Kate Evans, Peters Fraser & Dunlop

---

## Why Kate Evans

Evans has stated explicitly that she is drawn to nonfiction "that says something about how we live," to "strong narrative voices," and to "the extraordinary texture of personal experience." She has cited *The Rules Do Not Apply* by Ariel Levy and Andre Agassi's *OPEN* as memoirs she loves — both books defined by unflinching personal exposure, formal control, and the refusal to let the subject become merely sympathetic. *Fathering Without Fear* has all three qualities.

Her "passionate manifesto" framing is also relevant: the book makes an argument, even if it never states one explicitly. The argument is that fatherhood is a practice that can be maintained under almost any pressure, and that the cost of maintaining it is the cost of the book. That is a passionate manifesto in precisely her sense.

PFD is one of the UK's two or three leading literary agencies. A successful submission to Evans would mean strong UK representation with strong transatlantic reach.

---

## What This Agent Responds To

- Extraordinary personal experience that illuminates something beyond itself
- Narrative voice that is specific, controlled, and not generic
- Memoir with clear human stakes that extend beyond the individual
- Formal quality — the sense that the author has made deliberate choices about shape and structure
- The "passionate manifesto": a personal story that has a position
- Her memoir touchstones (*The Rules Do Not Apply*, *OPEN*) suggest she values exposure that is also formally accomplished; raw feeling is not sufficient — it must be made

---

## What to Emphasise in Interactions

If Evans responds with questions or requests more material:

1. **The ancestral lineage**: The Isua-to-Lagos movement, Iyalode's walk, the tailor, the name. This is what gives the book its reach beyond a personal story — it is a study of what is transmitted across generations
2. **The formal architecture**: 24 short chapters, each a closed unit. The brevity is not an incompleteness — it is a commitment to restraint that mirrors the narrator's own discipline in those contact rooms
3. **What the book refuses**: It refuses the legal argument, the victim arc, the memoir-as-catharsis structure. These refusals are active choices, not evasions
4. **The Agassi comparison**: Both books — *OPEN* and *Fathering Without Fear* — are about a man who stayed present at enormous personal cost, and who found that the cost clarified rather than diminished what mattered

---

## What NOT to Lead With

- Do not lead with the family court or supervised contact material in any follow-up
- Do not describe the book as "about the legal system" or "about institutional failure"
- Do not pitch it as a social justice narrative — the justice element is implicit, not polemical
- Do not pitch it as a faith memoir or devotional — faith is a strand, not a category
- Do not mention parallel queries to other agents

---

## Agency Logistics

- **PFD one-agent rule:** Do not query Daisy Chandley, Adam Gauntlett, Tim Bates, Laurie Robertson, Kieron Fairweather, or any other PFD agent while Evans is active. If Evans passes, wait for that confirmation before querying another PFD agent.
- **Response window:** PFD does not publish a standard window. Allow 12 weeks.
- **Follow-up:** A single brief email after 12 weeks is acceptable and professional.
- **Format:** PFD does not restrict to Word-only (unlike DHA). PDF attachments are acceptable.

---

## Positioning Reminder

This book should reach Kate Evans as:

> "A compressed literary memoir about fatherhood, inheritance, faith, and the cost of refusing to disappear — written from inside extraordinary personal experience, with the formal control and refusal of sentimentality of the best narrative nonfiction."

Not as: a court story, a campaign, a community story, or a faith testimony.

---

## Risk Assessment

**Upside:** Evans's stated interests are as strong a match as exists on the UK market. The Levy and Agassi touchstones confirm she reads memoir for formal quality and human exposure, both of which this manuscript has. She is also a very active agent — not overwhelmed and ignoring submissions.

**Risk:** PFD receives high volume. The query must earn its way through with the opening paragraph alone. Evans is unlikely to be won over by subject matter — it is the voice and the discipline that will move her.

**Mitigant:** The opening of Ch.1 (the court call, the posture of not being notified, the refusal to hang up) is one of the stronger opening hooks in the manuscript. It earns the space quickly. Ensure Ch.1 is as strong as it can be before sending.

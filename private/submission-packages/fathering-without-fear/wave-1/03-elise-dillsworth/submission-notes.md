# Submission Notes — Elise Dillsworth, Elise Dillsworth Agency

---

## Why Elise Dillsworth

Dillsworth is one of a very small number of UK agents who combines a stated interest in international literary memoir, a deep institutional background in diverse voices (Virago, Diversity in Publishing Network), and an independent agency structure that gives her full curatorial control over her list. She does not represent a broad commercial range; she chooses books she believes in and works them seriously.

The Nigerian-British arc of *Fathering Without Fear* — from Isua to Lagos to London across four generations — is precisely the kind of international writing she has stated she seeks. Her Virago background is particularly relevant: Virago built its reputation on finding serious literary work by and about people whose stories British publishing had historically passed over. *Fathering Without Fear* sits exactly inside that tradition.

She runs her own agency, which means she is the sole reader and the sole decision-maker. There is no assistant filter, no pre-screening. If she requests a manuscript, it is because she has made the decision herself.

---

## What This Agent Responds To

- International arc: stories that move between cultures, languages, and continents without treating the movement as the drama
- Literary seriousness in form: prose that earns its shape; not just compelling subject matter
- Diverse and underrepresented voices — specifically in the sense of writers whose lives are not yet well-represented in British literary memoir
- Memoir that is simultaneously personal and culturally wide: the private story as an entry point to a larger inheritance
- Biography and autobiography as serious literary acts, not just confessional writing
- Work that a Virago reader would recognise: politically alert, formally careful, not sentimental

---

## What to Emphasise in Interactions

If Dillsworth responds or requests more:

1. **The four-generation structure**: The book does not begin with Abraham — it begins with a tailor in Isua. The family line is the book's first subject, and Abraham arrives inside it, not in front of it
2. **The Iyalode figure**: A woman who carried both Yoruba spiritual knowledge and Christian faith without perceiving them as contradictions, who walked from Osun to Lagos, and who lived to 118. She is the book's deepest ancestral presence — and she is not incidental; she is the source of what the rest of the family carries
3. **The book's formal position**: It is neither testimony nor grievance; it is a literary act. This distinction matters to Dillsworth, whose Virago background makes her alert to the difference
4. **The diaspora arc specifically**: Lagos → Norwich → London. Not the standard Nigerian-to-UK immigration narrative; this is a man from a faith and family tradition that predates the colonial encounter, arriving in a city that sees only his surface

---

## What NOT to Lead With

- Do not lead with the legal/family court material in any communication
- Do not describe the book as "about the system" — the system is one pressure among several, not the subject
- Do not pitch it as a Black Lives Matter-adjacent work or tie it to any current political moment — its scope is too long and too ancestral for that framing
- Do not pitch it as a women's-press or feminist-press book — even though Virago is that context; Dillsworth is working as an agent of literary quality, not ideology
- Do not over-explain Yoruba cultural content — let it speak through the prose

- **Response window:** Three months; no reply after three months = rejection (stated policy); mark the send date and the three-month deadline clearly
- **Format:** Confirm current preference (Word or PDF) at elisedillsworthagency.com before sending; if not specified, send both

## Positioning Reminder

This book should reach Elise Dillsworth as:

> "A Nigerian-British literary memoir tracing four generations of inheritance across Isua, Lagos, and London — written in compressed, literary prose that is serious in form and wide in cultural range."

Not as: an immigration story, a court memoir, a faith testimony, or a social justice campaign.

---

## Risk Assessment

**Upside:** Her alignment with the book's cultural and literary character is the strongest of any UK agent on the list. She has explicitly built her career around exactly this kind of work.

**Risk:** She is an independent with a small list. This means she takes fewer books, and she will assess commercial potential alongside literary quality. The book's 50,000-word length and the absence of any established platform will be factors. The proposal needs to make the commercial case clearly: diaspora memoir is an expanding category with no current direct competitor; the fatherhood angle is fresh; the multigenerational Nigerian-British arc is not yet occupied by another recent title.

**Mitigant:** The proposal's market section and comparable titles must be precise. Don't undersell the book's position — it is the first major literary memoir about a Black British father navigating family courts and surviving. That is a real market position.

# Submission Notes — Michael Bourret, Dystel Goderich & Bourret

---

## Why Michael Bourret

Bourret has stated explicitly that he has "a special interest in BIPOC literature and writers" and seeks "offbeat projects that break the mold." Both statements apply directly to this manuscript. DGB is one of the leading US literary agencies with a strong nonfiction track record, and Bourret personally has one of the most visible commitments to BIPOC authorship on the US agent list.

The book's unconventional architecture — 25 short compressed chapters, an ancestral lineage as the opening movement, no linear cause-and-effect legal narrative — is exactly the kind of structural offbeat that Bourret's profile signals. Most memoir about fathers in family court is either polemical (the system is wrong) or confessional (here is what happened to me). *Fathering Without Fear* does neither. It is a study of inheritance. That is unusual enough to register.

DGB is also a strong US agency for UK authors — they have the transatlantic rights infrastructure to handle a book with dual UK/US market potential.

---

## What This Agent Responds To

- BIPOC voice and cultural specificity — not as identity performance, but as the substance of the narrative
- Projects that don't fit existing categories cleanly: this book is part family history, part spiritual memoir, part institutional account, part portrait of a father and child
- Narrative nonfiction with strong structural choices — Bourret handles memoir that has been deliberately made, not simply assembled from events
- Ancestral and multigenerational scope: the book goes back four generations before it arrives at the narrator
- The "offbeat" quality: the short-chapter compression is not conventional memoir architecture; it reads more like connected lyric essays or prose poetry sequences

---

## What to Emphasise in Interactions

If Bourret responds or requests more material:

1. **The four-generation structure** — this is not a standard memoir about the narrator; it is a study of what is transmitted
2. **The BIPOC specificity** — Nigerian-British, not just "Black British"; the Ondo/Lagos origin is specific and different from the more commonly represented West African diaspora experiences in US publishing
3. **The fatherhood gap** — there is no major US literary memoir currently occupying the position of a Black man writing about fatherhood under institutional pressure with literary seriousness. That position is open.
4. **The cultural stakes** — inheritance, faith, naming, and the refusal to disappear: these are broad stakes made specific through one man's life

---

## What NOT to Lead With

- Do not lead with family court, legal proceedings, or allegations under any circumstances with a US agent — US family law is different from UK family law, and the specifics will be confusing; the institutional pressure needs to be described at the level of experience, not legal structure
- Do not describe the book as a Christian memoir — faith is a strand, not the genre
- Do not use the phrase "social justice" — it is true but it narrows the pitch into a category that will sound like a campaign, not a literary memoir
- Do not mention UK-specific legal terminology (CAFCASS, Section 7, etc.)

## Positioning Reminder

This book should reach Michael Bourret as:

> "A Nigerian-British literary memoir that breaks the standard form of immigrant narrative and legal grievance — tracing a family line across four generations to a father who refuses to disappear, written in 25 short, formally compressed chapters."

Not as: a legal case study, a faith testimony, a social media–ready diversity narrative, or a campaign.

## Risk Assessment

**Upside:** Bourret's explicit BIPOC focus and appetite for structural unconventionality make this one of the strongest fits on the US agent list. DGB has strong institutional positioning and established transatlantic rights experience.

**Risk:** US agents typically expect clear market comparables and an established author platform. This is a debut without prior publications. The query must make the case that the book occupies a vacant position in the market, not that it is exceptional in a crowded one.

**Mitigant:** The comparables chosen (Coates, Wilkerson, Miller) are strong anchors — they establish literary quality and market credibility without overselling. Lead with the ancestral structure and the BIPOC identity strand; these are the two strongest hooks for Bourret specifically.

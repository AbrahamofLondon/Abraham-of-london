# Submission Notes — Reiko Davis, DeFiore & Company

---

## Why Reiko Davis

Davis has stated clearly that she is drawn to "intimate personal stories that function as lenses for larger issues" and to narratives about "social justice, the history and experiences of women and people of color." This is the most direct statement of fit for this book on the US list. The book's structural logic — one man's fatherhood as a lens on inheritance, faith, ancestral transmission, and institutional power — is exactly the "personal story as social lens" framework she describes.

She was confirmed open as of late 2025. Her profile on defliterary.com lists narrative nonfiction and memoir as primary genres. DeFiore & Company is a well-established US literary agency with strong nonfiction credentials.

---

## What This Agent Responds To

- Personal narrative that opens outward — stories that say something beyond themselves
- Social justice framing at the level of experience, not polemic: the injustice is lived, not argued
- History and experiences of people of colour — the Nigerian-British arc, the ancestral lineage, the encounter with UK family courts as a Black man, are all directly relevant
- "Immersive narrative nonfiction" — this describes the book's texture: it puts the reader inside the rooms, the contact sessions, the hearing, the fire
- Debut voices with urgency and specificity — she is building a list, which means she is looking for writers with a clear point of view

---

## What to Emphasise in Interactions

If Davis responds:

1. **The "social lens" structure**: The private story is the lens; the larger picture is inheritance, faith, and what institutional systems do to Black British fathers. Make this structure explicit if asked.
2. **The ancestral arc as social history**: Four generations of a Nigerian-British family moving from colonial-era Isua to contemporary London is a historical arc as much as a personal one
3. **The contact room chapters**: The supervised contact room experience (Chapter 18, "Seven Years") is one of the most socially legible parts of the book for a US reader — the experience of having one's fatherhood assessed and constrained by an institutional system is a recognisable injustice that doesn't require UK legal knowledge to understand
4. **The child's autonomy**: Damisi is not a victim, not evidence, not a symbol. He is a child — specific, funny, precise, insisting on his own identity ("I am not your bro, I am your son"). This is the book's most important social argument, made through character, not through advocacy.

---

## What NOT to Lead With

- Do not present the system as the main character — Davis specifically values the human story as the centre, with the system as context
- Do not use UK legal terminology; describe institutional pressure at the level of experience
- Do not pitch as a grievance — "social justice" in Davis's framing means writing that illuminates and humanises, not writing that accuses
- Do not describe the faith strand as devotional — it is ancestral, tested, and specific; not evangelical

---

## Agency Logistics

- **Email submission:** reiko@defliterary.com with subject `QUERY [Title]` — this format is stated and should not be varied
- **Body-only submission:** All materials pasted in email body; no attachments
- **Response window:** DeFiore does not publish a standard response window. Allow 12 weeks.
- **Follow-up:** Not standard practice for email submissions. If 12 weeks pass with no response, treat as implied pass.

---

## Positioning Reminder

This book should reach Reiko Davis as:

> "A literary memoir in which one Nigerian-British man's account of fatherhood becomes a study of what institutions do to fathers, what faith does to families, and what it means to inherit a name across four generations."

Not as: a legal narrative, a grievance, a campaign, or a religious memoir.

---

## Risk Assessment

**Upside:** The fit between Davis's stated interests and the book's structure is among the cleanest on the list. Her confirmation that she was open in late 2025 suggests an active list-builder, which is the right profile for a debut memoir.

**Risk:** DeFiore is a mid-sized US agency. They have strong nonfiction placement but are less visible on the transatlantic stage than a UK agent would be. If the goal is strong UK publication first, Davis is the right US Wave 1 query — but a UK deal does not depend on her response.

**Mitigant:** The email body format suits this book well — the first 20 pages include Ch.1 (the court call) and Ch.2 (the ancestral lineage). Those two chapters alone demonstrate both the human urgency and the social-lens architecture that Davis responds to. The submission leads with the book's strongest opening.

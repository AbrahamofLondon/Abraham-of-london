## A. Locked elements (inherited, unchanged)

These are carried forward exactly. Nothing below revises them.

- **Governing premise.** A novel can only show a true thing's shape. Allegory is the protection: "I am not telling you what happened. I am telling you what it was like to be inside it." **LOCKED.**
- **The world.** Liminal register, structural not decorative. Real-adjacent place-names; grief-and-dream time; the supernatural never explained or rationalised; "was that real?" structurally forbidden. By the second half the registers genuinely blur. **LOCKED.**
- **The central female figure (Obianuju).** A deliberate merge of the Ch.13 spiritual material and the later-marriage pattern. Never a villain. Every harm sits near a gift. The journal-before-they-met is written as a gift, not only a retrospective warning. Duality sustained to the last page. **LOCKED.**
- **The three forces.** Spirit Husband = CLAIM (felt as absence). The Voice = INTIMIDATION (speaks once or twice, names a cost). The Trance = WARNING ARRIVING TOO LATE TO PREVENT, IN TIME TO CONFIRM (morally neutral, horror is in accuracy). Distinct, in tension, none defeatable. **LOCKED.**
- **The ending shape.** The three forces swap roles, they do not resolve: the Voice goes silent; the Trance reactivates pointing at something *new* and unspecified; the Spirit Husband's claim simply stops being the loudest thing in the room. Obianuju's unfinished business rhymes with his, is never explained. The final scene is his alone, small and ordinary, all three forces present but no longer organising his life. **LOCKED.**

## B. RESOLVED — The protagonist

The source asked which quality the book should foreground and said not to resolve it prematurely. The recommendation below is made *because* the chapter architecture follows his arc, and the arc cannot be built without it. It is the strongest of the source's own candidates.

### B1. Foregrounded quality (RECOMMENDED): the fearlessness born of early near-death certainty.

He is a man who, as a child, came so close to death and survived by something he could not name that he has carried since a private certainty he is *not finished* — that an accounting governs him, that the price of being kept is a life owed, and that death therefore cannot frighten him.

This is the right engine for four reasons:

1. **It generates the central tragic irony.** A man immune to the fear of death is *defenceless against forces that do not kill*. The three forces claim, threaten, and warn — none of them simply ends him. His one strength is useless against all three. The thing that kept him alive is the thing that walks him into the current and keeps him there too long.
2. **It is the most cinematic, most universal material** the source can draw on, and it does not require any private fact to be exposed.
3. **It rhymes, deliberately, with the memoir.** *Fathering Without Fear* makes fearlessness a discipline; the novel makes fearlessness a *flaw*. The two books become thematic siblings — the same quality seen as virtue and as wound. This is a marketing and a literary gift (see `03` and `04`).
4. **It earns the ending.** A man who has always believed he was kept must, by the last page, learn the difference between being kept and being spared — the same distinction the memoir's Ch.23 reaches, arrived at here through fiction rather than confession.

### B2. The name (RESOLVED as a device, not a word).

Follow the Iyalode instinct: function and weight, not biography. **He carries no proper name to the reader.** This is deliberate and marketable (cf. the unnamed narrator tradition; cf. *Piranesi*, named by the force that holds him).

- The **Spirit Husband never names him** — to name is to grant standing, and the claim refuses him standing.
- **Obianuju** calls him by a single tender diminutive (to be discovered in drafting), which becomes unbearable once the reversal comes.
- The only "true" name in the book is the **function-word the Trance finally gives him at the end** — left open until the closing chapters reveal which quality the world actually needed from him. (The source's instinct, preserved.)

This keeps the protagonist question productively open *where the source wanted it open* (the final naming) while resolving it *where drafting needs it closed* (the foregrounded quality).

## C. RESOLVED — Structural homes for the three forces

The source flagged this as worth deciding once chapter architecture begins. Decision:

- **The Spirit Husband governs the domestic/interior register.** Cold rooms, the unreachable hour, the shape of a silence. He is the weather of every scene set inside the shared life. Present-as-absence; rarely staged directly.
- **The Voice governs the single mid-book hinge.** It speaks at the structural turn (end of Part II) and almost nowhere else. Its rarity is its power. One decisive deployment; at most a faint second echo near the end.
- **The Trance governs the novel's relationship to its own ending.** It is the device through which the registers blur in the second half and the device that delivers the final reactivation. The Trance is how the book *ends without resolving* — it points past the last page.

This gives each force a formal territory and prevents them collapsing into one antagonist in three masks (the source's explicit fear).

## D. RESOLVED — Register mechanics (the craft device that sells the liminal)

To make the second-half blur *enactable* rather than merely asserted:

- **"Real" scenes:** close third person, past tense.
- **Trance scenes:** close third person, **present tense.**
- **The argument of the form:** as the book proceeds, present tense begins to **bleed into the "real" scenes** until, by the final movement, the reader can no longer reliably use tense to know which register they are in. The novel never tells them. This is the source's "central formal argument" given a concrete, teachable mechanism — and it is the kind of device that wins literary prizes and review attention.

## E. RESOLVED — The continuation problem (child protection)

The merged figure includes "the reversal once a child was conceived." In the allegory this is the most dangerous material and the most powerful. Resolution:

- The reversal is catalysed by **continuation, not by a depicted child.** The Spirit Husband's claim is shown reaching to extend itself into what comes next — the horror is the claim seeking a second generation.
- **No child is portrayed on the page.** Continuation appears as threshold, expectation, a future the forces are already contesting — never as a living character. This honours the memoir's child-protection architecture and keeps the novel's legal-ethical firewall intact.

## F. RESOLVED — In-world title

"The Fiction Adaptation" is a working/meta label. It undersells and confuses a buyer. **Retire it as the public title** (it can survive only as a private frame). The novel needs a real, evocative, book-club-legible title. Shortlist, lead first:

1. **The One Who Arrived First** *(lead)* — carries the claim and the prior-right; immediately evocative; book-club-able; signals the spiritual-literary lane without genre-locking.
2. **The Current** — the in-medias-res image (a river you are already in); clean, literary, screen-friendly.
3. **Cold Rooms** — the Spirit Husband's signature; quiet menace; strong cover potential.
4. **The Unreachable Hour** — lyrical; foregrounds absence; slightly softer commercially.

Recommendation: draft under *The One Who Arrived First*; hold *The Current* as the screen/festival alternative. Final title is an agent/editor decision (as with the memoir).

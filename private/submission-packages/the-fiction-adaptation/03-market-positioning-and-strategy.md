# Market Positioning & Strategy — The One Who Arrived First

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Objective:** Position the allegorical novel to reach its maximum credible ceiling — the prize-and-book-club lane of contemporary literary fiction with a spiritual/supernatural register.

---

## 1. The Lane (and why it is the right one)

This book belongs to the **most awarded and most sellable lane in contemporary literary fiction**: serious literary novels that treat African/diaspora spiritual cosmology as real, render it with cultural authority, and never explain it away. This lane wins prizes (Booker, Women's Prize, Nommo), drives book-club sales, and attracts screen options. It is not a niche — it is a proven commercial-literary engine.

The novel's high concept is genuinely strong and rare: **"a novel in three forces — one claims her, one threatens him, one only ever tells the truth too late to act on it — and none of them can be defeated."** That is a one-line pitch an agent can sell and a reader can repeat. Most literary fiction has no such hook. This one does. Protect it.

---

## 2. Category & Shelf

- **Primary:** Literary Fiction.
- **Secondary signals:** literary supernatural / spiritual; psychological; mystery-adjacent (the teaser already tags these).
- **Comparative shelf:** Morrison, Okri, Emezi, Obioma — not horror, not fantasy, not romance. The supernatural is literary apparatus, not genre furniture.

**Pitch positioning line (one sentence):**
> *In the liminal tradition of Ben Okri and Akwaeke Emezi, a novel in three forces about a man who cannot fear death and the love he cannot reach because something arrived first — a book about what it costs to keep walking inside a world you cannot fight.*

---

## 4. Title Strategy

- **Retire "The Fiction Adaptation" as the public title.** It reads as a working label, undersells the book, and confuses a buyer. Keep it only as a private frame, or drop it.
- **Submit as *The One Who Arrived First*** (carries the claim, evocative, book-club-legible).
- **Hold *The Current*** as the screen/festival alternative.
- Final title is an agent/editor decision — flag the author as open (as with the memoir), but do not go out under the meta-label.

---

## 5. Length

Target **82–90k words** — full literary-fiction length. The memoir is deliberately compressed (~49k); the novel must not be. The market expects a literary novel to be a full-length object, and the liminal second-half blur needs room to accumulate. Under-length here would read as a novella and halve the commercial options. (This is the one place "more" is correct — the opposite of the memoir discipline.)

## 6. Audience

- Readers of Emezi, Okri, Obioma, Morrison, Helen Oyeyemi, Marlon James.
- Book clubs drawn to "discussable" spiritual/psychological fiction.
- Diaspora and African readerships — and, crucially, the **crossover literary audience** that bought *Piranesi* and *The Vanishing Half*: readers who want a strange, serious, talked-about novel.
- The memoir's eventual readership (see `04`) — a built-in first audience.

## 8. Prize Ecology (real upside, plan for it)

This lane is prize-rich. Build toward:
- **Booker Prize / Booker (international pipeline)** — the Okri/Obioma precedent.
- **Women's Prize** — N/A for the author; note for comp framing only.
- **Nommo Awards** (African speculative fiction) — the supernatural register fits squarely; a credibility and visibility lever.
- **British Book Awards / debut-fiction lists**, festival debut showcases.
- **Excerpt placement** in literary magazines (Granta, Guernica, *The White Review*, *Lolwe*, *Catapult*-adjacent) as prize-and-attention fuel — the same platform mechanism recommended for the memoir, here doubling as fiction credibility.

---

## 9. Screen / Adaptation Value (ancillary, real)

The three-forces high concept is castable and atmospheric; the lane has a strong option record (Emezi, Obioma, James optioned). The present-tense/past-tense register device translates to film as a tonal blur a director can build. Treat screen as **ancillary upside to be flagged in the pitch**, not as a driver of the writing. A novel written to be optioned reads thin; a novel written well gets optioned.

---

## 10. The One Strategic Risk to Manage

**Over-commercialising the supernatural** (turning it into a haunting-to-be-solved) would forfeit the prize/book-club ceiling for a lower genre-horror ceiling. The market value *is* the literary restraint. The manuscript plan's anti-genre-collapse rule (`02` §7) is therefore also the market plan: the book is worth more *because* the forces are never defeated.

---

## 11. Summary

The book sits in a proven, prize-rich, screen-friendly lane; it has a rare, repeatable high concept; it has a built-in first audience and a vetted agent list via the memoir; and it has an author whose cultural authority over the material is genuine. The maximum-potential plan is therefore not to make it *more commercial* — it is to make it *more fully itself*, at full literary length, under a real title, pitched as the literary-fiction half of a two-book career.

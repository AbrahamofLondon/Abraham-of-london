### Where Ch.1 grew (the three additions)
- **Addition 1 — the town as embodied life (~200 w):** the fish errand where the route delivers him to the wrong quarter; the sea "moving against the stones the way a neighbour moves about on the other side of a shared wall." The unmappable motif planted through *use*, not explanation.

Establishes the whole, slightly fierce person the hour takes — explicitly *not* a haunted-wife figure.
- **Addition 3 — fearlessness given a body (~110 w):** the brown-water flash (silt and salt at the back of the throat, the lungs finished, "no fear in any of it… only a great patience that was not his own").

Corrected to the **brown water**, which also delivers Addition 3. The same stray fire reference in Ch.2 ("the burning room of his boyhood") was corrected to "the brown water of his boyhood." ## 3. Chapter 2 — diagnostic and changes

**Diagnostic.**
- *Irreplaceable:* the gift framing; the two weighted lines ("Before you say anything. I wrote this before I knew your name." / "Because I was not the only one waiting. I only thought I was."); the resewn-spine detail.
- *Must not disturb:* the closing window movement and the final "she may not have fully known it either."
- *Over-compressed:* the journal's physical life; his *interior* reason for choosing the generous reading; the misrecognition seed.
- *Cut/fix:* the fire→water continuity fix (above).

**Changes (all scene/interiority, no exposition):**
- Journal texture deepened: the swollen damp pages, the breath of old paper, the child's upright hand giving way mid-entry to the woman's slant; a pressed flower that "had pressed back… left itself twice," rhyming with the cold hour leaving itself on every room.
- New interiority — **the danger of the generous reading**: being read so exactly *flatters* the very lateness he is ashamed of; his generosity toward the journal is "self-interested in the deepest, least visible way." This is the brief's "loving reading can also be blindness," dramatised, not stated.
- **The pages he avoids:** the unfinished line "*he came to the door again last night and I did not*," turned past quickly, explained away as damp. Intimacy beginning to tip into misrecognition.
- The final sentence's misreading made explicit at the level of his deafness, not the narrator's exposition: "a sentence about being waited for can have a second person in it, and… she had not said which of them she meant. He heard only the half that loved him."

No spirit-spouse tradition explained; no plot-device handling; foreshadowing kept quiet.

## 4. Chapter 3 — diagnostic and changes

**Diagnostic.**
- *Irreplaceable:* the drowning origin paragraph; the "handed down… the way a sentence is handed down in a court" passage; the trance.
- *Must not disturb:* the trance's internal logic and its closing arithmetic ("I was here for the long rains before you were born to be late to them").
- *Over-compressed:* the town's embodied life; the town's *different memory* of the drowning; the trance's status as more-than-dream.
- *Cut/fix:* none required beyond consistency with the drowning canon.

**Changes (all scene, no tourist texture, no magical-realism wash):**
- **The town remembers the drowning differently:** the men's lowered-voice version (he surfaced *calm*, not gasping); the old woman who said "the water gave him back because it wanted something kept" as flatly as a remark about the rains. Impossibility "folded… among the ordinary intelligence a person needed in order to live there."
- **People treating impossibility as ordinary** (new vignette): the neighbour who has built the same house three times where the sea quietly takes it; the girl "pulled blue from a well at four" who now blesses the sick — "the ordinary economy of who blesses and who is blessed." Ends by tying his fearlessness to the town's calm: "He had not got his fearlessness only from the brown water. He had got it from a town that had never once, in his hearing, been surprised." (Deepens character *and* theme.)
- **Embodied market beat:** highlife through a blown speaker, the net-mender, the crayfish liturgy — and the contrast that "a man could move through all that life and be no one in it, which was the exact opposite of the cold hour."
- **The Trance reframed as a competing jurisdiction**, not a dream: "it was not a dream… This had laws… a place with its own jurisdiction… subject to it, the way a man who crosses a border is subject to the country he has entered." And the refusal of the easy exit: "He did not think *I imagined it.*"

## 5. Confirmations

- **No padding.** Every addition is scene, embodied detail, or load-bearing interiority. The test "would this still belong if the blueprint did not exist?" was applied to each; nothing was added to satisfy a target. (Ch.1 deliberately left at its floor rather than inflated.)
- **No cosmology exposition.** The supernatural is rendered through behaviour, the town's calm, and the trance's jurisdiction. Nothing is explained. "Was that real?" is never asked; in Ch.3 the easy exit is explicitly refused.
- **No memoir leakage.** No real names; no court/contact/immigration/child material; the near-death is the novel's own drowning (the stray fire references were removed). Not readable as litigation allegory.
- **Voice held** against `foundation/01`: swell-and-stop rhythm, the one-line beats, the corrected-sentence antithesis, abstraction grounded by concrete verbs, register modulation (grave / tender-domestic / uncanny-plain / lyric-trance / plain-market all present).

## 6. Does this support the full-length strategy?

**Yes.** The chapters breathe at ~2,150–2,300 without padding and without losing pressure; the town now reads as an inhabited place with impossible laws; Obianuju is human before she is claimed; the protagonist's fearlessness has a body and a lineage (the town's calm). Crucially, the expansion *added consequence and life*, not length for its own sake — proving the foundation can carry the novel to ~90k through scene. The opening still functions as a submission opening: Ch.1 could make an agent stop reading everything else.

The strategy note holds: not every early chapter must hit 3,000; the opening seduces and establishes authority, and later chapters will carry the larger scene-structures as the plot widens. Cumulative Movement I tracking begins here (Chs 1–3 = 6,923 of the ~29,000 Movement I target).

## Chapter function

Ch.6 introduces the Elder as the Iyalode-function figure without making her an oracle. She:

- sees the pressure without naming it as a genre mechanism;
- refuses to make Obianuju wicked;
- refuses to tell him to stay or leave;
- gives him one sentence he cannot yet use;
- holds the church and older knowledge in one ordinary household;
- treats the supernatural as weather and work, not spectacle.

## Ordinary action

The Elder's authority is carried through ordinary acts:

- shelling melon seeds;
- receiving groundnuts;
- moving a Bible away from the edge of the stool;
- checking a pot before it boils over;
- adjusting a wrapper;
- hanging a rosary, key, and red cloth together without explanation;
- sending him home with beans.

These acts keep the chapter ancestral and domestic, not prophetic.

## Obianuju protection

The chapter explicitly refuses to make Obianuju a monster. Mama Nneoma grants both truths:

- Obianuju loves him.
- Love does not make him first.

The Elder's strongest protection of Obianuju is: `Because your wife is not a road I can send you down or call you back from. She is a person.`

That line keeps Obianuju from becoming warning, weather, or prize.

### Acceptance-Gate Sentence

> *He had never asked her where she went. He had called it courtesy, respect, love. But the trance had shown him something he could not unsee: she was not merely absent during the hour. She was present somewhere else. She was watching a door. And the door was not for him.*

**Verdict: ACCEPTED — per editorial ruling.**

This sentence does four things at once:
1. Corrects the protagonist's earlier misreading (courtesy, respect, love → avoidance)
2. Gives Obianuju interior life inside the hour (she is present somewhere else)
3. Turns the door into a future engine (she is watching a door)
4. Shifts the novel from "where does she go?" to "what is she waiting for?"

The previously reported evidence/escalation sentence remains structurally important but is not the acceptance-gate sentence:

> *He had brought something back. He did not know what it meant. He did not know whether it was a gift or a warning or a receipt for a debt he had not known he owed. But he knew that the trance had never left him with a physical mark before.*

This sentence proves the Trance has become material. The acceptance-gate sentence carries the chapter's deepest revelation.

### Acceptance-Gate Sentence

> *"The cold does not care who lives there. It only cares that someone does. It wants a life to attach itself to. It wants to continue."*

**Verdict: ACCEPTED — under one condition per editorial ruling.**

This sentence is accepted only if it remains testimony, not doctrine. It works because it belongs to the shrimp seller's lived knowledge, trauma, memory, and partial understanding. It must not become the narrator's explanation of the system. Future chapters must not repeat "it wants to continue" as a slogan. The sentence should haunt the book, not define it.

The sentence carries:
- Kene's return (he brought the protagonist to her)
- Social widening (someone outside the house knows the history)
- The protagonist's first investigation (he came to ask)
- Visible external stakes (the cold wants to continue — to reach into the future)
- The cold is no longer only private (it has happened before, to other people)
- The shift from endurance into action (the protagonist must now choose)

It is the shrimp seller's voice — plain, ancestral, unadorned. It does not explain the cosmology. It states what the cold wants, in the plainest possible terms.

## Special Risk Checks

### Risk 1 — Exposition Through Investigation

**PASS.** The shrimp seller does not explain the cosmology. She tells her story. She provides rumour, remembered detail, warning, practical knowledge, and partial evidence — never a system. Kene does not interpret. The protagonist does not receive answers.

### Risk 2 — Commercial Gear Change Becoming Genre Machinery

**PASS.** The gear change is achieved through visible consequence (salt on his hand), human exchange (Kene's yard, the market, the doorstep), social pressure (the house has a history), and action with cost (he moves, but the movement is partial and wrong). No villain reveal, chosen-one energy, heroic confrontation, spirit lecture, cliffhanger, or melodrama.

### Risk 3 — Obianuju Becoming Plot Object

**PASS.** Obianuju has multiple moments on her own terms:
- Her laugh in the kitchen yard (Ch.9) — ordinary joy that belongs to her
- In the trance (Ch.9) — she has her own purpose, her own waiting
- Her confession (Ch.10) — her own story, her own voice
- "I have been trying to find out ever since" (Ch.10) — she is actively investigating her own condition
- She knows what continuation means before he can finish the sentence (Ch.10)

## Recommendation

**Ch.10 accepted as rewritten. Movement I locked. Ready for Ch.11 — The Reversal.**

### Movement II Governing Standard

Every chapter in Movement II must exact a cost. Not atmosphere. Cost. Each chapter must make something harder after it happens. If a chapter does not cost anyone anything, it is not a Movement II chapter.

### Immediate Priority: Ch.11 — The Reversal (~3,300 words)

The reversal must be rendered as grief, pressure, and something done *to* Obianuju — not sudden cruelty. The reader must feel wounded by her turn, not invited to judge her. The question is not "Why is she turning against him?" but "What has the pressure made love unable to carry?"

### Future Chapter Laws (from editorial ruling)

1. Beauty must now carry consequence. No ornamental beauty.
2. The protagonist must keep acting — from flaw, not wisdom.
3. Obianuju must become more alive, not more symbolic.
4. Kene must remain a man, not a device.
5. The social world must widen chapter by chapter.
6. The cold must remain implicit. Never write a cosmology lecture.
7. The Trance must remain messenger — precise location, present tense, one governing action, one new consequence.
8. The Voice must be withheld until it can alter the book.
9. Emotional register must vary. The reader needs humour, tenderness, appetite, ordinary irritation.
10. Every chapter needs an acceptance-gate sentence that could only belong to this author, this protagonist, this Obianuju, this town, this pressure, this book.

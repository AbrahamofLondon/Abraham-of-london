## Re-Audit Results

### 1. Kene — Does he truly challenge?

**Verdict: PASS.**

Kene refuses to let the protagonist's self-story stand unchallenged. He:
- Disagrees: "You had proof. Real proof. Something you could touch and taste. And you hung it back on the line."
- Refuses to flatter endurance: "My question is not whether you can leave. My question is whether you want to."
- Exposes the difference between staying and wisdom: "I am asking whether you are staying because you love her, or because you have decided that leaving would make you the kind of man who leaves."
- Names the cost: "You are going to stay in that house until the cold has taken everything it wants, and you will call it faithfulness while it does."
- The protagonist defends himself and tries to escape the question; Kene pulls him back: "Sit down. There is someone you need to see."
- The exchange creates pressure without cruelty. Kene's voice sharpens "not with anger, but with the effort of saying a thing he had been holding back for months."

### 2. Shrimp Woman — Does she become a person, not evidence?

**Verdict: PASS.**

She is emotionally and physically present:
- She resists speaking. Her hands are shaking. She presses them flat against her thighs. The gesture is "so private that he almost looked away."
- The memory costs her: "I have not spoken of it since. Not to anyone. Not even to myself, not in words." Her knuckles whiten.
- Specific lived details: the progression (front room → hour → bed), the hands that kept working, the cold above her child — "It was standing where a father would stand."
- She remains dignified. She does not weep. She does not beg.
- She avoids explaining the system. She says what happened. She does not name the cold.
- "It only waited for the next warmth" lands as testimony, not doctrine. It is her voice, her trauma, her partial understanding.

### 3. Obianuju's Confession — Does it wound the marriage?

**Verdict: PASS.**

The strongest scene in the rewritten chapter:
- The protagonist asks directly: "When did you know?"
- She resists, looking at the no-colour door, deciding "how much of the truth she could say without breaking something between them."
- The truth arrives late enough to wound: the first week.
- He feels it as theft of choice: "You took it from me."
- She remains understandable: "Because I was afraid that if I told you, you would stay." She knew him well enough to know a warning would make him plant his feet.
- The reversal of expectation: "Not that I would leave. That I would stay."
- She does not defend. She says "I know" twice — tired, not defiant.
- The reader feels: *She should have told him. But I understand why she could not.*

### 4. Stakes — Are they felt, not merely understood?

**Verdict: PASS.**

Stakes are embodied through:
- Kene's question (motivation as problem)
- Shrimp woman's physical reaction (the body remembers)
- The child in the shrimp woman's story (specific, not depicted)
- Obianuju's first-week knowledge (the marriage built on withheld truth)
- The closing movement (he leaves; the cold stays; it has been waiting thirty years)

### 5. Protagonist Action — Does he act now?

**Verdict: PASS.**

- He physically leaves the house. He walks to the front door, opens it, steps out.
- The chapter ends in movement: "And he walked."
- The action arises from his flaw: he leaves not because he is wise, but because he cannot sit still anymore.
- It creates momentum into Ch.11. The Mama Nneoma encounter is preserved.
- The no-colour door material is preserved and now functions as pressure toward the door he must approach elsewhere.

## Acceptance-Gate Sentence

> *He walked through the dark toward Mama Nneoma's compound. He did not rehearse the question. He did not know what he would say when he arrived. He only knew that he had spent months proving he could sit in the cold, and that tonight, for the first time, he had chosen to leave it behind him.*

**Verdict: ACCEPTED.**

The sentence carries action, movement, the refusal to wait, the shift from endurance to agency, and the book's cold/staying architecture. It preserves ambiguity (he does not know what he will say) while carrying the pivot.

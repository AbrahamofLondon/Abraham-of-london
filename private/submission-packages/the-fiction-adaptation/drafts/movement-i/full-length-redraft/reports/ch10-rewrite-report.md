## What Changed in the Kene Scene

**Old:** Kene listened comfortably. He received the story, asked one question ("Are you sure you were not dreaming"), and moved on to the shrimp woman. His challenge was mild.

**New:** Kene is a pressure scene. Key changes:

1. **The net is tangled.** He is not working with easy rhythm — he is worrying a knot. The repair metaphor is inverted: he cannot fix this.
2. **He does not work while listening.** His hands stay still. The stillness is worse than any question.
3. **He challenges the protagonist's self-story directly.** "You had proof. Real proof. Something you could touch and taste. And you hung it back on the line."
4. **He forces the question Kene must ask:** "I am asking whether you are staying because you love her, or because you have decided that leaving would make you the kind of man who leaves. Because those are different things."
5. **He names the flaw without cruelty.** "You are going to stay in that house until the cold has taken everything it wants, and you will call it faithfulness while it does."
6. **He refuses comfort.** The protagonist stands up to escape the question; Kene pulls him back: "Sit down. There is someone you need to see."

Kene remains a full character — practical, loyal, afraid for his friend, sharp enough to tell the truth. He is not cruel. He is not a plot device. He is a friend who refuses to let endurance pass for wisdom.

## What Changed in the Shrimp Woman Scene

**Old:** She delivered information calmly. The reader received the history but did not feel the wound.

**New:** The revelation is emotionally costly. Key changes:

1. **Physical reaction.** Her hands are shaking. She presses them flat against her thighs to still them. The gesture is private and involuntary.
2. **Reluctance.** She says she has not spoken of it in thirty years — "Not to anyone. Not even to myself, not in words."
3. **Specific remembered detail.** She describes the progression (front room → hour → bed) with the exactness of trauma. "My hands kept working. That was the worst part. My hands kept working while I was gone."
4. **The child moment is specific and restrained.** "I woke one night and the cold was not above me. It was above her. It was standing where a father would stand."
5. **The continuation line is softened through image.** The old line "It wants to continue" is preserved but preceded by: "It did not miss us when we left. That was the worst of it. It only waited for the next warmth."
6. **Dignity, not melodrama.** Her eyes are dry, but "the effort of keeping them dry was visible in the set of her jaw."
7. **The protagonist does not thank her.** He cannot. The word would be too small.

The continuation line remains testimony, not doctrine. It belongs to her lived experience. It is not repeated as a slogan.

## What Changed in the Obianuju Scene

**Old:** She confessed too easily. The revelation did not cost enough.

**New:** The scene is restrained rupture. Key changes:

1. **The protagonist asks directly.** "When did you know?"
2. **She resists.** She looks at the no-colour door, deciding how much truth she can say without breaking something.
3. **She admits she knew in the first week.** Not later. The first week.
4. **The withheld truth is framed as fear, not deception.** "Because I was afraid that if I told you, you would stay."
5. **The reversal of expectation is devastating.** Not "I was afraid you would leave" — "I was afraid you would stay." She knew him well enough to know that a warning would make him plant his feet.
6. **He names the cost.** "You took it from me." (The choice.)
7. **She does not defend.** She says "I know" twice. She does not make excuses. She is tired, not defiant.
8. **She gives him the choice now.** "I did not take your choice. I delayed it. The choice is still yours. It has always been yours. You just did not know you were making it."

She remains sympathetic throughout. Her concealment comes from knowing him too well, not from deception. The reader should think: *She should have told him. But I understand why she could not.*

## How the Stakes Are Now Felt

The stakes are no longer intellectual. They are felt through:

1. **Kene's question:** "Are you staying for love or for the fear of being the man who leaves?" — this makes the protagonist's motivation a problem, not a given.
2. **The shrimp woman's hands shaking** — the physical cost of remembering.
3. **The child in the shrimp woman's story** — not depicted, but present as the reason they left. The cold wanted "the next life in the house."
4. **Obianuju's confession** — she knew in the first week. Every day since has been a day of withheld truth. The marriage is now costly.
5. **The protagonist's action** — he does not wait for morning. He leaves that night.

The reader feels that if nothing changes, something ordinary and future-facing will be taken. The stake is continuation, not spectacle.

## What Present-Tense Action the Protagonist Takes Before the End

**Old:** He decides to go to Mama Agnes in the morning. He sits with his hands not open.

**New:** He does not wait for morning. He walks through the dark toward Mama Agnes's compound that night.

The chapter ends with him in motion:

> *He walked through the dark toward Mama Agnes's compound. He did not rehearse the question. He did not know what he would say when he arrived. He only knew that he had spent months proving he could sit in the cold, and that tonight, for the first time, he had chosen to leave it behind him.*

The no-colour door material is preserved (he touches the handle, does not open it) but now functions as pressure toward the door he must approach elsewhere, not merely a refusal to open the one in front of him.

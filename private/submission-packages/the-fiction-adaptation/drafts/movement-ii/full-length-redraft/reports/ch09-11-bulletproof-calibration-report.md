## Chapter 10 — changes

- **Fix 1 (cold's mind):** "And I think the cold knows that. I think the cold is counting on it." → **"And I think you are doing exactly what it wants."** Diagnoses his behaviour, not the cold's psychology.
- **Fix 2 (writerly line):** "Not even to myself, not in words." → **"I told myself I had forgotten."** She sounds like a woman carrying memory, not a novelist arranging language.
- **Fix 3 (child detail):** added one physical detail to the shrimp woman's testimony — the child's breath "rising off her in little clouds into air that had not been cold until I reached it," the hands "cold like a thing that had already been held." Her history, not the protagonist's future fatherhood; not memoir-coded.
- **Fix 4 (room for Obianuju's line):** added a preparing beat before "Because I was afraid that if I told you, you would stay." — her hand flat on the window ledge, the look away to the no-colour door, "the voice she kept for the things that cost her the most." The line then lands.
- **Fix 5 (closing tightened): YES, tightened.** "He only knew that he had spent months proving he could sit in the cold, and that tonight, for the first time, he had chosen to leave it behind him." → **"He only knew that he had to walk."** Read-aloud judgment: the chapter has already dramatised the realisation; the original restated it as summary. The tighter line ends on intent and sets up the final beat ("The cold stayed in the house. / And he walked."). Ends on movement, not summary.
- **Kept (untouched):** Kene's love-vs-"needing-not-to-be-the-man-who-leaves" challenge; "you had proof… and you hung it back on the line"; "It did not miss us when we left. That was the worst of it."; the walk toward Mama Nneoma.

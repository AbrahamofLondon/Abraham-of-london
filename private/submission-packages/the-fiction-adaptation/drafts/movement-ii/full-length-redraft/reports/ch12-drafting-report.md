## How the chapter opens with social life

It opens in the market with the pepper woman — not the cold, not Ch.11's wound. The protagonist is doing the market errand that is Obianuju's domain (a Ch.11 consequence: she asked for space), and he does it badly. The pepper woman performs being robbed, mocks his haggling, taxes him a free tomato ("the tax the town levied on anyone it had decided to keep"). The town is loud, hungry, alive.

## Where warmth / humour / appetite appears

- **Warmth & humour:** the pepper woman's affectionate insults and the haggling lesson ("You know what it is. You do not know what it costs. That is a different education."); Kene eating smoked fish "with the focused dishonesty of a man pretending he had not bought it to comfort himself."
- **Appetite:** tomatoes, pepper "sharp enough to make the eyes of strangers water," smoked fish, the overpayment as "rent on a thing he could not have named."
- The warmth is not pasted on; it is the thing the chapter then makes the reader pay for ("That was the warmth. He should have left inside it.").

## Secondary pressure point introduced (returnable)

**Mr Stephen, the landlord** — owns the house and three others; files men under their addresses ("the husband from the lane"); has not lost a house in thirty years; he **will not cross his own threshold** (collects at the gate). His desire: keep the house occupied *and quiet*, because "a house that empties is a house that talks." He has filled the house twice before with tenants "who did not ask the town for its memory." He is practical, not prophetic — and his livelihood quietly depends on the cold staying private, which makes him a human accomplice to the claim's appetite for continuation (rhyme, never stated). He can return.

## How the town begins to respond

- The pepper woman reveals the talk has started (his visits to the shrimp woman and Mama Agnes went "past forty windows").
- A public sign of the cold: the young fish-seller whose catch "would not soften" in the heat, and the other sellers who have "drifted" their tables a hand's width clear of him over weeks — social avoidance, not spectacle.
- After his misstep: the pepper woman's warmth withdraws to mere courtesy ("Greet the house" — "the cruelest thing she had given him all day, because it was what she gave to strangers"); the lane receives him differently (late greetings, a lifted chin that is "a greeting and not a welcome," the standpipe congregation bending as he passes).

## How Ch.11's private reversal creates public consequence

He honours, and then fails, the new term of the marriage. Doing her errand (badly) is the visible sign of the reversal. When the snail woman reduces Obianuju to "the fast one… the one the cold chose," he **answers** — "You do not know her" — which tells the market there *is* a her, and that she can be wounded. The private "do not make me public" becomes a public name forming. Kene names it exactly: "She told you to stop trying to save her. This is what she meant… The saving that makes a private grief into a public name."

## How the protagonist acts and what it costs

He has learned to move but not to aim ("Movement is not the same as aim" — Kene). He asks Mr Stephen, **aloud, in the market's hearing**, who lived in the house before and why they left — turning the house's private dread into a public question ("this is the knocking," Mr Stephen says, his face closing "like a ledger shut"). Cost: Mr Stephen withdraws with a quiet warning; the pepper woman cools to courtesy; the snail woman sharpens the rumour toward Obianuju; the lane's attention changes; the house stops being private. He gained a hundred witnesses, "and not one of them was on his side."

## How Obianuju is protected from rumour reduction

She is physically absent the whole chapter and never diminished. The town's cruelty toward her is shown *as cruelty* (carried by his anguish and the reader's sympathy), never endorsed. Crucially, **the town does not have her name** — "he thanked God it did not" — so the reduction is shown to fail to fully land. Her dignity is held by his memory of the true her (the woman who "counted the tomatoes… and the snail woman's children in a single look") against the rumour's flat shape. Warmth beat protecting her: the pepper woman's "your wife is fast… she has counted the tomatoes, counted my children" — affection, not reduction.

## Kene

Present, **not softened**. He enters on his own business (selling a creel, buying twine, eating). Humour, appetite, irritation, loyalty, fear, and a refusal to be the protagonist's witness on demand ("The moment I become the man who confirms the cold, I become part of the cold's address. I have my own house to keep warm."). He rebukes the protagonist for answering the snail woman, then softens at the last ("Go home… do not be the one who carries it to her"), because "Kene could not stay hard at a friend any longer than he could stay angry at wood."

## How the cold remains implicit

Through social weather only: the fish that will not soften; the drifted tables; the landlord who will not enter his own house; the bending of the standpipe congregation; the lane's changed greetings. No cosmology, no naming of the Spirit Husband in prose (grep-confirmed: 0 occurrences in the body; the term appears only in the hidden drafting note).

## Motifs used (and why organic)

- **Cold** (fish, lane, withdrawal) — the claim's public weather.
- **Door / threshold** (Mr Stephen who won't cross his threshold; the final "door open between them" he cannot hang shut) — the chapter's spine image; pays off Ch.11's door/control.
- **Naming** (he withholds Obianuju's name; the town gropes toward a wrong one) — the book's naming motif made social.
- **Hands / salt / market** — the embodied town (the pepper woman's hands, salt drying on his, the basket).
- **Lateness** (he is late to the prices, late to seeing the windows) — his signature flaw in a new key.
All arise from scene, none deployed as decoration.

## Acceptance-gate sentence

> "He had wanted a witness… and that morning the town had given him a hundred witnesses, and not one of them was on his side; the town had been glad to feed him and would be just as glad, now, to feed on him and on the woman whose name it did not yet have but had already begun to sharpen toward."

It carries the social widening, the town's appetite (feed/feed-on), the first public consequence, his flawed action (he *wanted* the witness), and the private cold entering public life — and it protects Obianuju (the town does not yet have her name). It glows and cuts.

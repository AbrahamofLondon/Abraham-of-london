## Structure
- **Opens from the knock.** Confirmed — begins on the Ch.13 cliff: the cup that "did not come back to life," the ordinary humiliation of waiting to be admitted to "the place where your own clothes are folded," a neighbour with a ladder who "decided he had not seen it." No summary, no leap, no opening reflection.
- **How the threshold scene functions.** The door becomes house-threshold + marriage-threshold + the line between welcome and right. She lets him in and it changes nothing, because "a door is not the only threshold a house contains." The folded shirt (her care outliving her welcome) and the cup set in "the neutral country" of the table — present, reachable, offered to neither — render altered access as objects, not statements.
- **The new term entering the marriage.** *Do not speak for me outside this house; do not use my silence as permission; the hour is mine; you are not to follow me to the door I watch, nor carry my quiet into the lane and call it your right to know.* Capped by the line: **"being let in is not the same as being let through."**
- **Obianuju active and sympathetic.** She is the chapter's agent, never merely affected: she shut the door; she pours water and withholds the push; she **closes the no-colour door** (re-taking "the one thing I could control" she lost in Ch.11); she names the terms under which she survives. Sympathy held by the *almost* — her hand rising from two years' habit toward the salt on his knuckles and stopping ("the almost was worse than a refusal… a refusal is a decision and the almost was a grief").
- **Protagonist misreads, then partly understands.** He enters certain he has acted, become brave, protected her ("I was defending you"). He discovers **explanation is not repair** ("a man telling the lock how much he deserves to be on the other side of it"). He partly understands by the end and is still not wise — wounded and wrong, exactly as briefed.
- **The Elder sentence returns through consequence.** Not quoted into the threshold scene; it returns when the closed door drives him to Mama Nneoma, who **reframes its burden** without explaining cosmology: he is not late to *her* (he walked through her journal-door on time, "the only thing in your life you have ever been on time for") — he is late to *the other thing*, which "arrived first… has been surviving without you… does not want your help." Welcome reframed: "being let stand in the warm part of a house that is not yours to command." (The thematic turn — late not to love but to ownership of what already survives without him — is rendered through her idiom of a guest moving the host's furniture, never stated baldly.)
- **Mama Nneoma handling.** Practical, not oracular: refuses pity, makes him drink ("Do not call your refusal of water humility"), sits him on the low stool by design, asks what he did after being welcomed, completes the sentence by changing its burden. **Human counter-note:** the dry bicycle-chain exchange ("he will go happier than you, because he has made his peace with the chain. You are still insulted that yours exists.").
- **Ch.13 town/Obiefuna carryover.** Felt, not staged: the neighbour at the doorway; Obiefuna's "a house that becomes loud is a house he must fill again" made intimate; Mama Nneoma's "the landlord and the cold have both walked into your marriage without once crossing a threshold." The town presses against the walls.
- **Where warmth/humour/human counter-note appears.** The bicycle-chain beat; Obianuju's almost-gesture toward his hand; the corner of her mouth moving ("the old hospitality… across the door she had shut"); the one true small chore he completes (putting the shirt away).
- **How the cold remains implicit.** The warm-but-shut wood; the cup going quiet; the no-colour door; the warmth leaving "toward the higher ground" as the hour comes; the cold coming "in low along the floor." Never named (grep: 0 "Spirit Husband" in prose).
- **Trance:** absent. **Voice:** absent. (Neither required here.)
- **Motifs (organic, deepened):** **door/threshold** (the spine — ajar-door now shut, the no-colour door re-closed by her, the handle he does not try); **the cup** (offered/ withheld — the Ch.5 push reversed); **hands/salt** (her almost-tending of the standpipe salt); **welcome/late** (the Elder sentence made literal); **the hour** (he no longer follows her into it); **naming** ("greet the room… the one that does not answer").

## Acceptance
- **Acceptance-gate sentence:**
  > "I let you in because you are my husband and the door was warm and I am not made of the cold. But being let in is not the same as being let through."
  It could belong only to this chapter: it carries the closed door, welcome-without-entitlement, Obianuju's agency, and (through the chapter's consequence) the Elder sentence made flesh; the protagonist's partial understanding is its echo at the close, where "he did not call it love." It cuts and is neither summary nor proverb.
- **No cosmology exposition.** Confirmed — the cold is never explained; no force named; Mama Nneoma reframes a sentence, she does not define a system.
- **No memoir leakage.** Confirmed — no real names, no court/contact/immigration/child/fatherhood material, no fire. House/rent pressure is fictional and local (Obiefuna, gatepost language); no legal-case rhythm.
- **No site/`content/`/FWF/build files touched.** All changes inside `private/submission-packages/the-fiction-adaptation/`.
- **All required beats achieved:** opens on the knock ✔; Obianuju answers but not as before ✔; threshold becomes the marriage scene ✔; she stays active (closes the door, withholds the cup) ✔; he misreads then partly understands ("explanation is not repair") ✔; Elder sentence returns through consequence ✔; Mama Nneoma appears without explaining the system ✔; ends on a concrete restriction (he waits on the warm side of the shut door and does not call it love) ✔.

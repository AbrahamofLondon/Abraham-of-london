## Structure
- **Opens with restraint under pressure.** Morning after he did not follow her into the hour. The Ch.14 cup still sits "in the neutral country," gone room-temperature; he will not move it because moving it would be "to decide something." He slept against the shut door — and despises the part of himself that *wanted the restraint seen*: "Held too tightly, [restraint] began to want an audience… A man who does nothing, and waits to be praised for the nothing, has not stopped acting." Not-acting shown as also a kind of acting.
- **The bargain attempted.** Through Mr Stephen: a deadline made of weather (the house must go *quiet* before the rains break, or he fills it with the cement-road family). The protagonist spends himself in three rising offers — **more rent** (refused: "the number was never the difficulty"), **taking the blame** (refused: "A man cannot take the blame for weather. He can only stop standing in the doorway shouting at the rain"), and finally **leaving so she may stay** — which turns to ash as he hears it: "not a sacrifice. A purchase," trying to buy "the standing to dispose of where his wife would live, to hand her, like a key, to the man at the gate."
- **Why the bargain fails / changes the board.** Mr Stephen gives the deadline (the rains). Obianuju refuses the trade and sets her own term. The house stays occupied but **no longer protected by silence** — and the protagonist realises the price he offered was refused because he offered the wrong thing: himself, when the only currency was a silence he never paid. The failure is productive: it strips him to the helplessness Ch.16 must answer.
- **Mr Stephen — behaviour-carried menace.** Civility + leverage, no villainy, no name-meaning. The clean shirt, the rent book on the gatepost, the gate he will not cross, the "interest" in the house, the deadline phrased as a courtesy ("I will have done you the courtesy of telling you first"). He sounds like a man discussing ordinary business; that is the threat.
- **Obianuju — active and protected.** She refuses to be negotiated over: she comes to the threshold ("the one place in the world that was unarguably hers"), names that her husband "is offering you things that are not his to offer," and **pays Mr Stephen herself** — revealing she has paid the *true* rent (silence, competence, the keeping of a face) for two years, managing the landlord better than the husband knew. She sets her own term (the roofer to the step and no further). She is the competent party throughout; the men's bargain is exposed as her negotiation barged into. Her dignity is the chapter's spine. Her later "you were going to give me away — to keep me" lands the wound on him, not her. She leaves the door open the width she chose — "the nearest thing to tenderness the evening held, and it did not soften the boundary by a hair."
- **Kene — pressure, not shelter.** Appears first (the bread-heel haggle = appetite + humour), brings the practical news (the cement-road family, the forming deadline), and warns without comforting: "Do not bargain with that man… because of what you would offer… a thing that wants to *pay*." Refuses public witness ("I have my own house") but gives the true help — the warning, and the key line that seeds Obianuju's reveal: "the only person who has ever known what the rent really is… is the one you keep trying to protect from the conversation." Loyal, irritated, hungry, funny, afraid.
- **Mama Agnes — task, not answer; the sentence deepens.** She gives him no comfort and no noun — a **verb**: "Stay out of the way of a woman doing the thing that is keeping you both alive." She takes the locked sentence apart without doctrine: "*Late* is one thing. *Useless* is another… late is not useless… the work that is left is not the owner's work. It is the late man's work. Smaller. Lower. Unthanked." And — the chapter's quiet rhyme — she lets him leave **empty-handed** for the first time, "because empty hands were exactly the thing he had come, at last, to be ready to hold."
- **Warmth / humour / appetite.** Kene eating the unpaid bread-heel ("He is tasting… by the time I am sure it is gone, and we are even"); Mr Stephen's civility tipping toward the absurd (squaring the rent book, being "insulted" so gently it becomes a kindness); Obianuju's "I always did know which silence was the rent"; the corner of her mouth moving at the end.
- **How Ch.14's threshold term carries forward.** The cup in the neutral country; the warm side / shut door; "welcome is not ownership" now tested as *use is not ownership*; her boundary held while he learns the verb of staying out of the way.
- **How Ch.15 prepares Ch.16 without using the Voice.** All evening's bargains are conducted in words and **none settles the thing**, because the thing "dealt in something none of them had named." When he finally does the one useful thing — being present and unpaid, "no use" — the house, for the first time, **waits with him**: the cold gathers not at the threshold but in the centre of the room and *holds*, "the way a room holds when a person in it has drawn breath to speak and not yet spoken." The chapter ends: "It was a room making room. / For a voice." The conditions for intimidation are set (failed bargain, narrowed choices, the deadline, the boundary, restraint cracking into stillness) — and the Voice does **not** speak.
- **How the cold remains implicit.** The room-temperature cup; the rent book; the gate; the cold "sure of its lease"; the held silence after a practical sentence. Never named (grep: 0 "Spirit Husband" in prose; the court/legal simile flagged in QA was removed — 0 court/fire).
- **Trance:** absent. **Voice:** absent (prepared, not used).
- **Motifs (organic):** **the cup** (the neutral country, undecided, uncrossed); **rent / silence-as-currency** (the chapter's engine — "I always did know which silence was the rent"); **threshold / gate / door** (Mr Stephen's gatepost, Obianuju's doorway, the door left open her width); **the warm side**; **late vs useless** (the Elder sentence split); **the held breath / room making room** (the new motif that hands off to Ch.16).

## Acceptance

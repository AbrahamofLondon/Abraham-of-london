## Structure
- **Begins in held silence.** The rent book and the bread heel sit together on the table — "a deadline and a breakfast." He has done Mama Agnes's verb (being no use) through the evening and night; the cost has bought "a truce… not warmth… the kind two people keep when both are too tired to fight." The fatal beat: "A man who thinks he has learned the lesson has stopped watching for the next one."
- **Ordinary life's last attempt.** Kene brings unasked-for palm oil and will **not sit** (new) and will **not finish** "I do not like to be in this house when the weather changes," and — for the first time — does not say his old refrain *if it gets worse, come*. Then a full hour of ordinary pre-rain maintenance (Obianuju checking the roof seam, the high shelf, the catch-pot; he practising not-helping) with the rent book "keeping its time by not keeping it." The Voice interrupts *ordinary maintenance*, exactly as the brief required, not supernatural anticipation.
- **The Voice speaks — plain, brief, un-located, no italics** (italics remain reserved for trance; the Voice is rendered in plain double quotes, which is why it is terrifying). It comes when he stands to help with the shutter and steps toward the no-colour door — his nature reaching for a task, and under that, the need to "go through."
  - **First line:** **"Leave the door alone."** — "said at the volume a person uses across a kitchen, in the flat tone of a man telling another man a fact about a tool… there was no one in the house to have said it."
  - **Second (and last) line:** **"Your courage is not the price. She is."**
  - The Voice **explains nothing** — not the cold, not the system, not what is behind the door. It names a cost and draws a line.
- **The protagonist's fearlessness is the danger.** He does not respond with fear (he has none); he responds with *relief* — "You spoke to me" / "I am not afraid of you" — and takes the step. He mistakes being addressed for being chosen: "his whole starved usefulness had stood up inside him and called it honour." The chapter then exposes the curse inside the gift: "a man who cannot be frightened cannot be deterred by the one threat that would stop a wiser man — the threat to himself," so the thing reaches past him and names *her*.
- **Obianuju active, not a prize.** She returns unheard, puts her hand on his arm, and **stops him** — "Do not answer it… It is not interested in your courage… It spoke to you because you were about to cost me something." She has lived with it speaking "longer than you have lived in this house"; she is "past being frightened." She knows the danger before he does, refuses to explain more than she chooses, keeps her boundary, and gives him — as a gift, not a weapon — the truth and the one task left him: "You hold still, and you let me be the one who knows the price." She restores the room object by object. She has agency throughout; the Voice speaks *about* the cost to her, but she is the one who acts.
- **The cost named, not explained.** A new contract enters the book — not a bargain (Ch.15) but a **warning**: his next action will cost her. How, the Voice does not say. The reader feels a line drawn.
- **The hinge.** The room returns to ordinary temperature the instant the rain breaks — "and that was the worst of it." Being addressed was **exposure, not honour**. Before this chapter the pressure could be endured; after it, endurance is dangerous, because the one move his nature demands (to act, to enter, to save) is the move that makes her pay. The second half is now unavoidable. The chapter ends on the debt "named and not yet called, sitting in the house between them like a third resident… entirely sure that the man who could not be frightened would, in time, reach for the door again."
- **Cold distinct from the Voice.** Made explicit: "the cold was the house, the cold was the lease… but the cold had never, in two years, *said* anything. The cold claimed. The thing that had spoken was not the cold." Claim prepares the room; the Voice intimidates. The forces are not merged.
- **Trance:** does not appear. **New named characters:** none.
- **Human counter-note.** Kene's bread/oil and his dry "the same sentence a poor man and a holy man both say, and only one of them means it"; the absurd domesticity of rent book, stale bread, palm oil, and terror sharing one table; Obianuju's ordinary competence under the coming rain.
- **Motifs (organic, deepened):** **the door** (the no-colour door — the Voice's command names the book's spine image and the protagonist's exact temptation); **the rent book / the rains** (Mr Stephen's deadline becoming literal weather — "The sky had begun to keep the appointment"); **water/drowning** (the warning-image recast onto his own element — "the lip of a well… the drowning would be loud"; "nearer the water than you do"); **the cup** (the undecided neutral country, carried from Ch.14–15); **held breath / room making room** (Ch.15's hand-off paid off — the room *had* made room, and the room spoke).

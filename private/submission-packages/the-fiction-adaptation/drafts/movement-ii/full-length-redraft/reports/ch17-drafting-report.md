## Structure
- **Opens after the Voice, not with it.** Morning after rain; the rent book "dry, square, patient" with a bead of damp beside it. The Voice's silence is the new fact: "a man can brace against a thing that speaks; there is nothing to set the shoulder against in a thing that has said its piece and gone quiet." The line *Your courage is not the price. She is.* is **not repeated** as dialogue — it has "stopped being a thing he remembered and become a thing he lived inside." No supernatural echo in the opening.
- **Ordinary life attempts recovery.** The rinsed, cheerful lane the morning after the first big rain (the drowned hens, the scrubbed child); "the world, having heard nothing in the night that he had heard, went cheerfully on." Obianuju prices the roof leak (the wept seam over the back room). The world does not stop — "that is part of the terror."
- **Restraint tested — and the error exposed.** The leak is over the back room, under the no-colour door's lintel; the old him volunteers brightly to "look at it" (= climb a chair in the forbidden doorway and call it fixing the roof). He restrains — and **performs the restraint**: hands laid open "where the openness could be observed," sitting back down "with a slowness… meant to be witnessed." The chapter names it: "He was doing nothing, and he was doing it at her." **Kene** exposes it ("You have found a way to make sitting still into the loudest thing in the room… You have only changed its coat") and forces the distinction: "Doing nothing is not the same as wisdom… only one of them is honest about which it is. You have to know which yours is." **Mama Agnes** deepens it without proverb-machining: "Empty hands are not clean hands… You are counting your own stillness like coins." By the close he reaches the **un-performed** version (below).
- **Restraint vs performance — held throughout.** The arc: performed restraint (witnessed, waged) → exposed by Kene and Mama Agnes → real restraint at the end (un-witnessed, un-waged: "his hands open and unwatched, wanting nothing to be noticed").
- **Obianuju more alive, not less.** She prices the repair; reads Mr Stephen's lever exactly ("He wants me to have to choose between the floorboards and the door, and he wants the choosing to be loud"); **refuses Brother Paul the back room** up to the man on the roof ("The floor is mine to lose… I would rather lose a floor than open that room to the lane"); makes the decision the protagonist does not control; and at the end **opens the forbidden door herself** (her domain, her price) to deal with the leak. Plus fatigue and tenderness-with-boundary: she restores the small two-finger "push" of the cup as *habit, not forgiveness* — "the smallest possible amount of their old life, kept alive by a tired woman." She is the price not because she is passive but because *his* wrong action would make her pay — that distinction is held explicitly.
- **Kene — human counter-pressure and breath.** Appetite/humour (the unpaid groundnuts; "I will deny you in the market in front of children"); the impossible middle ("a third thing, between the brave fool and the still fool… no audience and no name"); practical menace-reading ("A landlord who sends his own roofer… is not thinking about the roof. Do not let his man past the step"); and — paying off Ch.16's withheld refrain — he finishes the sentence he could not finish: "If it gets worse, come… there will be room." Loyal, irritated, hungry, afraid.
- **Mr Stephen's deadline tightens.** A half-sheet under a stone on the gatepost: the rains have started; he is sending **Brother Paul**, who "will want the inside as well as the out"; "A quiet house is easy to keep in repair… Let us keep it so." Civility plus leverage — the repair the rains make urgent, the access it makes reasonable, the quiet the access endangers, the deadline the weather counts. And the productive cost: by refusing the back room, Obianuju *confirms* to Mr Stephen that there is "a room its tenants would lose a floor before they would open" — "He had his lever now, cleaner than before."
- **Mama Agnes — felt, sparing, practical.** No explanation of the Voice; she gives the chapter's hardest reframing (empty ≠ clean hands; counting stillness like coins) and a task harder than stillness: "the standing-by while she does the dangerous thing, with your hands open and not on display."
- **Warmth/humour/appetite.** The drowned-hens exchange; Kene's groundnuts and market threat; the restored cup-push; Brother Paul "who liked roofs better than people," happy up the ladder "talking down… about the wickedness of last year's nails."
- **No new Voice line.** Confirmed — the Voice does not speak; one *memory/presence* echo ("not spoken, not repeated, only present… leave the door alone") at the moment of temptation, rendered lowercase, explicitly not an utterance. **No Trance.** Confirmed.
- **Cold distinct from the Voice.** The cold is climate (the cheer "lay in the room without warming it"; the debt "certain of its lease, sure, the way the cold was sure"); the Voice is the silence it left. Never named (grep: 0 "Spirit Husband").
- **Motifs (organic):** **the door** (the forbidden no-colour door — he does not touch it; she opens it); **the rent book** (Mr Stephen's clock, "counting", paired on the table with his note — "a small archive of the ways a house can be owned"); **rain/leak/the pot/water** (the deadline made weather; the elemental register kept to water, not fire); **the cup** (the restored two-finger push); **hands** (open/unwatched vs displayed — the chapter's spine).

## Acceptance
- **Acceptance-gate sentence:**
  > "…a man in a chair, holding still while the woman he loved opened the door he had been forbidden and dealt with the weather, and the keeping-still cost him everything the walking-in never had, and bought nothing, and was witnessed by no one, and was, at last, the truest thing he had ever done."

## Audit Verdicts

### 1. Practical Pressure Opening
**Verdict: PASS.**

The chapter opens with the pot under the seam full before the kettle boils. The pressure is roof, rain, floorboard, repair, and habitability. It does not begin as a supernatural event.

### 2. Repair/Access Problem Creates the Test
**Verdict: PASS.**

The core problem is exactly the right one: the room that most needs repair is the room no one can safely open. Brother Paul functions as practical repair pressure and public witness, not as a symbolic device. Mr Stephen is felt through repairability, quietness, rent, weather, and the social consequences of an uninspectable room.

### 3. Obianuju Acts First
**Verdict: PASS.**

Obianuju decides the access terms, has already sent for Brother Paul, assigns the protagonist permitted tasks, enters the room on her own terms, handles the pot/rope/repair, pays Brother Paul herself, relocates the rent book, and controls what may be spoken afterward. She remains practical, active, tired, dignified, and unfinished.

She does not become the object of his restraint.

### 4. Protagonist Test
**Verdict: PASS.**

The test is not inactivity. He holds the ladder, keeps his mouth when public translation would steal her refusal, holds the rope at the exact tension she asks for, takes the smaller basin, pours water where instructed, and oils his own palms. The chapter asks whether he can be accurate when usefulness cannot look heroic.

### 5. Trance Law
**Verdict: PASS.**

The Trance obeys the rules:
- italic present tense;
- precise, not hazy;
- a place with jurisdiction, not a dream;
- one governing action: standing outside while holding a rope;
- no explanation;

It does not become montage, prophecy, lore, or spectacle.

## Structure
- **Opening before inspection:** the chapter opens with Obianuju moving the cup, folding Mr Stephen's note into the rent book, arranging chairs, setting the wedges aside, and preparing the house to be seen. The first pressure is presentation, not supernatural event.
- **Mr Stephen arrives correctly:** he arrives on time, courteous, careful, with a narrow notebook rather than drama. His danger is ordinary business: politeness, property language, responsibility, and quiet attention.
- **Obianuju controls the inspection:** she decides the sequence of entry, controls the front-room threshold, clarifies what may be seen, corrects the written record, and prevents the closed room from becoming a man's narrative about disorder or refusal.
- **The protagonist fails in a new way:** he opens the gate before Obianuju asks, then answers for the quiet in a way that turns privacy into a promise. He is partly better than before but still wrong: his usefulness makes the inspection easier and therefore makes the room more visible.
- **Practical consequence created:** Mr Stephen gives the room a condition. After three dry days, Brother Paul is to assess the board from the threshold; if that is not enough, the room must be opened before the next full rain. The no-colour door now has a date attached to it.

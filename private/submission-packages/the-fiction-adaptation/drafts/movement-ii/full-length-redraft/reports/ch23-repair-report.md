## Fix 1 — Protagonist Consequential Action

**Added:** He draws a second chalk line beside Brother Paul's line.

This is:
- **Specific:** He picks up the chalk Brother Paul left behind and draws a second line on the wet wood.
- **Visible:** Brother Paul sees it the next day and erases it.
- **Irreversible:** The line is erased, but the act cannot be undone. Brother Paul now knows someone is marking the wood.
- **Morally mixed:** He does not know why he does it. It is not heroism. It is not wisdom. It is the impulse of a man who has been waiting too long and finally moves without knowing what the movement means.

Kene's response: "That is the problem with people who are not used to acting. When they finally act, they do it without knowing what the action means."

Kene also advises him not to tell Obianuju: "She has enough to carry without adding your experiments."

This prevents the action from becoming a clean redemption arc. The protagonist acts, but the action is wrong, and he knows it.

## Fix 2 — Elder Sentence Restored

**Added after Obianuju's diagnosis of the wrong key:**

> *You can be welcomed and still be late.*
>
> The sentence came back to him without ceremony. Not as comfort. Not as wisdom. As accusation. He had been late to the house, late to the door, late to the cost, and now even his waiting had begun to arrive late. The house had accepted the wrong key while he was still learning how to hold the right one.

The sentence arrives as pressure inside action — after he realises the wrong key has changed the house, before he chooses what to do. It is not explained. It is not sermon. It cuts once and moves on.

## Fix 3 — Warm Human Beat Added

**Added after the rain scene, before the thread diagnosis:**

He sits down across from Obianuju and shells beans with her. He has never done this before — not once in two years. He cracks the first shell badly. She shows him the seam, the pressure, the place where the thumb finds the natural break. He tries again. The second shell cracks cleanly. She nods once.

The beat does three things:
1. **Gives the reader breath** — a moment of ordinary domestic rhythm before the pressure returns
2. **Shows Obianuju alive beyond the room** — she is teaching, not suffering
3. **Teaches the protagonist through ordinary action** — he learns "shape" and "handling" without sermon

The cold recedes briefly: "for a few minutes the house was only a house and the cold was only a word he had used too often."

## Fix 5 — Obianuju's Solution Made Costly

The solution is no longer a clean trick. Obianuju chooses a photograph — a piece of her history, surrendered to be found. She does not show it to the protagonist. She holds it the way she held the thread: "not with fear, not with anger, but with the attention of a woman who had learned that small things could carry more weight than large ones."

The cost is visible:
- She removes it from the cloth pouch where it has been hidden
- She places it on the table where it can be found
- She does not explain what it shows
- The protagonist understands that asking would make her explain, and explaining would cost her more than the giving already has

The hierarchy is correct: the protagonist acts (the chalk line), his action reveals a possible path (he went to the woman with the blue door), Obianuju understands the cost before he does, and she decides what to give.

---

## Fix 6 — Ending Rewritten with Consequence Now

The old ending pointed to "in the morning, she would tell him." The new ending lands now:

- The photograph is on the table. The red thread is beside it.
- The protagonist understands that she is choosing among losses.
- He does not ask to see the photograph.
- He goes to the no-colour door, touches the handle, does not open it.
- He returns to the front room.
- The photograph and thread remain on the table.
- The final line: "And in the morning, Mr Stephen would receive something he had not asked for — a piece of the house, surrendered, not because it would satisfy him, but because it would slow him down long enough for the house to decide what to do next."

The ending lands with an object (the photograph), a plan chosen (the surrender), and a cost accepted (her history given away). The next chapter is unavoidable because the photograph has already been placed on the table.

## How the Chapter Opens with Story Already Altered

The chapter opens on the fourth day with Kene arriving before the market has fully woken. The pepper woman has invented a false version of the story — that the old tenant died in the back room, and that is why the house is cold. The story has reached the fish-seller, the oil-seller, the tailor, and the boys at the wall. The protagonist sees the market reacting to him differently — people looking, then looking away.

The opening proves: the story is no longer theirs. It has been altered, amplified, and turned into something that brings Mr Stephen back before the week is up.

---

## How the Story Has Left the Photograph

The story has fully detached from the photograph. The pepper woman has never seen the photograph. She does not need to. She has a piece of a story and has finished it the way the town finishes things — by making it complete enough to carry. The false version (death in the back room) is now the dominant story. The true version (a woman left, the cold stayed) is no longer relevant.

---

## How Obianuju Chooses What Not to Correct

Obianuju decides to let the wrong story travel. She says: "We let the wrong story travel. We do not correct it. We do not confirm it. We let it become the story that people carry, because the wrong story is easier to disprove than the right one."

She also decides to let Mr Stephen see the room. She says: "The photograph bought us time. The time is up."

Her control under distortion is tested and holds. She does not panic. She does not correct the false story publicly. She chooses the smaller loss (the room being seen) over the larger loss (the story growing).

---

## How the Protagonist Acts Accurately Without Owning

The protagonist:
- Tells Obianuju exactly what Kene told him, without adding or softening
- Does not correct the pepper woman's story himself
- Does not argue with Mr Stephen at the gate
- Asks Obianuju before agreeing to anything
- Opens the room for Mr Stephen without commentary
- Does not try to explain what Mr Stephen should have seen

His progress is tested publicly and holds. He repeats only what is given. He allows his silence to look like helplessness. He acts accurately and receives no reward.

---

## How Mr Stephen Uses the Travelling Story

Mr Stephen returns on day five, not day seven. He uses the false story as leverage:
- He offers to inspect the room to disprove the story
- The offer is reasonable; refusal would be unreasonable
- He admits he does not believe the story, but says he must be seen to act
- He reveals genuine curiosity: "I came myself because I wanted to see whether the story had changed you"
- He says he will not ask for the photograph again, but this is not a gift — he has begun to wonder what it really means

His power: he can make their private cost look like a public management issue.

---

## How Kene Appears

Kene arrives before the market has fully woken, with no food. He:
- Brings the false story first
- Describes the social pressure (the pepper woman, the fish-seller, the tailor, the boys at the wall)
- Presses the protagonist on whether to tell Obianuju
- Reveals the woman who took the photograph was crying
- Offers to go to her and tell her that Obianuju is not angry

Kene is loyal, irritated, socially intelligent, and useful in an ungentle way. He does not rescue the chapter — he sharpens it.

## What Final-Act Turn the Chapter Creates

**Mr Stephen has seen the room, found nothing, and has begun to wonder what the photograph really means.**

The false story will die. The room is no longer protected by the photograph. Mr Stephen no longer needs the photograph — he has begun to wonder about the thing behind it. The cold has not weakened, only become quieter. The no-colour door is still closed. The next chapter must show what Mr Stephen's wondering brings.

## How the Chapter Opens with Preparation for Truth

The chapter opens with the morning of Mr Stephen's visit. The protagonist wakes before dawn. The cold is at the edges. The door is closed. The quiet is no longer the quiet of waiting — it is the quiet of preparation. Obianuju gives him one sentence he is allowed to say: "It is part of the house. It has been closed since before we arrived. We have chosen not to open it." He repeats it three times to feel the shape of it in his mouth before he needs to speak it aloud.

---

## What Truth Obianuju Tells

Obianuju tells Mr Stephen:
- She does not know what is behind the door
- She has chosen not to open it
- Some doors are not opened — they are lived beside
- The door is not dangerous to her, but she is afraid of what would happen if she opened it
- She has kept it closed because she feared she would not be able to close it again

She does not explain the cold. She does not name the Spirit Husband. She does not turn the door into a mystery. She tells the truth — the human truth — and lets it stand.

---

## How Mr Stephen Reaches His Limit

Mr Stephen arrives as a reasonable man. He asks reasonable questions. He accepts the answers he is given. He acknowledges that he has been a landlord long enough to know that some things in a house are not his to touch. He says: "I have been given the only answer I am entitled to. The door exists. It is closed. The people who live here have chosen not to open it. That is not a mystery I need to solve. It is a fact I need to accept."

He leaves without opening the door, without demanding access, without asking again. His limit is reached not through defeat but through acceptance.

---

## How the Protagonist Acts Accurately Without Ownership

The protagonist:
- Repeats the sentence Obianuju gave him
- Does not add anything
- Does not explain what he does not know
- Stands at the door during the encounter
- Does not speak unless asked
- Does not try to rescue the conversation
- Returns the photograph pouch to the shelf when asked

His growth is visible in precision, not triumph. He acts without owning the meaning.

## How Kene Appears or Is Felt

Kene arrives in the late afternoon with bread. He:
- Acknowledges that Mr Stephen will not return
- Reflects on the difference between wondering and asking
- Admits he was afraid — the first time he has said it directly
- Accepts the protagonist's admission of fear
- Leaves without making a joke, which is the final proof that something has changed
- Promises to return tomorrow even though nothing will happen

Kene is loyal, afraid, and present. He does not rescue the chapter — he witnesses it.

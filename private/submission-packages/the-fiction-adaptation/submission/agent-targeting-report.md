## STRATEGIC POSITIONING FOR AGENTS

### What Agents Will Understand

**Genre:** Literary Speculative Fiction (restrained, literary-first, not fantasy/horror)

**Market Positioning:** 
Marriage under metaphysical/psychological pressure; protagonist learns to accept what cannot be changed or understood; no resolution of the supernatural (intentional)

**Comparable Books:**
- *Dept. of Speculation* (Jenny Offill) — philosophical marriage narrative, restrained
- *Our Wives Under the Sea* (Sarah Read/Catherine Armfield) — domestic relationship under strange transformation

**Audience:**
Readers seeking literary fiction with emotional and psychological depth; character-driven narratives; restrained supernatural elements; marriage as subject

**Author Positioning:**
London-based writer with two linked projects—a memoir on fatherhood/faith/institutional pressure and this novel transforming these themes into fiction. This positioning signals literary authority and thematic coherence without claiming the novel is disguised memoir.

### What Agents Will NOT Expect

- ❌ Explanation or resolution of supernatural elements
- ❌ Plot driven by mystery or revelation
- ❌ Romance arc or relationship resolution
- ❌ Unnamed protagonist as a problem to solve
- ❌ Character transformation through action
- ❌ Gothic horror or commercial thriller tone

## RISK ASSESSMENT & CONTINGENCY

### Research Risks & Mitigation

**Risk:** Agents marked open to queries are actually closed  
**Mitigation:** Verify submission status on agency website within 2 weeks of sending query

**Risk:** Recent client information is outdated  
**Mitigation:** Use Publishers Marketplace and recent acknowledgements pages; verify dates

**Risk:** Agent interests have changed  
**Mitigation:** Look for recent interviews, MSWL posts, deal announcements (2024–2026)

**Risk:** Submission guidelines are outdated  
**Mitigation:** Verify on agency website immediately before sending each query

### Submission Risks & Mitigation

**Risk:** Manuscript is strong but agent fit is wrong  
**Mitigation:** Tier 1 agents are high-alignment; even if all pass, Tier 2/3 expand reach

**Risk:** Multiple requests at once creates management challenge  
**Mitigation:** Submission strategy staggers phases to avoid this; if it occurs, it's a good problem

**Risk:** No requests from any tier  
**Mitigation:** Gather feedback for potential query refinement; manuscript strength remains separate from agent fit

# Pitch Positioning
## The Fiction Adaptation

---

## Logline

A man learns that his courage endangers his wife. A woman teaches him that some doors survive only because they stay closed.

---

## Short Pitch (2–3 Sentences)

*The Fiction Adaptation* is a literary novel about a marriage under metaphysical pressure. When the protagonist moves into a house inhabited by a territorial force he does not understand, his wife's silence becomes a form of knowledge, and her refusal to open the forbidden door becomes an act of power. The novel explores what it means to love someone by learning not to save them.

---

## Long Pitch (1 Page)

An unnamed protagonist arrives in a coastal town carrying the weight of lateness—a sense that he has missed something essential, though he cannot name what. He rents a house and marries Obianuju, a woman who moves into the space with deliberate knowledge. The house is cold in ways that have nothing to do with weather. It is inhabited by something older than the tenancy, something territorial and claiming.

For two years, Obianuju maintains an equilibrium of silence and performance. She has chosen this house deliberately. She understands its pressure. She establishes boundaries: certain hours are dangerous. A door in the back wall must remain closed. The protagonist's fearlessness—his willingness to enter every room, to solve every problem—becomes the liability. His courage invites escalation.

When the house speaks—not in words, but in the presence of an intimidating sentence—it names the cost: his wife. The protagonist realizes that his lack of fear is not a gift. It is a danger. She is the one the territorial force has been waiting for. His willingness to protect her through action is precisely what makes her a target.

As a landlord's reasonable pressure mounts and a misdirected photograph buys temporary escape, the protagonist learns something harder than courage: restraint. He learns to sit still while his wife manages what he cannot control. He learns that holding a line for someone—asking nothing, understanding little, offering only presence—is more complete than any act of rescue could be.

The novel ends not with resolution but with acceptance. The door remains closed. The house remains cold. The protagonist sits in the front room with his hands open, and for the first time in his life—truly, cleanly, without audience—he wants nothing but for his wife to be safe in a room he is not allowed to enter.

*The Fiction Adaptation* is a meditation on marriage as a relationship of discovery, on privacy as power, and on the quiet courage required to live beside what cannot be changed.

## Themes

1. **Marriage as Discovery** — The novel explores what happens when two people with radically different knowledge structures choose to share a life
2. **Privacy as Power** — Obianuju's refusal to explain or open creates a boundary that even a reasonable landlord cannot cross
3. **Fearlessness as Liability** — The protagonist's lack of fear is not an asset; it is the thing that makes his wife a target
4. **Restraint Over Action** — The novel valorizes stillness, presence, and the refusal to solve
5. **Living Beside vs. Living Through** — Some things cannot be changed; the fidelity is in the acceptance
6. **Lateness as Truth** — The protagonist was late to something; this lateness is not a failure but a fact that shapes everything
7. **Silence as Strategy** — What Obianuju does not say is as powerful as what she does say
8. **Boundaries as Love** — Protecting someone sometimes means not entering the rooms they keep closed

## What This Book Is

- A novel about marriage as a primary relationship of discovery and power negotiation
- A meditation on what it means to love someone by learning not to save them
- A literary examination of metaphysical pressure without fantasy mechanics
- A study of female agency, knowledge-keeping, and the power of refusal
- An exploration of privacy, boundaries, and what it means to protect silence
- A novel that treats the supernatural as a form of literary pressure rather than plot
- A restrained, sentence-level narrative that achieves emotional power through control
- A book about the cost and benefit of arrival, lateness, and learning to stand still

---

## What This Book Is NOT

- **Not horror.** Supernatural elements function as literary pressure and metaphysical presence, not as threat or danger narratives
- **Not fantasy.** No magic system, no lore explanation, no cosmological architecture
- **Not a ghost story.** The cold is territorial and claiming, not vengeful or haunting
- **Not memoir disguised as fiction.** Protagonist is entirely fictional; no true-name reveal or biographical basis
- **Not a mystery to solve.** The no-colour door remains closed; the cold is never explained; the ending does not reveal secrets
- **Not domestic noir or thriller.** No betrayal, no violence, no whodunit structure
- **Not speculative genre-first.** Supernatural elements are subordinate to the literary and relational center

   - Above typical literary fiction target (~90k)
   - Manuscript is lean; no padding detected
   - Cadence passes I & II have thinned repeated patterns materially

2. **Supernatural Elements Require Literary Frame**
   - Not marketed as speculative genre
   - Never explained or resolved
   - Agents unfamiliar with literary-speculative blend may initially position incorrectly
   - Requires precise comp selection (not Stephen King, not Shirley Jackson horror)

3. **Protagonist's Unnamed Status**
   - Deliberate literary choice, not oversight
   - Frames protagonist as witness rather than central actor
   - Requires careful positioning: "narrative strategy" not "missing information"
   - Third-person limited POV maintains intimacy while preserving mystery of identity

4. **Obianuju Cannot Be Framed as "Mysterious Wife"**
   - She is the agentic force; protagonist learns from her
   - Query and positioning materials must make her agency clear
   - Some agents may initially misread her silence as passivity; requires correction
   - She is the one who knows; her refusal to explain is power

5. **Ending Does Not Resolve**
   - The cold remains
   - The door stays closed
   - No lore revelation
   - No supernatural explanation
   - This is intentional and must be defended as literary choice, not failure

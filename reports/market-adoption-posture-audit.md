## Market Pain Mapping

| Product | Market Pain | Trigger Event | Current Alternative | Product Resolution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| fast_diagnostic | Leaders make expensive decisions from incomplete evidence and need a fast signal before committing. | A decision is live, stakes are high, and internal confidence feels stronger than the evidence. | Ask a senior colleague, consultant, or generic AI prompt for a second opinion. | Capture the case, derive judgement by pattern, expose consequence, next move, falsification, and escalation trigger. |
| team_assessment | Teams hide disagreement behind consensus language until execution exposes the split. | A leadership team says it agrees but execution signals, priorities, or ownership are diverging. | Run a workshop, survey, or retrospective. | Surface alignment gaps, decision risk, accountability, and the next evidence or operating move. |
| enterprise_assessment | Enterprise decisions pass through committees without a clear evidence state or authority owner. | A strategic initiative is moving through governance with unresolved dependencies or unclear ownership. | Governance deck, steering committee pack, consultant assessment. | Bind decision risk to evidence, authority, limitations, and escalation conditions. |
| personal_decision_audit | A founder or leader needs a defensible decision record, not another reflective worksheet. | A personal or commercial decision has consequences but lacks a structured decision record. | Coaching call, template, generic AI analysis, private notes. | Use a decision instrument to convert the case into a governed judgement and action path. |
| decision_centre | Organisations repeat the same decision failures because cases, evidence, and lessons are not retained in one operating surface. | Multiple decisions are moving at once and leadership cannot see evidence quality or risk state. | Spreadsheets, PMO dashboards, Slack threads, board packs. | Create a live centre for decision cases, evidence state, authority state, and next action. |
| boardroom_mode | Boards approve strategy without seeing what would falsify the case or where authority is unsupported. | A board-level decision requires scrutiny before approval. | Board paper or consultant deck. | Present decision risk, evidence, falsification triggers, and unsupported claims in board-facing form. |
| global_market_reports | Leaders need market intelligence connected to decision consequences, not static commentary. | A macro or market shift changes strategic timing, exposure, or prioritisation. | Analyst report, newsletter, generic AI market summary. | Turn market signals into decision lessons, constraints, limitations, and reuse value. |
| control_room | Operators cannot prove which claims and products are externally supported versus internally asserted. | A product claim, launch, or sales page needs authority before going public. | Manual review, status spreadsheet, launch checklist. | Expose product authority, evidence ledger state, and blocked claims in one admin surface. |
| checkout_fulfilment | A buyer needs clear fulfilment after purchase and confidence that the delivered output matches the public promise. | A user is ready to buy or has paid for a decision product. | Consulting booking form or generic downloadable product. | Tie product claims, checkout, output route, and fulfilment contract together. |

## Implementation-Ready Recommendations

- Put the pain, the decision action, and the evidence state in the first viewport of every product entry route.
- Replace abstract authority language on customer surfaces with visible evidence state, limitation, next evidence action, and blocked-claim state.
- Wire the ProductAuthorityContract and evidence ledger into public product pages, result pages, checkout, and admin authority centre.
- Turn static market and boardroom surfaces into decision outputs with consequence, falsification trigger, and reuse value.
- Give every route a single next action that follows from the judgement state: test evidence, assign owner, escalate, reduce scope, or buy/submit.

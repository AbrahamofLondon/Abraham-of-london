# Voiceover Recording Script

Read slowly, clearly, with authority. Not excited. Not rushed. 
Tone: private executive briefing, not sales pitch.
Pace: ~130 words per minute (slower than normal speech).

---

## SCENE 1 — Opening (22 seconds)

[PAUSE 3 seconds — let the words appear]

Serious decisions rarely fail because leaders lack intelligence.
They fail because evidence is incomplete, authority is unclear, consequences are underestimated, and execution reality is treated as an afterthought.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 2 — The Category Problem (20 seconds)

Consulting gives recommendations.
Dashboards show data.
AI assistants generate suggestions.

[PAUSE 1 second]

But serious decisions need something different: a system that can test the decision itself.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 3 — Category Declaration (18 seconds)

Abraham of London is building Governed Decision Intelligence: a decision infrastructure system for operators, executives, and institutions facing decisions they cannot afford to get wrong.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## SCENE 4 — What the System Does (22 seconds)

The system does not begin by selling a service.
It begins by testing a decision condition.

[PAUSE 1 second]

If the evidence is weak, the system can restrict progression.
If the case is serious, it creates a governed path forward.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 5 — Fast Diagnostic (28 seconds)

The first entry point is a fast diagnostic.
A decision is submitted, challenged, classified, and converted into a governed record.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

This is not a personality test. It is a condition reading: what is unstable, what is missing, and what must be resolved before the decision moves forward.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 6 — Decision Instruments (25 seconds)

From there, the operator can use decision instruments.
Each instrument tests a specific dimension: exposure, escalation readiness, execution risk, mandate clarity, governance drift, priority conflict, or board readiness.

[PAUSE 1 second]

These are not static frameworks. Each one produces a scored, source-labelled output that can feed the wider corridor.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 7 — The Refusal Principle (22 seconds)

The category wedge is refusal.

[PAUSE 1 second]

Most products are designed to keep the user moving.
This system is designed to stop weak decision logic before it becomes expensive.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## SCENE 8 — Decision Centre (20 seconds)

Once a decision enters the system, it does not disappear after the session.
It becomes a living case: remembered, revisited, compared, and governed over time.

[PAUSE 1 second]

This is where the product moves from assessment into infrastructure.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 9 — Executive Reporting (22 seconds)

When the decision is serious enough, the corridor moves into Executive Reporting.
The report does not merely describe the issue. It tests consequence, contradiction, stakeholder pressure, intervention readiness, and board-level exposure.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## SCENE 10 — Strategy Room (18 seconds)

Strategy Room is where the decision moves from diagnosis to intervention.
The system carries evidence forward, tracks commitments, detects avoidance, and creates a checkpoint record.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## SCENE 11 — Boardroom and Oversight (22 seconds)

For institutional cases, the corridor extends into Boardroom and Oversight.
Boardroom produces the dossier.
Oversight preserves cadence, memory, suppression boundaries, outcome history, and continuity risk.

[PAUSE 1 second]

The more serious the decision estate becomes, the more valuable continuity becomes.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

---

## SCENE 12 — What Is Not Claimed (25 seconds)

The system is deliberately bounded.
It does not claim guaranteed outcomes.
It does not expose internal scoring mechanics.
It does not pretend thin evidence is strong evidence.

[PAUSE 1 second]

It uses automation where governance benefits from automation, and human review where judgement is required.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## SCENE 13 — Why This Matters (20 seconds)

The aim is simple: help serious operators make fewer weak decisions, preserve what the organisation learns, and create a governed path from signal to execution.

[PAUSE 4 seconds]

---

## SCENE 14 — Closing (22 seconds)

I am inviting a small number of serious operators to review the corridor before wider market push.

[PAUSE 1 second]

The question is not whether it is interesting.
The question is whether this is credible enough to become a new category of decision infrastructure.

[PAUSE 2 seconds]

If the category lands, the next step is a focused operator pilot.

[PAUSE 3 seconds]

---

## RECORDING NOTES

Total word count: ~520 words
Target duration: 4:30 – 5:30 at deliberate pace
Format: WAV or MP3, 44.1kHz or higher
Silence: leave 1 second of silence at start and end

After recording, save as:
  video/public/assets/voiceover-main.mp3

Then update video/src/Video.tsx to add:
  import { Audio } from "remotion";
  <Audio src="/assets/voiceover-main.mp3" />
